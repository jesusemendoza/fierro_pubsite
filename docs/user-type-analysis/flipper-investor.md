# Flipper / Real Estate Investor Perspective Analysis

> "I'm running 3-8 flips at a time. Margins are everything. I need to know my all-in cost per property and catch overruns before they eat my profit."

## Verdict: Interested but would NOT convert

## Trust Barriers

### No ROI Story
- Site never quantifies the value: no "save X%," no "reduce overruns by Y%"
- Flippers are ruthlessly ROI-driven — every tool needs to justify its cost in dollars saved
- "Stay profitable" is a wish, not a value proposition
- No case study showing a flip where Fierro caught an overrun

### No Proof It Works at Scale
- Zero testimonials from investors or flippers
- No portfolio-view screenshots showing multiple projects side by side
- Flippers need multi-project comparison — is my labor cost higher on this flip vs. the last three?

### Same Trust Gaps as All Users
- No team, no social proof, no screenshots, no demo video
- An investor managing $500K+ across active flips won't trust an unknown tool

## Messaging Problems

### Doesn't Speak the Flipper Language
- No mention of: ARV, rehab budget, holding costs, closing costs, profit margin
- "Construction cost control" is accurate but generic
- Should say: "Know your all-in cost per property in real time"
- Should say: "Compare rehab costs across your portfolio"

### No Portfolio-Level Pitch
- Site focuses on individual project tracking
- Flippers need portfolio analytics: average cost per sqft across flips, labor cost trends, best/worst performing contractors
- The "Analytics & Reporting" section hints at this but doesn't commit

## Pricing Concerns

### Plus Tier Is Limiting
- 5 projects at $49/mo — if running 6+ flips with overlap, already at the ceiling
- No option between Plus (5 projects) and Builder (unlimited, custom pricing)
- A $79/mo tier with 15 projects would capture this audience

### Builder Tier Is a Black Box
- "Contact Sales" with no price range is a red flag for solo investors
- Flippers assume "contact sales" = expensive enterprise pricing
- Many will bounce rather than start a sales conversation

### No Annual ROI Framing
- $49/mo = $588/year. On a $50K rehab, one caught overrun pays for 5 years of the tool
- This math is never presented on the site

## What Would Fix This

1. Add a "For Investors" or "For Flippers" landing page
2. Show a portfolio dashboard screenshot — multiple flips, their budgets, and margin health
3. Add a case study: "How [investor] tracked 6 flips and saved $12K in overruns"
4. Frame pricing in ROI terms: "One caught overrun pays for a year of Fierro"
5. Add a mid-tier plan (~$79/mo, 15 projects) for active investors
6. Show the Builder tier price range ($150-300/mo) to remove friction
7. List integrations (QuickBooks, Xero, bank feeds) or be honest about the roadmap
8. Add rehab budget templates as a feature
