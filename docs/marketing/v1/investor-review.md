# Fierro Website Review: Real Estate Investor Perspective

**Reviewer Profile:** Active real estate investor managing 8-15 properties at any time. Mix of BRRRR, flips, and light value-add. Currently using a combination of QuickBooks Online, Google Sheets, and CoConstruct (which I hate). My CPA sends me angry emails every Q4 because my expense data is a disaster. I have burned money on overruns I didn't catch until the HUD statement.

**Date:** March 2026
**Pages Reviewed:** Homepage, Pricing, Why Fierro

---

## The Honest Review

### First 10 Seconds

"Know exactly where your money goes."

That headline hit me in the chest. That is literally the sentence I mutter under my breath when I'm looking at a draw sheet at midnight wondering if my GC is padding the electrical line. Good start.

The subheadline broadens to "remodeling your home or flipping your next investment" which dilutes the punch a little. An investor reading this thinks: "OK, is this for me or for a homeowner redoing their kitchen?" But it doesn't lose me. The word "investment" is in the first paragraph, which is more than most construction tools bother with.

The hero screenshot appears to show a project dashboard. That's the right call -- show me the product, not stock photos of hard hats.

### Who Is This Actually For?

The site tries to serve three audiences:
1. Homeowners & DIYers
2. Flippers & Investors
3. Real Estate Teams

I appreciate the explicit callout to flippers and investors. Most tools in this space either target general contractors (Buildertrend, CoConstruct) or property managers (Buildium, AppFolio). The fact that Fierro even *has* "Flippers & Investors" as a segment on the homepage puts it ahead of 90% of tools I've looked at.

**But here's the problem:** The copy leans heavily homeowner. "Remodeling a kitchen? Building an addition? Finally finishing that basement?" -- that's nice, but it's not my world. My world is "I'm closing on a 3/2 ranch in 18 days, my GC needs a draw schedule, and I need to know if the $42K rehab budget is going to hold or if I need to renegotiate the purchase price."

The "Flippers & Investors" card says: "Know your all-in cost per property in real time. Compare rehab budgets across flips. Catch the overruns that kill your margins before closing day." NOW you're talking to me. That's perfect investor language. But it's buried in one card in an audience section. That should be above the fold for investor-targeted traffic.

### Does It Solve My #1 Problem?

My #1 problem is **knowing my true all-in cost across multiple active projects at any given moment.** I need to look at a screen and know: "123 Oak St is at $38K of a $45K rehab budget, 456 Elm is at $12K of a $30K budget, and 789 Pine is bleeding -- $28K spent against a $25K budget and we haven't even started flooring."

Based on what I see on the site: **yes, in theory.** The product claims real-time budget tracking, per-project views, budget vs. actual, and a portfolio view. The audience section specifically shows a screenshot labeled "Multi-property portfolio view with budget progress" for the flipper persona. That is exactly what I need.

The "Why Fierro" page drives this home with the pain point: "You didn't realize the flip was over budget until closing day." That's not theoretical for me. That happened on a deal in 2024. It cost me $11K in margin I thought I had.

**Verdict on core problem:** The product appears to be designed exactly for this. But I can't fully confirm without seeing the actual app. The website tells me the right story but doesn't go deep enough on the specifics. How granular can my budget categories get? Can I do sub-sub-categories (e.g., Kitchen > Cabinets > Hardware)? What happens when I have 12 active projects -- does the dashboard still make sense or does it become a wall of noise?

### Multi-Project Capability

The pricing page is where this gets interesting -- and where I start doing math.

- **Free:** 1 project. Useless for an investor. Fine for testing.
- **Home ($20/mo):** 2 projects. Still useless. I've never had only 2 active projects.
- **Plus ($49/mo):** 5 projects, 20 sub-projects each, 10 team members.
- **Builder (Custom):** Unlimited everything.

**Here's my math:** I typically have 8-12 active projects at once. Plus tier caps at 5. That means I'm immediately in "Contact Sales" territory for the Builder plan. I have no idea what that costs. Could be $99/mo (fine), could be $499/mo (less fine), could be $2,000/mo (not happening).

For a typical investor, 5 projects might actually be enough. Most flippers I know run 2-5 at a time. But anyone doing serious volume -- BRRRR investors, small development shops, investor groups -- will blow past the Plus cap instantly.

**The gap:** There needs to be something between $49/mo (5 projects) and "Contact Sales" (unlimited). A $99/mo tier with 15 projects would capture a huge slice of the serious-but-not-enterprise investor market. Right now you're pushing your best customers into a sales conversation they don't want to have.

### Financial Reporting & Export

The site mentions:
- "Export reports for your partner, lender, or accountant"
- "Generate a project cost summary in seconds"
- "Share a dashboard instead of scrambling to build a report"

This is encouraging but vague. What I actually need to know:

1. **Can I export a per-property P&L that my CPA can use for Schedule E?** The site doesn't say.
2. **Can I get a CSV/Excel export of all expenses with vendor, date, amount, category, and receipt links?** Not explicitly mentioned.
3. **Can I generate a draw request report for my hard money lender?** Many HML require a specific format. No mention of lender-specific reporting.
4. **Can I export data to QuickBooks or Xero?** The comparison table shows "API access" only on the Builder tier. So no, not unless I'm paying enterprise pricing.
5. **Is there a year-end summary across all projects for tax prep?** Not mentioned.

The "Instant Reports" AI feature says I can "generate a project cost summary in seconds" and share it with my "partner, lender, or accountant." That's great marketing, but I need to see what that report actually looks like. My CPA doesn't want a pretty dashboard -- she wants a spreadsheet with columns she can map to tax categories.

**This is where the site undersells itself badly.** If Fierro can actually produce clean exports, that alone is worth the subscription. My current process involves me spending two full weekends in January exporting from QuickBooks, cross-referencing against bank statements, and crying. If Fierro eliminates that, say so explicitly. "Give your CPA a clean export, not a shoebox of receipts" would be a killer line.

### Vendor Management

The site says:
- "Build a directory of everyone working on your project"
- "Link invoices to specific vendors and budget categories"
- "Compare costs across projects to spot who's overcharging"

That last bullet is pure gold for investors. If I can look across all my projects and see that Electrician A charges me an average of $4,200 per house while Electrician B charges $3,100 for similar scope -- that's actionable intelligence. That's money in my pocket on every future deal.

**But vendor management is only available on Plus ($49/mo) and above.** That's a reasonable upsell, honestly. The people who need cross-project vendor comparison are running multiple projects, and they should be on Plus anyway.

What I'd love to see on the site: a specific example. "Investors using Fierro discovered they were overpaying their plumber by 30% compared to similar-scope work on other properties." That kind of specificity converts.

### Budget vs. Actual Tracking

This appears to be the core of the product. Every page hammers on it: set a budget, track against it, get alerted before you go over.

For an investor, this is literally the lifeblood of deal analysis. Here's what I do today:

1. I underwrite a deal with an estimated rehab budget
2. I get a contractor bid
3. I start the rehab
4. Costs happen
5. I have no idea where I actually stand until I do manual reconciliation
6. Sometimes I find out I'm over budget after the work is done

If Fierro truly provides real-time budget vs. actual at the category level, with alerts at threshold triggers, that alone justifies the cost. Missing a $5K overrun on one flip costs me more than a year of Fierro's Plus plan.

### The AI / MCP Angle

The site mentions "Fully configurable AI/MCP" on Plus and Builder tiers. The "Why Fierro" page talks about "Smart Budget Insights" with trend alerts and instant reports.

I'll be honest: as an investor, I don't care about AI buzzwords. I care about results. "Fierro uses AI to surface what matters" -- fine, but what does that mean practically? The two examples given (trend alerts for over-budget categories, instant report generation) are genuinely useful. But calling it "AI" makes it sound like you're chasing a trend rather than solving a problem.

The "MCP" mention means nothing to most investors. If it means I can connect Fierro to my other tools programmatically, say that in plain language. "Connect Fierro to your existing tools" is better than "Fully configurable AI/MCP."

### Pricing Analysis for Investors

Let me do the real math:

**My current costs:**
- QuickBooks Online: $30/mo (Plus plan)
- CoConstruct: $99/mo (and I barely use it for cost tracking)
- My time reconciling spreadsheets: ~10 hrs/month at an opportunity cost of $100+/hr = $1,000/mo in lost time
- Average overrun I catch late: $3-5K per project, maybe 2-3x per year = $6-15K annually

**Fierro Plus at $49/mo ($470/yr annual):**
- If it replaces my CoConstruct cost tracking: saves $99/mo immediately
- If it cuts my reconciliation time by even 50%: saves $500/mo in opportunity cost
- If it catches one overrun early per year: saves $3-5K

The ROI math works overwhelmingly, even at the Plus tier. But I might need Builder, and I have no idea what that costs. That unknown is a friction point.

**Suggestion:** Put a starting price on Builder. "$149/mo starting" or whatever it is. "Custom" pricing with no anchor makes investors (who think in numbers) uncomfortable. We assume the worst.

### Competitive Position

Let me compare Fierro to what I've actually used:

**vs. QuickBooks Online:**
QuickBooks is accounting software pretending to be project management. I can track expenses, sure, but there's no budget-vs-actual dashboard per project without custom reports. No receipt capture that's worth using. No contractor collaboration. Fierro wins on UX and project-specific tracking. QuickBooks wins on accounting completeness, tax integration, and CPA familiarity.

**vs. CoConstruct:**
CoConstruct is built for custom home builders, not investors. It's overkill for flips. The budgeting is decent but the interface is from 2012. The learning curve is steep. At $99/mo for their basic plan, Fierro at $49/mo with better UX and investor-specific features is a no-brainer IF the product delivers what the site promises.

**vs. Buildium / AppFolio:**
These are property management platforms, not construction cost trackers. They handle rent collection, maintenance requests, and tenant screening. They're not trying to solve the rehab tracking problem. Fierro doesn't compete with these -- it complements them.

**vs. Excel / Google Sheets:**
The eternal competitor. Sheets are free, infinitely flexible, and I already know how to use them. The FAQ addresses this ("spreadsheets don't alert you when your electrical budget is 80% spent") which is a good response. The truth: Fierro needs to be significantly easier than maintaining a spreadsheet AND provide features a spreadsheet can't (alerts, receipt capture, contractor collaboration, real-time dashboards). The site makes a reasonable case for this.

**What's missing from the competitive positioning:** The site never directly names competitors. The "Can't I just use a spreadsheet?" FAQ is the closest it gets. For investors, a direct comparison page ("Fierro vs. QuickBooks for Real Estate Investors") would be powerful SEO and conversion content.

### Trust & Credibility

Here's where the site has a gap. I see:
- No customer testimonials
- No case studies
- No logos of companies using it
- No mention of how many users/projects are on the platform
- No named founders or team page
- No investor/backer information
- Security claims are generic ("encrypted in transit and at rest, Supabase infrastructure")

For a tool I'm going to trust with my financial data and business decisions, this matters. I need to know:
- Who built this and are they going to be around in 2 years?
- Are other investors actually using this successfully?
- What happens to my data if Fierro shuts down?
- Is there a real company behind this or is it a side project?

The lack of social proof is the single biggest weakness of the site right now. One testimonial from a real flipper saying "Fierro saved me $12K on my last three flips" would do more than any feature description.

### Design & UX of the Site Itself

The site is clean, fast, and professional. Dark theme with orange accents feels modern without being gimmicky. The copy is clear and jargon-free. Page structure is logical. Mobile screenshots show the product actually works on phones (important -- I'm often on job sites).

The "Why Fierro" page is the strongest page on the site. The pain-point-first approach (problem > cause > solution) is effective and feels like the writer actually understands renovation chaos. That page would convert me faster than the homepage.

One complaint: the homepage tries to do too much. It's a features page, an audience page, a how-it-works page, and a CTA all in one. For investors specifically, a dedicated landing page with investor-specific messaging would convert better than the catch-all homepage.

### What Would Make Me Sign Up Today

1. A **real demo or video walkthrough** showing multi-project portfolio management. Not a screenshot -- I want to see the flow.
2. **One real testimonial** from someone managing multiple flips.
3. **Explicit export details** -- show me a sample CSV or PDF report. Tell me the export formats.
4. A **clear Builder price** or at least a range.
5. **QuickBooks integration** or a clear path to getting data from Fierro into my accounting stack.

### What Would Make This Product Undeniable

The website is 80% there. The core value proposition is strong. The product appears to solve a real problem that real investors have. But it's trying to be everything for everyone, and in doing so, it's slightly underwhelming for everyone.

If Fierro leaned harder into the investor persona -- if the homepage for investor traffic said "Know your all-in rehab cost across every property, in real time, and stop leaving money on the table" -- I'd be entering my credit card already.

The product needs to answer one question better than anything else: **"What did this property actually cost me?"** If Fierro can answer that question faster and more accurately than anything else on the market, it wins. The site hints at this but doesn't own it.

## The Bottom Line

Fierro is solving the right problem for the right people. The product positioning is smart -- there's a genuine gap between "Google Sheets" and "CoConstruct/Buildertrend" for real estate investors who need project-level cost tracking without enterprise complexity.

The website does a good job of explaining what the product does and why it matters. The copy is clean, the pain points are real, and the pricing is reasonable.

**Three things that would move the needle from "interesting" to "shut up and take my money":**

1. **Social proof.** Even one investor testimonial, one case study, one concrete number ("investors using Fierro catch overruns 3 weeks earlier on average") would transform credibility overnight.

2. **Export and integration details.** Tell me exactly what I can export, in what formats, and how it connects to QuickBooks/Xero. My CPA is the gatekeeper to every financial tool I adopt. If she says "I can work with this data," I'm in.

3. **A real investor tier.** 5 projects isn't enough for serious investors, and "Contact Sales" isn't a price. Give me a $99/mo tier with 15 projects and basic API access, and you've captured the entire serious-flipper market without forcing them into an enterprise conversation.

Fierro has the bones of something great. Right now it's a promising tool with a good website. With social proof, better export storytelling, and smarter investor-tier pricing, it becomes the obvious choice for every investor who's sick of spreadsheets and frustrated with tools built for GCs.

The question isn't whether investors need this. We do. The question is whether Fierro can prove it delivers before we hand over our financial data and workflows.
