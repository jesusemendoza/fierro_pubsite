## The 5 Things Killing Conversions (All Three Reviewers Agree)

### 1. ZERO SOCIAL PROOF — The #1 Problem
Every single reviewer flagged this as the biggest blocker. No testimonials, no case studies, no customer count, no founder story, no logos, no "trusted by X users." For a product that handles financial data, this is a dealbreaker. **All three scored Trust at 3/10.**

**Fix:** Get 3 real users (one from each segment), record 2-sentence testimonials, and plaster them on the homepage. Even "47 projects tracked" or a founder story would help.

### 2. Receipt Capture Paywalled on Free Tier
The entire pitch is "stop losing receipts." Then the free tier doesn't let you capture them. This kills the try-before-you-buy hook. Every reviewer noted this contradiction.

**Fix:** Make receipt capture free on all tiers. Gate the AI categorization, analytics, and exports instead.

### 3. Messaging Is Split — Nobody Feels Like the Primary Audience
- Homeowners see "For solo contractors" on the free tier and think "not for me"
- Investors see homeowner-first messaging and feel like an afterthought
- Flippers see generic language and wonder if this understands their workflow

**Fix:** Either create dedicated landing pages (`/for-homeowners`, `/for-investors`, `/for-flippers`) or restructure the homepage to lead with the universal pain point and branch into persona-specific sections with equal weight.

### 4. Pricing Cliff Between Plus ($49/mo, 5 projects) and Builder (Contact Sales)
Serious investors and flippers run 8-15 projects. They don't need "enterprise" features but the 5-project cap doesn't work. There's a missing tier.

**Fix:** Add a $99/mo tier with ~15 projects, or raise the Plus cap to 10-15 projects.

### 5. "AI/MCP" Jargon on a Consumer Product
Technical language like "Fully configurable AI/MCP" means nothing to homeowners, investors, or flippers. It actively hurts credibility with non-technical users.

**Fix:** Replace with plain language: "connects to your existing tools" or "AI that auto-categorizes your expenses."

## What's Actually Working (Don't Touch These)

- **"Know exactly where your money goes"** — All three reviewers said this headline lands immediately
- **The Why Fierro page** — Pain-point-first structure is genuinely effective, especially the "closing day surprises" paragraph
- **Copy quality** — Refreshingly free of SaaS buzzwords, sounds like a human wrote it
- **The core product idea** — Every reviewer said Fierro fills a real gap between spreadsheets and enterprise GC software
- **$20/mo Home tier** — Fair price point for homeowners doing a single renovation

---

## Persona-Specific Gaps

### Homeowner-Specific
- No mention of change orders (the #1 way homeowners get burned)
- No demo video despite "See How It Works" CTA
- No mobile app details despite mobile being central to the pitch
- Why Fierro page skews too heavily toward flippers

### Investor-Specific
- No export format details — "CPA-ready" data is the gatekeeper and it's never explained
- No QuickBooks/Xero integration mention
- No portfolio-level rollup views shown
- Needs cost basis / tax implications language

### Flipper-Specific
- No deal-level P&L (purchase + rehab + holding costs vs. ARV)
- Speed of expense entry never communicated — flippers need sub-30-second logging
- No before/after case study showing how Fierro saved a flip's margin
- 5-project limit is a non-starter for active flippers

## Priority Action Items (Ordered by Impact)

1. **Add social proof** — testimonials, user count, case study (BLOCKS ALL CONVERSIONS)
2. **Ungating receipt capture** on free tier (KILLS THE HOOK)
3. **Fix free tier label** — "For solo contractors" → "For your first project"
4. **Add a mid-tier** — $99/mo, 10-15 projects (CAPTURES SERIOUS USERS)
5. **Replace AI/MCP jargon** with plain language
6. **Create persona landing pages** or rebalance homepage messaging
7. **Add a 60-second demo video** showing the mobile receipt capture flow
8. **Show export samples** — what the CPA actually gets
9. **Add deal economics for flippers** — purchase + rehab + holding vs. ARV
10. **Competitor comparison section** — "vs. spreadsheets / vs. doing nothing / vs. enterprise tools"

---

## The Verdict

Fierro has a genuinely good product idea that fills a real market gap. The copy is strong and the pain points are real. But the site is asking people to trust an unknown product with their financial data and giving them zero reasons to trust it. The product is currently a **6/10 across all personas** — good enough to try, not good enough to recommend.

To get to "why the fuck am I NOT using this?" territory, the priorities are:
1. Social proof (trust)
2. Free the receipt hook (conversion)
3. Speak to each persona directly (relevance)
4. Fix the pricing gap (retention)

The bones are strong. The meat needs work.
