# Fierro Pubsite Review: The Homeowner Perspective

**Reviewer profile:** Homeowner who has managed 2 kitchen remodels, a bathroom gut-reno, and a basement finish over 8 years. Currently tracking everything in a Google Sheet that is held together with duct tape and prayer. Has a shoebox of receipts from the last project that will never be reconciled. Has been burned by a contractor going 40% over budget with zero warning.

**Date reviewed:** March 2026
**Pages reviewed:** Homepage, Pricing, Why Fierro

---

## First Impression (Landing on the Homepage)

The headline "Know exactly where your money goes" lands immediately. That is literally the sentence I mutter to myself at 11pm when I am staring at a contractor invoice that does not match anything in my spreadsheet. Good start.

The subheadline mentioning "remodeling your home or flipping your next investment" tells me right away this is not some enterprise construction management tool that wants me to create Gantt charts. It knows who I am. That is rare.

The hero screenshot showing a kitchen remodel budget dashboard is the right call. I can see myself in that. It is not showing some 200-unit apartment complex. It is showing a kitchen. My kitchen.

**Verdict:** The first 5 seconds are strong. I know what this is, I know it is for me, and I know the price is free to start. That is better than 90% of SaaS landing pages.

---

## Does It Speak to My Pain Points?

### What the homepage gets right

The four value props hit real problems I have actually experienced:

1. **"See Every Dollar in Real Time"** -- Yes. The single worst part of renovation is having no idea how much you have actually spent until you sit down and add everything up, which you never do until the project is over.

2. **"Catch Overruns Before They Hurt"** -- This is the killer feature pitch. My contractor told me electrical was "running a little over" and it turned out to be $8,000 over. An alert at 80% would have changed that entire conversation.

3. **"Track From Anywhere"** -- I buy things at Home Depot at 7am on a Saturday. If I cannot snap the receipt right there, it goes into my pocket and dies. Mobile receipt capture is not a nice-to-have. It is the entire point.

4. **"Built for Real Projects, Not Accountants"** -- This one line does a lot of heavy lifting. It tells me I am not going to open this thing and see a chart of accounts. Good.

### What the Why Fierro page gets right

The Why Fierro page is actually the strongest page on the site. The problem/solution pairs are devastatingly accurate:

- "Your contractor sent you a bill and you have no idea if it's right" -- I have literally paid invoices I suspected were wrong because I had no organized way to verify them. This section describes my life.

- "You set a budget but you have no idea if you're actually on track" -- The $35K kitchen example is perfectly calibrated. Not too small (a paint job), not too big (a full addition). It is the exact size of project where you think you can track it yourself but you absolutely cannot.

- "Your partner asked for a project update and you spent an hour pulling numbers" -- My wife asks me "how much have we spent so far?" and I visibly flinch. Every time. A shared dashboard would save my marriage.

### What is missing

The copy barely mentions the emotional reality of renovation cost chaos. There is no moment where it says something like "that pit in your stomach when you realize you are $15,000 over budget and the drywall is not even up yet." The pain points are described clinically. They could hit harder.

There is also zero mention of change orders. Change orders are where homeowners get destroyed. A contractor says "we found something behind the wall" and suddenly you owe another $4,000. I want to know how Fierro handles tracking change orders against the original budget. This is a massive gap in the messaging.

No mention of tracking payments vs. invoices vs. quotes. As a homeowner, I get a quote for $12,000, the invoice comes in at $13,500, and then I pay $6,000 up front and $7,500 on completion. That lifecycle is my entire financial life during a renovation. Does Fierro handle this? The site does not say.

## Pricing Analysis

### The Good

The free tier at 1 project with 6 sub-projects (budget categories) is genuinely useful. A simple bathroom remodel has maybe 5-6 categories: demo, plumbing, tile, fixtures, labor, misc. This is not a crippled free tier. You could actually use it for a real project. Smart move -- get me hooked, then I will pay when the kitchen reno starts.

The Home tier at $20/month is priced right. When I am spending $35,000 on a kitchen, $20/month for 4 months ($80 total) to not lose track of thousands of dollars is a no-brainer. That is less than one trip to Home Depot for stuff I do not need.

Annual pricing at $16/month ($192/year) makes sense if you are mid-project and know it will take 6+ months, which most renovations do.

### The Bad

**The Free tier description says "For solo contractors getting started."** This is a problem. I am not a solo contractor. I am a homeowner. The first pricing tier actively tells me this product is not for me. The Home tier says "For homeowners managing a renovation or remodel" which is perfect, but I have to pay $20/month before I see language that acknowledges my existence. The Free tier should say something like "For your first project" or "For a single renovation" -- something that does not make homeowners feel like second-class citizens on the free plan.

**6 sub-projects on Free vs. 15 on Home is a big jump.** A kitchen remodel can easily have 10+ categories: demo, framing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, drywall, paint, cabinets, countertops, flooring, appliances, fixtures, permits. That is 13. The free tier caps me at 6, which means I am forced to lump things together in ways that defeat the purpose. This feels like an artificial squeeze to push me to Home. I do not love it.

**Receipt photo capture is not on the Free tier (based on the comparison table).** This is the single most important feature for a homeowner. If I cannot snap receipts on the free tier, I will never experience the core value of the product. I will just keep using my spreadsheet. This is a conversion-killing decision.

### The Ugly

**"Fully configurable AI/MCP" on Plus and Builder means nothing to me.** I do not know what MCP is. I do not care. This is developer jargon on a page that is supposed to sell to homeowners and small-time flippers. Replace it with something human like "Smart budget insights" or "AI that catches problems before you do."

**"Limited AI access" on Free is equally meaningless.** Limited how? What does AI do in this context? The Why Fierro page mentions AI for trend alerts and instant reports, but the pricing page does not connect these dots. Tell me what the AI actually does at each tier.

## Would I Switch From My Spreadsheet?

This is the real question. My spreadsheet is free. It is ugly but it works (sort of). It does not alert me to anything. It does not let my contractor log expenses. It does not have receipt photos. It requires me to manually enter everything, which means I fall behind by week 2 of every project.

Fierro's FAQ has a good answer to the spreadsheet question, but it buries the best argument. The killer line is: "spreadsheets don't alert you when your electrical budget is 80% spent with half the work remaining." That single sentence should be on the homepage in 48px font. That is the moment a homeowner goes from "my spreadsheet is fine" to "oh, I need this."

**My honest answer:** If the free tier included receipt photo capture, I would sign up today and try it on my next project. Without receipt capture on free, I would probably not bother, because entering expenses manually is exactly what I already do in my spreadsheet. The receipts are the hook. Gate the analytics, gate the AI, gate the team members -- but do not gate the receipts.

## Copy Quality

### What works

The copy is refreshingly free of SaaS buzzwords. There is no "leverage synergies" or "unlock insights" or "empower your workflow." It sounds like a person wrote it, not a committee. Specific phrases that land:

- "No more wondering where you stand"
- "Before your contractor delivers the bad news"
- "No accounting degree required"
- "Stop wondering where your money went" (strong CTA line, used effectively as the closer)

The three-step getting started section ("Set Your Budget," "Track Everything," "Stay on Budget") is clean and makes it feel easy. The "Takes about 5 minutes" detail is smart -- it preempts the "I don't have time to learn a new tool" objection.

### What does not work

- **"For solo contractors getting started"** on the Free tier is tone-deaf for homeowners. Fix it.
- **"Fully configurable AI/MCP"** is jargon that should never appear on a consumer-facing pricing page.
- The audience section has three segments (Homeowners, Flippers, Real Estate Teams) but the copy for Real Estate Teams feels like it belongs on a different product. "One source of truth across your entire portfolio" is enterprise language that makes homeowners feel like they wandered into the wrong store.
- The Why Fierro page uses "flippers" language heavily. Two of the four pain point sections are about flipping. A homeowner doing one renovation might feel like this product is really for investors and they are just an afterthought.

### What is missing from the copy entirely

- **Social proof.** Zero testimonials. Zero case studies. Zero "saved $X on their renovation." Zero "Y homeowners trust Fierro." Nothing. I have no evidence that any real human has used this product and found it valuable. This is the single biggest credibility gap on the entire site.
- **A demo or walkthrough video.** The "See How It Works" button in the hero -- where does it go? If it just scrolls down, that is a missed opportunity. A 60-second video of someone snapping a receipt, it appearing in their budget, and an alert firing would sell this product better than all three pages combined.
- **Comparison to alternatives.** How is this different from Quickbooks? From a shared Google Sheet? From just texting my contractor? The FAQ touches on spreadsheets but never mentions any actual competitor.

## Trust and Credibility

This is where things get thin.

- No company information beyond "Fierro." Who makes this? A company? One person? A VC-backed startup? I have no idea.
- No physical address, no team page, no "about us."
- The security FAQ says "we use Supabase for infrastructure" which means nothing to most homeowners but actually signals "small team / indie project" to anyone technical.
- No SOC 2 mention, no specific security certifications.
- No customer logos, no testimonial quotes, no usage numbers.
- The copyright says 2026 which tells me this is very new.
- Terms and Privacy pages exist (good) but that is the bare minimum.

**The trust problem is severe for a financial product.** You are asking me to enter every expense from my $50,000 renovation into your system. I need to believe you will be around next month, that my data is safe, and that you are not going to jack up the price once I am locked in. Right now, I have zero basis for any of that trust.

Minimum fixes: Add a founder story ("I built this because I got burned on my own renovation"), add 3-5 testimonials (even beta user quotes), add a data export guarantee, and add a clear statement about what happens to my data if Fierro shuts down.

---

## Mobile Experience

The copy mentions mobile receipt capture multiple times, which signals they know this matters. But I cannot evaluate the actual mobile experience from the marketing site alone. Key questions:

- Is there a native app or is it a mobile web app? The site never says. This matters enormously for receipt capture quality.
- Can I snap a receipt and have it auto-categorize? Or do I have to manually fill in fields?
- Does it work offline? I am sometimes in a basement with no signal.
- How fast is the receipt capture flow? If it takes more than 10 seconds I will not use it.

The site needs to show the mobile experience. A screenshot or video of the receipt capture flow on a phone would be extremely compelling. Right now I am just taking their word for it.

---

## Competitive Landscape

As a homeowner shopping for this, I would compare Fierro to:

1. **Google Sheets / Excel** -- Free, flexible, but no alerts, no receipt capture, no collaboration with contractors. Fierro wins on functionality but needs to prove it is worth the switch cost.

2. **Buildertrend / CoConstruct / Houzz Pro** -- These are the big construction management platforms. They cost $100-500/month and are designed for professional contractors, not homeowners. Fierro's positioning as the simpler, cheaper alternative is smart but the site never explicitly makes this comparison. It should.

3. **Mint / YNAB / personal finance apps** -- These track spending but not against a project budget with categories. They are not built for renovation. But homeowners might try them first because they already have accounts.

4. **The literal shoebox of receipts** -- This is the real competitor. Most homeowners do not track renovation expenses at all. They just pay bills and hope for the best. Fierro needs to sell against inertia, not against other software.

The site does not acknowledge any of these alternatives. It should. A "Fierro vs. Spreadsheets" or "Fierro vs. Contractor Software" section would help homeowners understand where this fits.

## The Brutal Summary

Fierro has a genuinely good product idea aimed at a real problem. The copy is better than most SaaS sites -- it is specific, clear, and avoids buzzword hell. The pricing is reasonable. The feature set covers the basics.

But the site has three critical problems that would prevent me from signing up today:

### 1. Zero Social Proof (Trust Killer)
You are asking people to trust you with their financial data and you have not provided a single reason to do so. No testimonials, no customer count, no founder story, no case study. Even "47 homeowners tracked $2.3M in renovations last month" would be something. Right now there is nothing. This is the #1 thing to fix.

### 2. Receipt Capture Gated Behind Paywall (Conversion Killer)
The entire pitch is "stop losing receipts, track everything from your phone." Then the free tier does not include receipt photo capture. This is like a gym saying "get in shape!" and then locking the weights behind the premium membership. Let people snap receipts for free. Gate the analytics, gate the AI, gate the team seats. But the receipts are how you hook people.

### 3. Free Tier Messaging Says "For Solo Contractors" (Identity Mismatch)
The homepage says "homeowners and investors." The pricing page's first tier says "for solo contractors." These are different people. A homeowner who reads "for solo contractors" thinks "this is not for me" and leaves. The Free tier description should say something like "For a single project" or "Perfect for your first renovation."

---

## What Would Make Me Say "Shut Up and Take My Money"

1. **One real testimonial** from a homeowner who caught an overrun using Fierro. Just one. With a name and a dollar amount saved.
2. **Receipt capture on the free tier.** Let me try the core experience for free. I will upgrade when I need more categories or a second project.
3. **A 60-second demo video** showing the receipt-to-budget-alert flow. Show me snapping a receipt at Home Depot and it appearing in my budget 5 seconds later.
4. **Change order tracking** as a feature. Show me you understand that the budget I set on day 1 is not the budget I will have on day 30.
5. **A founder story.** "I built this because I lost $12,000 on a bathroom renovation and I never wanted that to happen again." That is all it would take.
6. **Fix the Free tier description** to say literally anything other than "for solo contractors."

Do those six things and this goes from "interesting, I will think about it" to "signing up right now." The product idea is right. The execution on the marketing site is 70% of the way there. The last 30% is trust, emotional resonance, and removing friction from the free tier.
