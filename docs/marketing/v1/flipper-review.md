# Fierro Website Review: House Flipper Perspective

**Reviewer Profile:** Full-time house flipper, 3-5 active flips at any time, managing multiple crews, buying materials personally, obsessed with knowing my all-in cost vs. ARV at all times. I live on my phone at job sites. Every dollar of overrun comes directly out of my profit.

**Date:** March 2026

**Pages Reviewed:**
- https://getfierro.com/ (homepage)
- https://getfierro.com/pricing (pricing)
- https://getfierro.com/why-fierro (why page)

---

## First Impression (The 5-Second Test)

The headline is "Know exactly where your money goes." That hits. As a flipper, I have felt the pain of NOT knowing exactly where my money went — usually when I'm sitting at closing and my profit is $15K less than I projected. The subheading mentions flipping specifically, which tells me someone here has at least thought about my use case.

The hero screenshot shows a budget dashboard. Good. I don't need to see a stock photo of a smiling couple in a kitchen. Show me the product.

But here's what I noticed immediately: the site leads with homeowners. "Whether you're remodeling your home or flipping your next investment." Homeowners first, flippers second. The audience section goes Homeowners & DIYers, then Flippers & Investors, then Real Estate Teams. I get it — homeowners are probably a bigger market — but it makes me wonder if this was actually built for me or if I'm a secondary audience getting a few features tacked on.

## What the Site Gets Right

### 1. It names the actual problem
The Why Fierro page has a pain point that says: "You didn't realize the flip was over budget until closing day." That is disturbingly accurate. The explanation — "Flippers juggle multiple properties, multiple contractors, and hundreds of line items. When costs live in different spreadsheets and bank statements, overruns hide until the final accounting" — reads like someone has either flipped houses or talked to someone who has. This is the single most compelling paragraph on the entire website for a flipper.

### 2. Multi-project visibility
The Flippers & Investors card says "Know your all-in cost per property in real time. Compare rehab budgets across flips." That is exactly what I need. If my plumber is charging me $8K on one flip and $12K on a comparable flip, I need to see that immediately. The fact that they show a "multi-property portfolio view" screenshot tells me they've at least thought about the multi-flip workflow.

### 3. Contractor can log expenses
"Your contractor logs expenses, you approve them." This is huge if it actually works. Right now, I'm chasing contractors for receipts via text. If my GC could just log what he spent and I could approve it from my phone, that eliminates a massive pain point. The approval workflow is critical — I'm not giving anyone a blank check.

### 4. Receipt capture on-site
"Snap a receipt on your phone at the hardware store." Good. This is table stakes for me. I'm at Home Depot three times a week. If I can't photograph a receipt in 10 seconds and have it auto-categorized, the whole system falls apart.

### 5. Budget alerts
"Get alerts when a category is trending over budget — before your contractor delivers the bad news." This is where the real money is. If Fierro had told me my electrical budget was 80% spent when only half the work was done on my last flip, I would have caught a $4,000 overrun two weeks earlier. That alert alone would pay for years of subscription.

## What the Site Gets Wrong (or Misses Entirely)

### 1. No mention of ARV, purchase price, or deal analysis
This is the biggest miss for flippers. My workflow is: Buy at $X, budget $Y for rehab, sell at ARV of $Z, profit = Z - X - Y - holding costs - selling costs. Fierro only seems to address the "Y" part — the rehab budget. But I need to see my TOTAL all-in cost, including purchase price, closing costs, holding costs (loan payments, insurance, utilities), and projected selling costs. Without that, I still need a separate spreadsheet to know if I'm making money on the deal. The site never mentions ARV, purchase price, holding costs, or projected profit. For a flipper, the rehab budget is only one piece of the puzzle.

### 2. No mention of speed or how fast I can log expenses
The site says "create a budget in minutes" and "takes about 5 minutes" for setup. Great. But it never addresses how fast I can log a single expense. Can I do it in 30 seconds? 15 seconds? Is there a quick-add button? Can it read the receipt and auto-fill the amount and vendor? For a flipper doing 5-10 expense entries per day across multiple projects, the per-entry speed is everything. If it takes me 2 minutes per expense, I won't use it. If it takes 15 seconds, I'll use it religiously. The site never makes this case.

### 3. The "How It Works" is too generic
The three steps are: (1) Set your budget, (2) Track everything, (3) Stay on budget. This could be describing literally any budgeting app. Show me the FLIPPER workflow: Create a new flip project, import your rehab budget from your deal analysis, assign categories by trade (demo, framing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing, flooring, paint, landscaping), start tracking on day one, see your profit margin update in real time as costs come in. That's the story that would make me pull out my credit card.

### 4. No mention of draws, lender reporting, or hard money
Most flippers use hard money or private lenders. Those lenders require draw schedules — proof that work has been completed and money has been spent before they release the next chunk of funding. If Fierro could generate draw reports automatically, that would be a game-changing feature. The site mentions "export reports for your partner, lender, or accountant" exactly once, buried in the Analytics section. This should be front and center for the flipper audience.

### 5. "Sub-projects" is confusing terminology
The pricing page limits "sub-projects per project." For a flipper, what is a "sub-project"? Is that a budget category? A trade? A room? The site never explains this. If "sub-projects" means budget categories (like "kitchen," "bathroom," "electrical"), then 6 on the free tier is barely enough for one flip, and 20 on Plus is tight for a complex rehab. This needs to be clear because it directly affects whether the pricing works for me.

### 6. No social proof from flippers
Zero testimonials. Zero case studies. Zero "I saved $X on my last flip." I get that this might be a newer product, but even one story from one flipper who caught an overrun would be more persuasive than all the feature descriptions combined. Flippers trust other flippers. We share tools in our networks constantly. Give me one name, one story, one number.

### 7. 5-project limit on Plus ($49/mo) is too low
If I'm running 3-5 active flips and also have 2-3 in the pipeline or recently sold, I could easily need 6-8 projects. The jump from Plus (5 projects, $49/mo) to Builder (unlimited, custom/enterprise pricing) is a cliff. There needs to be something in between. A flipper doing 8-10 flips per year doesn't need "enterprise" features or a "dedicated account manager." They need 10-15 project slots and a vendor comparison tool for maybe $79-99/mo.

### 8. Free tier doesn't include receipt capture
This is the one feature that would hook a flipper during a trial. If I can't snap receipts on the free tier, I'm just entering numbers into a slightly fancier spreadsheet. The aha moment — photographing a receipt at Home Depot and seeing it auto-populate in my budget — should be available on day one.

### 9. Vendor management locked to Plus
Vendor tracking and cost comparison across projects is one of the most valuable features for a flipper. Knowing which plumber is consistently cheaper, which electrician always runs over — that intelligence compounds over time. Locking it behind the $49/mo tier means I can't experience this value until I'm already paying. At minimum, basic vendor tagging should be available on Home.

### 10. The site doesn't explain what "AI" does specifically
"Smart Budget Insights" and "Fully configurable AI/MCP" — what does this actually mean for me? Can AI read my receipts and auto-categorize them? Can it predict that I'm going to blow my plumbing budget based on spending velocity? Can it flag duplicate charges? "AI" is meaningless without specific use cases. Show me what the AI does in the context of a flip and I'll pay for it. Tell me it's "smart" and I'll assume it's marketing fluff.

## The Flipper Verdict: Would I Pay For This?

**Honestly? I'd try it.** The free tier is enough to test it on one flip and see if the workflow actually speeds things up. The core promise — real-time budget tracking with receipt capture and contractor collaboration — directly addresses my top three pain points.

But here's the thing: I'd be using it as a SUPPLEMENT to my existing spreadsheet, not a replacement. Because Fierro (as presented on this site) doesn't capture my full deal economics. I'd still need a spreadsheet for purchase price, holding costs, selling costs, and ARV tracking. That means Fierro becomes "one more thing" rather than "the one thing."

If Fierro added a deal-level P&L view — purchase price + rehab costs + holding costs vs. ARV — I would pay $49/mo without blinking. If it could generate draw reports for my lender, I'd pay $99/mo. If it had a vendor performance dashboard that showed me cost trends across all my flips, I'd tell every flipper I know.

Right now, the product seems genuinely useful but the website undersells it for flippers. The messaging tries to serve homeowners and flippers simultaneously, and the flipper message gets diluted. A dedicated /for-flippers landing page with flipper-specific language, flipper-specific screenshots, and flipper-specific pricing would convert significantly better.

## Top 5 Recommendations to Win the Flipper Market

1. **Build a /for-flippers landing page** with flipper-specific copy, screenshots showing multi-property dashboards, and a deal-level P&L view. Drop the homeowner messaging entirely on that page. Speak directly to the pain of "I lost $12K on my last flip because I didn't catch the overrun in time."

2. **Add deal economics to the product and the messaging.** Purchase price, rehab budget, holding costs, selling costs, ARV, projected profit. Show the margin updating in real time as rehab costs come in. THAT is the screenshot that sells to flippers.

3. **Make receipt capture free on all tiers.** It's the hook. It's the aha moment. It's what makes Fierro different from a spreadsheet. Paywalling it on Free means your trial users never experience the magic.

4. **Add a "Flipper" tier between Plus and Builder.** 10-15 projects, vendor analytics, draw report exports. Price it at $79-99/mo. This is the tier serious flippers would land on. The current jump from $49 to "Contact Sales" loses this entire segment.

5. **Get one flipper testimonial on the site.** One real person. One real project. One real number. "I caught a $6,000 plumbing overrun on my Elm Street flip three weeks before I would have discovered it at closing. Fierro paid for itself 10x over." That one sentence would do more than everything else on the Why Fierro page combined.

---

## The Bottom Line

Fierro is solving a real problem that costs flippers real money. The core product concept — real-time budget tracking with receipt capture and contractor collaboration — is exactly what the market needs. But the website is trying to be everything to everyone and ends up being mildly interesting to everyone instead of absolutely essential to someone. The flipper who is losing $5K-20K per flip to budget overruns needs to land on this site and feel like it was built specifically for them. Right now, they land on it and feel like they're the second audience listed in a product built for homeowners doing kitchen remodels.

Fix the positioning, add deal-level economics, and get one testimonial from a real flipper — and you've got a product that flippers will sell to each other.
