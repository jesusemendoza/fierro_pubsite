# Fierro Website Review: House Flipper Persona

**Reviewer profile:** Full-time house flipper. 3-5 active flips at any time. Crews of subs, materials bought personally at Home Depot / Lowe's / specialty suppliers. Current system: Google Sheets for budgets, Notes app for quick logs, shoebox of receipts, memory for the rest. Lost money on at least two flips last year because I didn't catch overruns until closing.

**Pages reviewed:** Homepage, Pricing, Why Fierro
**Date:** 2026-03-10

---

## The Honest Take

I came into this skeptical. I've tried QuickBooks (overkill, slow, built for accountants not flippers), I've tried Notion templates (cute but no budget enforcement), and I've tried doing nothing (expensive). Here's what I actually think about Fierro after reading every word on the site.

---

## Scoring

### 1. First Impression / Hook: 7/10

**The headline:** "Know exactly where your money goes."

This is good. It's not trying to be clever. It speaks directly to the anxiety I feel on every flip — the nagging feeling that money is leaking and I won't know until I do the final accounting. The subline — "track every dollar, catch every overrun, and stay on budget" — hits the right notes.

**What works:** It doesn't waste my time. I land on the page and within 5 seconds I know: this tracks renovation/flip costs and warns me before I blow my budget. That's exactly what I need.

**What doesn't work:** The page talks to homeowners AND flippers AND real estate teams. I get that you need to address multiple audiences, but when I see "Remodeling a kitchen? Building an addition? Finally finishing that basement?" I almost bounced. That's a homeowner remodel vibe. I'm not "finishing my basement" — I'm trying to figure out if the $180K all-in on 742 Elm Street leaves me any margin against a $235K ARV. The flipper section exists, but it's competing for attention with DIYers.

**Fix:** Lead with the highest-stakes user. A homeowner who goes $3K over on a kitchen remodel is annoyed. A flipper who goes $15K over on a rehab might not make payroll. The urgency is different. Consider a landing page variant for flippers that leads with "Know your all-in cost per property in real time" instead of the generic line. Or at minimum, put the flipper/investor section FIRST in the audience blocks — we're the ones who will pay $49/month without blinking if the product delivers.

---

### 2. Value Proposition Clarity: 8/10

**What's clear:**
- Real-time budget tracking (not end-of-month reconciliation)
- Alerts before overruns (not after)
- Receipt capture on mobile (not desk-only)
- Multi-project support (critical for flippers)
- Contractor can log expenses too (this is huge — more on this below)

**What's missing:**
The site never explicitly says "See your all-in cost vs. ARV." It talks about "budget health" and "how much is spent vs. left," but for a flipper, the magic number is: **What's my projected profit right now?** I need: Purchase price + Rehab spent + Holding costs + Projected remaining = All-in. Then: ARV - All-in = My profit. If Fierro does this, SAY IT. If it doesn't, BUILD IT.

The Why Fierro page has the best copy on the site: "You didn't realize the flip was over budget until closing day." That one line made me lean forward. It's specific, it's painful, and it's true. More of this everywhere, please.

**Fix:** Add a flipper-specific value statement on the homepage: "See your projected profit per flip — updated every time a cost hits." That's the money shot. Everything else is a feature. Projected profit is the outcome.

---

### 3. Speed & Ease of Use (perceived from site): 7/10

The site claims I can "set up a budget in 5 minutes" and "log expenses as they happen." The three-step process (Set Budget -> Track Everything -> Stay on Budget) is clean and simple.

**What gives me confidence:**
- "Snap receipts on your phone at the store or job site" — this tells me it's mobile-ready
- "Your contractor logs expenses, you approve them" — this means I'm not the bottleneck
- Receipt photo capture is explicitly called out

**What makes me nervous:**
- I don't see any mention of how fast expense entry actually is. Can I log a $347.82 Home Depot run in 30 seconds? Because if it takes me 2 minutes to categorize, tag, and upload, I won't do it. I'll "do it later" and later never comes. Show me the flow. A 15-second GIF of someone logging an expense from their phone would be worth 1,000 words of copy.
- No mention of OCR or auto-categorization from receipts. If I snap a Home Depot receipt, does it auto-fill the amount and vendor? Or am I manually typing everything? This is the difference between "I'll actually use this" and "I used it for two weeks then stopped."
- The ChatGPT integration is interesting but feels like a nice-to-have. I don't want to "ask ChatGPT about my budget." I want to glance at a dashboard and see red/yellow/green. AI is a feature, not the selling point — don't let it overshadow the core workflow.

**Fix:** Add a section or visual showing the actual expense entry flow. "Log an expense in 3 taps: Snap receipt -> Confirm amount -> Done." If OCR exists, shout it from the rooftops. If it doesn't exist yet, build it — it's table stakes for mobile expense tracking in 2026.

---

### 4. Flipper-Specific Features: 6/10

This is where I'm both excited and frustrated. The bones are here, but the site doesn't go deep enough on the flipper use case.

**What's here and good:**
- Multi-project support (up to 8 on Plus plan — enough for most flippers)
- Per-project budget tracking
- Vendor management with cross-project comparison ("Compare costs across projects to spot who's overcharging" — yes, exactly)
- Contractor access with approval workflow
- Budget alerts

**What's missing for flippers specifically:**

1. **Purchase price + rehab + holding costs = all-in cost.** I don't just track "renovation budget." I track acquisition, closing costs, holding costs (insurance, utilities, loan interest), AND rehab. Does Fierro handle all of these or just the rehab line items? Not clear from the site.

2. **ARV and profit projection.** I need to enter my expected sale price and see my projected profit update in real time as costs come in. This is THE metric for a flipper. Every spreadsheet I've ever built has this as the top row.

3. **Draw schedule tracking.** I use hard money lenders. They release funds in draws based on project milestones. I need to track what I've drawn, what's remaining, and how my actual costs compare to my draw schedule. This is flipper-specific and no one does it well.

4. **Sold/closed project archival.** When I sell a flip, I want that project's data frozen as a reference. "On my last 10 flips, my average rehab was $X, my average overrun was Y%, and my most reliable sub was Z." That's how I get better at this business.

5. **Cost per square foot benchmarks.** If I know my average rehab cost per sqft across my last 5 flips, I can underwrite my next deal in 10 minutes instead of 2 hours.

**Fix:** You don't need to build all of this on day one, but the SITE should signal that you understand this workflow. Even a line like "Track your full deal — from purchase to sale — not just the rehab" would tell me you get it. Right now the site reads like it's primarily a renovation budget tracker that also works for flippers. Flip the framing: it's a flip profitability tool that also works for homeowners.

---

### 5. Mobile / On-Site Usability: 7/10

**What's promising:**
- "Snap a receipt on your phone at the hardware store" — they explicitly call out the on-site use case
- "Track from anywhere" — phone and laptop in sync
- Receipt photo capture exists

**What's unclear:**
- Is there a native mobile app or is this a responsive web app? The site never says. This matters enormously. A PWA that I have to open in Safari is friction I won't tolerate at 7am on a job site with drywall dust on my hands. A native app with a home screen icon that opens in 1 second — that I'll use.
- Offline support? Job sites don't always have signal. If I can't log an expense because I'm in a basement with no cell service, it's useless in the exact moment I need it most.
- Camera integration for receipt capture — is it in-app or does it kick me to the phone's camera app and then I have to upload? The fewer taps, the more likely I use it.

**Fix:** If you have a native app, put "Download on the App Store" badges on the homepage. If it's a responsive web app, say "Works on any phone — no app to download." Either way, be explicit. Flippers will specifically look for this. Also: mention offline support if you have it. If you don't have it, put it on the roadmap and say "coming soon." It's a real need.

### 6. Pricing ROI: 9/10

This is where Fierro absolutely nails it.

**Free:** 1 project, good enough to try it on my current flip. No credit card. Zero risk. I can validate the tool before I spend a dime. Smart.

**Plus at $49/month:** 8 projects, 10 team members, unlimited contractors, vendor management, advanced analytics. Let me do the math: if this tool helps me catch ONE overrun of $2,000 on ONE flip, it's paid for itself for the entire year ($49 x 12 = $588). And I will absolutely catch more than one overrun. The ROI is obvious and immediate.

**What's great about the pricing page:**
- The FAQ "Can't I just use a spreadsheet?" answer is solid. "Spreadsheets don't alert you when your electrical budget is 80% spent with half the work remaining." That's real. That's happened to me.
- "I'm just doing one renovation — is this worth it?" — addressing the objection directly. Good.
- Annual billing saves ~$118/year. Not a huge discount but enough to signal commitment.

**What could be better:**
- The pricing page should explicitly calculate ROI for flippers: "The average flip has $8,000-15,000 in cost overruns. Fierro pays for itself if it catches even one." Put the math right on the page. Make the decision a no-brainer.
- The jump from $20 (Home) to $49 (Plus) is reasonable, but the feature gap is significant. Vendor management is Plus-only? That's arguably the most valuable feature for someone doing repeat business with subs. Consider including basic vendor tracking in Home, with advanced analytics (cross-project comparison) in Plus.

**Fix:** Add an ROI calculator or at minimum a "pays for itself" statement with real numbers on the pricing page. Flippers think in terms of deal economics. Show them the math.

### 7. "Game Changer" Factor: 6/10

Here's the honest truth: Fierro is solving a real problem, and the core feature set is right. But right now, based on what the site communicates, it feels like a better spreadsheet rather than a fundamentally different tool.

**What would make it a game changer:**

1. **The "am I going to make money on this flip?" dashboard.** One screen. Purchase price, rehab budget, rehab spent, projected remaining, holding costs, ARV, projected profit. Updated in real time. Color-coded. If my profit projection drops below my minimum threshold, it screams at me. THAT is a game changer. The site hints at this but never shows it.

2. **Historical deal analytics.** "Your average rehab cost is $32/sqft. This flip is trending at $41/sqft." That kind of insight turns expense tracking into a competitive advantage. It helps me bid smarter on my next deal.

3. **The contractor logging expenses for me.** This is mentioned but undersold. If my GC can log costs and I just approve them, that saves me 80% of the data entry. That's the killer feature for flippers. Lead with it harder.

4. **Lender-ready reports.** Hard money lenders want to see where their money went. If Fierro can generate a draw request package with receipts attached, that saves me hours per draw. The site mentions "export reports for your lender" but doesn't emphasize how painful this process is today and how much time Fierro saves.

**What holds it back:**
The site positions Fierro as a cost tracker. That's necessary but not sufficient. The game changer positioning is: "Fierro tells you if your flip is going to be profitable — and warns you the moment it's not." Tracking is a feature. Profitability insight is the product.

**Fix:** Reframe the narrative from "track your costs" to "protect your profit." Every feature should ladder up to: "and that's how you make sure this flip is profitable." The emotional hook isn't "know where your money went" — it's "make sure you actually make money."

---

### 8. Overall "Would I Pay For This?": 8/10

**Yes, I would pay for this.** Here's why:

- $49/month is nothing against the size of my deals
- My current system (spreadsheets + notes + memory) has cost me real money in missed overruns
- The contractor can log expenses — that alone might be worth it
- Multi-project tracking means I'm not juggling 5 separate spreadsheets
- The free tier lets me validate before committing

**What would make it a 10/10:**

1. Show me a real flipper's dashboard. Screenshots, a demo video, something. Let me see what my daily experience would look like. The site is all words, no visuals of the actual product. I want to see the UI. Is it clean? Is it fast? Can I parse it at a glance between talking to my tile guy and my electrician?

2. Speak my language more consistently. "Sub-projects" — do you mean rooms? Budget categories? Phases? I'd call them "cost categories" or "budget lines." The terminology should match how I already think about my rehab budget.

3. Address the switching cost. I have 14 months of data in Google Sheets. Can I import it? If I start using Fierro mid-flip, can I enter historical expenses? The site doesn't address onboarding for someone who's already mid-project.

4. Show me the vendor insights. "Compare costs across projects to spot who's overcharging" is mentioned once and never expanded. This is enormously valuable. I want to know that my plumber charges me 30% more than my electrician relative to market rates. I want to see who comes in on budget and who always has change orders. Give this feature a whole section.

## Top 5 Changes That Would Make This a 10/10 for Flippers

1. **Add a Deal P&L view.** Purchase + Rehab + Holding = All-in vs. ARV = Projected Profit. Update it in real time. This is the single most important thing for a flipper. Everything else is a feature; this is the product.

2. **Show the product.** Screenshots, a demo GIF, a 60-second walkthrough video. I need to see what my daily experience looks like. The site is entirely text-based for describing a visual tool. That's a miss.

3. **Lead with the flipper use case or create a dedicated landing page.** The homeowner/DIY angle dilutes the urgency. A flipper-specific page that says "You're juggling 4 flips, 12 contractors, and $600K in rehab budgets — here's how you stay profitable" would convert me immediately.

4. **Emphasize contractor-as-data-entry.** The fact that my GC can log expenses and I just approve them is the single biggest time-saver in the product. It means I don't have to choose between running my business and tracking my costs. Make this a hero feature, not a bullet point.

5. **Build (and show) vendor intelligence.** "Your plumber averaged $4,200 per flip across your last 6 properties. Your electrician has had change orders on 4 of 6 projects." This turns Fierro from a cost tracker into a business intelligence tool. That's the moat. That's what no spreadsheet can do.

---

## Bottom Line

Fierro is 80% of the way to being the tool every flipper needs. The core is solid: real-time budget tracking, multi-project support, contractor access, mobile receipt capture, and alerts. The pricing is fair and the ROI is obvious.

What's missing is the last 20% — the part that turns "I track my costs" into "I know exactly how much money I'm making on every flip, every day, and I'm getting better at this business because my data tells me what's working." That's the difference between a tool I use and a tool I can't live without.

The website needs to stop hedging between homeowners and investors. Pick the highest-stakes user — the flipper — and build the narrative around protecting profit margins. The homeowner use case can be a secondary page. But the person who will pay $49/month without hesitation, tell every investor friend about it, and churn the least? That's me. Talk to me.
