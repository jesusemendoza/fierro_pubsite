# Fierro Website Review: Real Estate Investor Perspective

**Reviewer profile:** Full-time real estate investor. 12 active properties across two markets. Running 5-8 rehabs at any given time. Team of 3 PMs, rotating roster of ~15 contractors. Currently using QuickBooks for accounting, a Google Sheets monster for project budgets, and CoConstruct for one JV partner who insists on it. Annual rehab spend north of $2M. I care about cost basis, margin protection, and giving my CPA clean numbers in February.

**Pages reviewed:**
- https://getfierro.com/
- https://getfierro.com/pricing
- https://getfierro.com/why-fierro

---

## The Honest Take

I spent 20 minutes going through every page. Here is where Fierro stands for someone running a real portfolio, not just one kitchen remodel.

---

## Does It Solve My #1 Problem?

My #1 problem is knowing exact project costs across multiple properties in real time so I can make hold/sell/refinance decisions with real numbers instead of gut feelings.

**Fierro partially addresses this.** The core concept — one dashboard per project, budget vs. actual by category, all expenses logged — is exactly right. The "all-in cost per property in real time" copy on the homepage tells me someone on this team has actually flipped a house. That line alone kept me reading.

**But there are gaps.** I need to know my cost basis (purchase price + rehab + holding costs + closing costs), not just my rehab budget. The site never once mentions "cost basis," "ARV," "holding costs," or "all-in cost" as a calculated field. If Fierro only tracks the renovation line items and not the full investment picture, it solves 60% of my problem. That other 40% is what makes or breaks a deal.

**Fix:** Add a section or FAQ explicitly addressing how investors can track non-renovation costs (acquisition, carrying costs, financing). If the product supports this, say so loudly. If it does not, build it. This is the difference between "nice budget tool" and "indispensable investor tool."

---

## Multi-Project Capability

The Plus tier gives me 8 projects. The pricing page description literally says "For investors & flippers managing multiple properties." Good — they know who I am.

**But 8 projects is not enough for a serious investor.** I have 5-8 rehabs active at any given time, plus I need to keep completed projects accessible for tax reference and warranty tracking. If a completed project counts against my 8-project limit, I will hit the wall within my first year. The next step up is "Builder" at custom pricing, which means a sales call, which means friction.

The sub-project structure (20 per project on Plus) makes sense for budget categories within a rehab (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, framing, etc.). That mapping is intuitive.

**What's missing:** There is no mention of a portfolio-level rollup view. Can I see total spend across all 8 projects? Total remaining budget? Which project is most over budget? The homepage shows a "multi-property portfolio view" screenshot, which is encouraging, but the marketing copy never describes what portfolio-level analytics actually look like. I should not have to guess.

**Fix:**
1. Either raise the Plus tier to 15 projects or create an "Investor" tier between Plus and Builder at maybe $79-99/mo with 15-20 projects. Do not make serious investors call sales unless they are truly enterprise scale (50+ projects).
2. Explicitly describe portfolio rollup capabilities. "See total spend across all projects. Identify which property is over budget at a glance. Compare rehab cost per square foot across your portfolio." That copy writes itself.
3. Clarify what happens to completed projects. Can I archive them without losing data? Do they count against my limit?

---

## Financial Reporting & Export

The FAQ says "You own your data and can export it anytime." The feature showcase mentions "Export reports for your partner, lender, or accountant."

**That is frustratingly vague.** Export to what? CSV? PDF? Excel? QuickBooks-compatible format? Can I export by project, by vendor, by date range, by category? At tax time my CPA needs every expense categorized by property with receipt documentation. This is not a nice-to-have. It is literally why I would pay for this tool.

The comparison table shows "Budget analytics" ranging from "Summary" (Free) to "Custom dashboards" (Builder), but says nothing about export formats, scheduled reports, or data portability.

**Fix:**
1. Add a row to the comparison table: "Data export" with values like "CSV" / "CSV + PDF" / "CSV + PDF + QuickBooks" / "Custom."
2. On the homepage feature section for Analytics & Reporting, replace the vague "Export reports for your partner, lender, or accountant" with specifics: "Export project costs as CSV or PDF. Group by property, vendor, or category. Hand your CPA a clean file in February instead of a shoebox of receipts."
3. If QuickBooks integration exists or is planned, say it. QuickBooks compatibility is a buying trigger for every investor I know.

---

## Vendor Management

This is on Plus tier only, which is fair. The copy says: "Compare costs across projects to spot who's overcharging."

**That is the right idea, poorly sold.** Every investor has been burned by a contractor who charges $85/hour on one job and $110/hour on the next because they think you are not paying attention. If Fierro actually lets me see vendor spend across projects and compare, that is worth the subscription by itself.

**What I want to know and the site does not tell me:**
- Can I see total spend per vendor across all projects?
- Can I see average cost per vendor vs. other vendors in the same category?
- Can I track vendor reliability (on-time, on-budget completion)?
- Can I flag a vendor as preferred or blacklisted?

**Fix:** The vendor management feature deserves its own section on the Why Fierro page aimed at investors. Frame it as margin protection: "Your plumber charged you $12K on the last flip and $18K on this one. Same scope. Fierro shows you the pattern before you sign the next contract." That is a visceral, wallet-level pain point. Use it.

---

## Budget vs. Actual Tracking

This is the core of the product and the site communicates it well. The ChatGPT integration mockup showing "Your Electrical budget is 82% spent with an estimated 40% of work remaining" is genuinely compelling. That is the kind of insight that saves thousands of dollars.

Budget alerts before category overruns are exactly right. Every experienced investor has a "plumbing ate my margin" story. Catching it at 80% spent instead of 120% spent is the difference between a profitable flip and a lesson.

**What is missing:** Change order tracking. In the real world, budgets change. The GC finds knob-and-tube wiring, and suddenly your electrical budget doubles. I need to see original budget vs. revised budget vs. actual. The site never mentions change orders, budget revisions, or contingency tracking.

**Fix:** Add change order / budget revision tracking to the feature list if it exists. If it does not exist, build it. This is non-negotiable for construction budgeting. Also add contingency line items — every experienced investor budgets 10-15% contingency and needs to track draws against it.

## Pricing Analysis

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Projects | My Take |
|------|---------|--------|----------|---------|
| Free | $0 | $0 | 1 | Good for tire-kicking. Not useful for investors. |
| Home | $20 | $16 | 2 | Too few projects. Homeowner tier, not for me. |
| Plus | $49 | $39 | 8 | Close but not enough projects. |
| Builder | Custom | Custom | Unlimited | Sales call friction. |

**For context on value:** I spend $200K+ per rehab. A single overrun I catch early saves $2K-5K easily. Fierro at $49/month is $588/year. If it catches ONE overrun per year, the ROI is 3-8x. The tool pays for itself trivially.

**The problem is the project limit, not the price.** I would happily pay $79 or even $99/month for 15-20 projects with all Plus features. The jump from $49 to "call us" is a gap where real money walks away. Investors are not enterprises. We do not want to get on a call. We want to enter a credit card and start working.

**Fix:**
1. Add a tier between Plus and Builder. Call it "Portfolio" or "Investor Pro." $79-99/mo, 15-20 projects, everything in Plus plus basic API access. Self-serve signup. No sales call.
2. Clarify archived vs. active projects. If I can archive completed rehabs and they do not count against my limit, say so prominently. That changes the math.
3. The annual pricing savings ($118/year according to the page) should be emphasized more aggressively. Investors think in annual terms anyway.

## Comparison to Alternatives

The site never names a competitor. The FAQ handles "Can't I just use a spreadsheet?" well, but that is it.

Here is how Fierro stacks up from my chair:

**vs. Google Sheets / Excel:** Fierro wins on real-time collaboration, alerts, receipt attachment, and mobile capture. Spreadsheets win on flexibility, formulas, and the fact that they are free. Fierro needs to emphasize that spreadsheets do not send you an alert when electrical is 80% spent. That is the killer feature.

**vs. QuickBooks:** QuickBooks is an accounting tool, not a project budget tool. I use it for bookkeeping, not for tracking whether my plumber is over budget on the Elm Street flip. They are complementary, not competitive. Fierro should lean into this: "Fierro tracks your project. QuickBooks tracks your books. Export from one to the other."

**vs. CoConstruct / Buildertrend:** These are contractor-facing tools built for GCs to manage clients. They are heavy, expensive ($400+ /mo for CoConstruct), and designed for the builder, not the investor. If Fierro is lighter, cheaper, and investor-first, that is a real position. But the site never makes this comparison.

**vs. Buildium / AppFolio:** These are property management platforms for landlords collecting rent. Different problem. Not competitors.

**vs. Doing nothing:** Most investors do nothing and eat the overruns. The "cost of inaction" argument is the strongest sales pitch Fierro has, and the Why Fierro page uses it reasonably well.

**Fix:** Add a comparison section to the Why Fierro page. You do not need to trash competitors by name, but you should address the categories: "Already using a spreadsheet? Here is what you're missing." "Using a GC tool like CoConstruct? Here is why investors need their own view." "Using QuickBooks? Great — Fierro is the project-level layer QuickBooks does not have."

## API & Integrations

API access is Builder-tier only (custom pricing). ChatGPT integration is available across all tiers with escalating permissions.

**The ChatGPT angle is genuinely interesting** and I have not seen it from competitors. Being able to ask natural-language questions about my project finances is novel. The permission model (read-only at Free, configurable at Plus) shows good security thinking.

**But ChatGPT integration is not the same as API access.** I want to pull data into my own dashboards, connect to my accounting system, or feed data into my deal analysis spreadsheet. Locking API access behind a sales call is a mistake for a developer-friendly investor audience.

**Fix:**
1. Give Plus tier read-only API access. Let people pull their own data. It builds stickiness.
2. List specific integrations planned or available. Even a "Coming soon" roadmap for QuickBooks, Xero, or Zapier integration would signal seriousness.
3. The ChatGPT integration deserves a dedicated landing page or case study showing real investor workflows. "Ask ChatGPT to compare your rehab cost per square foot across your last 5 flips" is a demo that would go viral in investor forums.

## Trust & Credibility

**Zero social proof.** No testimonials, no case studies, no logos, no user count, no "trusted by X investors" badge. For a tool that handles financial data, this is a problem. I am not entering my project financials into a tool that has no evidence other people use it and trust it.

"Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest" is the bare minimum. It is like a restaurant saying "we wash our hands." Good, but not a differentiator.

**Fix:**
1. Get 3-5 investor testimonials with real numbers. "Fierro saved me $4,200 on the Elm Street flip by catching my electrical overrun at 78% budget." Specific beats generic.
2. Add a security page or section that goes deeper than one FAQ line. SOC 2? Data residency? Backup policy? Investors managing seven-figure portfolios need more than "we use Supabase."
3. If the product is new and has few users, be honest about it. "We're in early access. Here's our roadmap. Here's the team." Transparency beats fake credibility.

---

## Marketing Tone & Audience Targeting

The site speaks to three audiences: homeowners, flippers, and real estate teams. The homeowner messaging is front and center (hero screenshot is a kitchen remodel). The investor messaging is secondary.

**This is a strategic choice I disagree with for one reason:** homeowners remodel once every 10-15 years. They are low-LTV customers. Investors do 5-10+ projects per year. They are high-LTV customers who will pay more, stay longer, and refer others. The marketing should lead with investors and position homeowners as a secondary audience that benefits from the same tool.

The "Flippers & Investors" card in the audience section has the best copy on the entire site: "Know your all-in cost per property in real time. Compare rehab budgets across flips. Catch the overruns that kill your margins before closing day." That should be the homepage headline, not buried in a card halfway down the page.

**Fix:**
1. Consider an investor-specific landing page at `/investors` or `/flippers` that leads with investor language: margins, cost basis, ROI, portfolio-level views.
2. The current headline "Know exactly where your money goes" is good but generic. For investors, consider a variant: "Know your true cost on every deal." Shorter, sharper, speaks directly to the investment thesis.
3. The Why Fierro page pain points are excellent but skew homeowner (3 of 4 scenarios). Balance it to 2 homeowner, 2 investor, or add investor-specific pain points: "You closed on a flip and realized your actual rehab cost was $22K higher than you thought. That is not a bad flip — that is a tracking problem."

## The Bottom Line

Fierro is building the right product for a real problem. The core concept — real-time budget tracking per project with alerts and vendor tracking — is exactly what investors need and do not have today. The ChatGPT integration is a genuine differentiator that no competitor offers. The price point is reasonable.

**But the marketing is leaving money on the table.** The site sells to homeowners first and investors second, when the investor segment is more valuable. The export and reporting capabilities — the single most important feature for investors — are barely mentioned. There is zero social proof, which is fatal for a financial tool. And the project limits push serious investors into a sales call they do not want to make.

### If I could change three things tomorrow:

1. **Add social proof.** Get 3-5 real testimonials with real numbers on the homepage. Nothing else will move the trust needle faster.

2. **Show the export workflow.** Add a section or even just 2-3 bullet points: "Export by project as CSV. Export by vendor. Export by date range for tax filing. Give your CPA a clean file, not a shoebox." If this capability exists, it is criminal that the website does not mention it.

3. **Add an Investor/Portfolio tier at $79-99/mo.** 15-20 projects, self-serve signup, no sales call. This is the tier I would buy today if it existed.

### The opportunity:

The construction/renovation budget tracking space for investors is wide open. QuickBooks does not do it. CoConstruct is for GCs, not investors. Spreadsheets are painful. Fierro is the closest thing to a purpose-built solution I have seen. The gap between "closest thing" and "the obvious choice" is mostly a marketing problem, not a product problem. Close that gap and you have a winner.
