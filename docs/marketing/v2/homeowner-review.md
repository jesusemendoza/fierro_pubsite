# Fierro Website Review: Homeowner Persona

**Reviewer profile:** Homeowner. Not a contractor, not a flipper. I've done two renovations in the last five years — a kitchen remodel ($42K) and a bathroom gut ($18K). Both went over budget. I currently "track" costs with a Google Sheet I update when I remember, a folder of photos of receipts on my phone, and a general sense of dread. My contractor texts me invoices. I pay them and forget to log it. Then I get surprised.

**Date reviewed:** March 10, 2026
**Pages reviewed:** Homepage, Pricing, Why Fierro

---

## The Gut Reaction (First 10 Seconds)

The headline — "Know exactly where your money goes." — is clean. It's not trying to be clever. It tells me this is about money tracking, and the subheadline narrows it to renovations and flips. Within five seconds I know what this product does and roughly who it's for. That's better than 90% of SaaS landing pages.

But here's the thing: I almost bounced. "Whether you're remodeling your home or flipping your next investment" — I'm the first one, not the second one. The word "investment" and "flipping" immediately made me wonder if this is really for me, or if I'm the afterthought audience tacked onto a tool built for real estate investors. That suspicion came back multiple times as I went through the site.

**What I liked:** No jargon. No "revolutionize your workflow." No abstract geometric animations. It feels like a real product.

**What gave me pause:** The screenshots show a "Kitchen Remodel" project, which is literally my use case — but the multi-property portfolio view and "Real Estate Teams" segment made me feel like the homeowner use case is the free-tier bait, not the real product.

---

## Page-by-Page Breakdown

### Homepage

**The good:**
- "See Every Dollar in Real Time" — yes, this is my problem. I never know where I stand mid-project. I'm either asking my contractor "where are we at?" or pretending the budget is fine until it isn't.
- "Catch Overruns Before They Hurt" — this is literally what I want. My kitchen remodel went $7K over budget and I didn't know until the granite countertops were already installed and the invoice showed up.
- The three-step getting started flow ("Set Your Budget, Track Everything, Stay on Budget") is straightforward. Five-minute setup sounds believable and not intimidating.
- Mobile receipt capture — this is the killer feature for me. I'm standing at Home Depot and I just paid $340 for tile. I need to snap that receipt right now or it's gone forever.

**The bad:**
- "Connect Your AI Tools" and "ChatGPT integration" — I don't care about this. At all. I'm not going to ask ChatGPT "am I over budget on electrical?" I'm going to open the app and look at a number. This feels like a feature built to impress Product Hunt voters, not to solve my problem. It takes up valuable real estate on the homepage that could be used to show me something I actually need, like "see all your receipts organized by category" or "your contractor can log expenses directly so you don't have to chase them."
- "Real Estate Teams" as a target audience on the homepage dilutes the message. If I'm a homeowner doing one kitchen remodel, seeing "portfolio-wide cost visibility" makes me feel like this tool is overkill for me. Pick your audience on the homepage or at least lead with homeowners.
- Six feature categories is a lot to scan. Budget Tracking, Expense Management, and Analytics overlap in my mind. I don't know the difference between "budget tracking" and "expense management" — to me, they're the same thing. Consolidate these or explain the distinction in human terms.

**The ugly:**
- No testimonials. No social proof. Not one quote from a real person saying "I used this on my bathroom remodel and it saved me $3,000." This is a massive trust gap. I'm being asked to put my financial data into a product I've never heard of, made by a company I've never heard of, and there isn't a single human voice vouching for it. More on this below.

### Pricing Page

**The good:**
- Free tier with 1 project and 6 sub-projects — this is actually perfect for a homeowner doing a single renovation. Kitchen remodel with 6 categories (demo, electrical, plumbing, cabinets, countertops, flooring) — that works. Smart.
- "No credit card required" — good. I can try it without committing.
- The FAQ that says "The Free tier gives you 1 project with 6 budget categories — enough for a simple remodel" — this is the most reassuring sentence on the entire website. It tells me someone actually thought about my use case. Move this higher.
- The FAQ about contractor access is great: "Invite your contractor as a team member. They can log expenses and receipts directly into your budget." This is a huge selling point buried in an FAQ. This should be on the homepage hero section.

**The bad:**
- $20/month for the Home tier. Let me do the math. My kitchen remodel took 4 months. That's $80. My bathroom took 2.5 months — call it $60. So I'm paying $60-80 per project to track expenses that I could track in a spreadsheet for free. That's not outrageous, but it's not an impulse purchase either. For $80 I need to be very confident this is saving me more than $80 in caught overruns or time savings. The site doesn't make that case with any specificity.
- Annual billing at $192/year — this makes no sense for a homeowner. I'm not renovating year-round. I need this tool for 3-6 months, then I'm done until my next project (which might be 2 years from now). Annual billing is an investor/flipper feature. For homeowners, consider a per-project pricing model or a pause option: "Done with your project? Freeze your account and come back when the next one starts."
- The jump from Free (1 project, 2 team members) to Home ($20/mo, 2 projects, 5 team members) is reasonable, but the jump to Plus ($49/mo) is steep for a homeowner. I'd never need 8 projects. The tiers feel designed around the investor persona, not the homeowner.
- 100 MB receipt storage on the free tier is tight. Phone photos are 3-5 MB each. That's 20-33 receipts. A kitchen remodel easily generates 50+ receipts from materials alone. I'd hit that wall fast and feel nickel-and-dimed into upgrading. Either make the free tier more generous on storage (500 MB would be plenty for a single project) or be upfront about this limit so I don't get frustrated.

**The ugly:**
- "Data security" FAQ: "We use Supabase for infrastructure and follow industry best practices." This is meaningless to me. I don't know what Supabase is. "Industry best practices" is the emptiest phrase in tech. If you're SOC 2 compliant, say it. If you have specific encryption standards, name them. If you don't have real security credentials yet, at least tell me where my data is hosted (US? EU?) and that you don't sell it. Be specific or don't bring it up.

### Why Fierro Page

**The good:**
- This is the best page on the site. The pain points are specific and real:
  - "Your contractor sent you a bill and you have no idea if it's right" — YES. This is my life. My plumber sent me a bill for $4,200 and I had to go back through texts and photos to figure out if that was the full amount or a partial.
  - "You set a budget but you have no idea if you're actually on track" — the $35K kitchen remodel example with "unclear if at $18K or $28K spent" is exactly the anxiety I feel mid-project.
  - "You didn't realize the flip was over budget until closing day" — this is the investor version but the emotion translates.
- The conversational tone works. This doesn't read like marketing copy, it reads like someone who's been through this.

**The bad:**
- No specifics on HOW Fierro solves each pain point. You describe the problem beautifully, then the solution is vague: "Fierro gives you and your contractor one shared place to log every cost." OK, but what does that look like? Show me. A screenshot of a contractor's view vs. my view. A gif of someone snapping a receipt and it auto-categorizing. Something concrete.
- The "Your AI Tools, Your Data" section feels out of place on this page. I was nodding along to real renovation problems, and then suddenly we're talking about ChatGPT integration. It's a momentum killer. Save this for a features page or put it last.
- No customer stories or testimonials here either. This page is begging for a pull quote: "I caught a $2,400 double charge from my electrician because Fierro showed me the duplicate." — Sarah, homeowner in Austin. Even if you have to get beta users to give quotes, this page needs real voices.

**The ugly:**
- Zero statistics. "Renovations and flips go over budget" — by how much? How often? The National Association of Home Builders says the average renovation goes 10-20% over budget. Use real data to make the problem feel urgent and quantified. Then you can say "Fierro users catch overruns an average of 3 weeks earlier" (or whatever the real number is).

## Scoring

### 1. First Impression / Hook: 7/10
The headline is strong and clear. I know what this is within 5 seconds. Loses points because the dual homeowner/investor positioning creates a split-second "is this for me?" hesitation, and the lack of any social proof means I'm taking the claim on faith.

### 2. Value Proposition Clarity: 7/10
"Track every dollar, catch every overrun, stay on budget" — clear and compelling. But the feature sections blur together (budget tracking vs. expense management vs. analytics) and the AI integration pitch muddies the core message. The value prop is strongest on the Why Fierro page, which most visitors won't reach.

### 3. Pain Point Resonance: 8/10
This is where Fierro is strongest. The scenarios on the Why Fierro page are pulled from my actual life. The contractor bill you can't verify, the budget you can't track, the surprise at the end — these are real. The homepage is more generic; the Why Fierro page is where the emotional connection lives. Consider swapping their energy.

### 4. Pricing Fairness: 5/10
The free tier is a great on-ramp for a single simple project, but the 100 MB storage limit will frustrate real users fast. $20/month is reasonable but hard to justify when you only need it for 3-4 months and a spreadsheet is free. The annual plan makes no sense for homeowners. No per-project option or pause feature is a miss. The pricing is designed for repeat users (investors), and homeowners are stuck paying a subscription for a one-time need.

**Fix:** Offer a "Project Pass" — $49 one-time for a single project, valid for 6 months. Or let users pause their subscription between projects. This alone would convert spreadsheet users who balk at ongoing monthly costs.

### 5. Trust & Credibility: 3/10
This is the weakest area by far. No testimonials. No customer logos. No case studies. No named humans behind the product. No security certifications. No "about us" or team page (that I could find). No press mentions. The security FAQ is vague. I'm being asked to enter my financial data — contractor payments, budget details, receipt photos — into a tool with zero social proof. For a financial product, this is a dealbreaker for cautious homeowners. I'd wonder if this company will still exist in 6 months.

**Fix (in priority order):**
1. Get 3-5 beta user testimonials with real names and project types. Even short ones.
2. Add an "About" or "Team" page — who built this and why?
3. Get specific on security: where is data hosted, encryption standards, data ownership/portability.
4. Add a "Built on [specific infrastructure]" badge if you use credible providers.
5. Offer a data export guarantee: "You can export all your data anytime, in CSV and PDF formats."

### 6. Feature Completeness (for my needs): 7/10
The core feature set — budget setup, expense tracking, receipt capture, contractor access — covers what I need. Vendor management and team permissions are nice-to-haves. The mobile receipt capture is the feature that would actually change my behavior day-to-day.

**What's missing:**
- Photo receipt OCR (auto-extract amount, vendor, date from receipt photos) — if this exists, it's not mentioned. This would be the single biggest time-saver.
- Integration with banks or credit cards to auto-import transactions. Even a CSV import of bank statements would help.
- A "project completion" summary/report I can keep for my records (or give to my insurance company, or reference when selling my house).
- Shareable budget view for my spouse. Not "team member" — just a simple read-only link I can text to my wife so she can see where we are without creating an account.

### 7. "Why Not Just Use a Spreadsheet?" Factor: 5/10
The site acknowledges this question in the pricing FAQ but doesn't kill it. The answer mentions "budget alerts, contractor expense logging, real-time dashboards" — but budget alerts are only in the paid tier, and I can share a Google Sheet with my contractor too.

The real answer to "why not a spreadsheet" is: **because you won't update it.** The spreadsheet requires discipline. Fierro (presumably) reduces friction — snap a receipt, it's logged. Your contractor logs their own expenses. You get alerts instead of having to remember to check. The site hints at this but never says it directly.

**Fix:** Add a section on the homepage or Why Fierro page: "The spreadsheet works great... until it doesn't." Then show the real comparison — not features vs. features, but behavior vs. behavior. "With a spreadsheet, you have to remember to log every expense. With Fierro, your contractor logs it for you, your receipts are captured on your phone, and you get alerted when something's off."

### 8. Overall "Would I Pay For This?": 5/10
Right now, probably not. Here's my honest thought process:

I'd sign up for the free tier to try it on my next project. If the mobile receipt capture is genuinely fast and frictionless, and if I can invite my contractor and they actually use it, I might upgrade to the $20/month Home tier for the budget alerts and extra storage. But $20/month feels like a lot for something I use for 3-4 months and then stop. I'd probably cancel the moment the project wraps and go back to my spreadsheet for the next one.

To get me to a "shut up and take my money" moment, I'd need:
1. Proof that other homeowners use it and love it (testimonials).
2. A pricing model that respects the fact that I'm not renovating year-round (per-project or pause option).
3. Receipt OCR that auto-extracts data so I'm not manually typing amounts.
4. A clear demonstration that my contractor will actually use this (a "contractor onboarding" flow or a video showing how easy it is for them).
5. A completion report: "Your kitchen remodel cost $44,200 across 6 categories. Here's everything, organized and exportable."

## Competitor Comparison (From My Perspective)

| What I'd Compare Fierro To | My Take |
|---|---|
| **Google Sheets / Excel** | Free, flexible, I already know how to use it. Zero friction to start. But no receipt capture, no alerts, no contractor collaboration. If Fierro can't demonstrably beat this in ease-of-use, I'm not switching. |
| **Mint / Monarch Money** | I already track personal finances here. But these tools don't understand "projects" or "budgets for a specific renovation." Fierro wins on structure. |
| **QuickBooks Self-Employed** | Overkill for a homeowner. Designed for businesses. But if I were a flipper, I might already be using this and Fierro would need to show me why it's better. |
| **Buildertrend / CoConstruct** | Contractor-side tools. Too complex, too expensive, not designed for the homeowner to manage. But they have the social proof and market presence Fierro lacks. |
| **Doing nothing** | The real competitor. Most homeowners just wing it. Fierro's biggest battle is convincing me that the effort of learning a new tool is worth the payoff. |

## The Three Things That Would Make Me Say "Shut Up and Take My Money"

1. **Show me the receipts (literally).** A 30-second video of someone snapping a receipt photo, having it auto-categorize and extract the amount, and seeing the budget update in real time. If that flow is as smooth as scanning a QR code, I'm in. If it requires me to manually type in amounts and pick categories from a dropdown, I'm out.

2. **Prove other homeowners love it.** Three real testimonials from people who did kitchen/bathroom/basement remodels. With dollar amounts saved or overruns caught. Named people, not "J.S. from California." Trust is the #1 gap on this site.

3. **Price it like a project, not a subscription.** $49 for a 6-month project pass. I'd pay that without thinking. $20/month indefinitely for something I use 3-4 months every couple of years? I'd agonize over it and probably not.

---

## Final Verdict

Fierro is solving a real problem that I genuinely have. The Why Fierro page proves the team understands the pain. The core features are the right ones. But the site doesn't close the deal because it lacks trust signals, prices like a SaaS for power users instead of a tool for occasional homeowners, and doesn't show me the product in action enough to overcome my spreadsheet inertia.

The bones are good. The pain point understanding is excellent. The execution gap is in trust and pricing psychology. Fix those two things and you have a product that homeowners would recommend to each other — which is how this market works. Nobody Googles "renovation budget tracker." They ask their neighbor what they used.
