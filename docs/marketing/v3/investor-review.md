# Fierro Website Review: Real Estate Investor Perspective (v3)

**Reviewer profile:** Full-time real estate investor. 14 properties across three markets. Running 6-9 rehabs at any given time with another 3-4 in various stages of acquisition or disposition. Team of 4 PMs, a full-time acquisitions person, and a rotating roster of ~20 contractors. Currently using QuickBooks Online for bookkeeping, a 47-tab Google Sheets nightmare for project budgets, CoConstruct for one JV partner who refuses to leave it, and my phone's Notes app for things I should not admit. Annual rehab spend north of $2.5M. I care about cost basis, margin protection, closing-day accuracy, and giving my CPA clean numbers in February so she does not fire me as a client.

**Pages reviewed:**
- https://getfierro.com/
- https://getfierro.com/pricing
- https://getfierro.com/why-fierro

**Review context:** This is a v3 review. I reviewed v2 of this site previously and gave it 41/80 (51%). The core criticisms were: homeowner-first messaging, insufficient project limits, invisible export capabilities, zero social proof, and a pricing gap between Plus and Builder. Let us see what has changed.

---

## Does It Solve My #1 Problem?

My #1 problem is deceptively simple to state and brutally hard to solve: I need to know the exact cost of every property at any point in time so I can make real decisions — hold, sell, refinance, pull out, double down — with real numbers.

**Fierro's homepage headline nails the problem statement:** "Know exactly where your money goes." Clean. Direct. No jargon. The subheading expands it: "Whether you're remodeling your home or flipping your next investment — track every dollar, catch every overrun, and stay on budget." The word "investment" is right there in the hero. Good.

**The "Flippers & Investors" audience card is still the best copy on the entire site:** "Know your all-in cost per property in real time. Compare rehab budgets across flips. Catch the overruns that kill your margins before closing day." This is the sentence that would get me to click "Start Free." It speaks my language — all-in cost, margins, closing day. Whoever wrote this has been in a closing room staring at a HUD-1 wondering where an extra $15K came from.

**But the gap from v2 remains.** The site still does not mention "cost basis," "ARV," "holding costs," "carrying costs," or "closing costs" anywhere. For an investor, rehab costs are maybe 60-70% of your total project cost. The rest is acquisition, financing, insurance, property taxes during the hold, utilities, and disposition costs. If Fierro only tracks the renovation line items, it solves the biggest chunk of the problem, but not the whole thing. The sub-project structure (up to 20 per project on Plus) could theoretically accommodate these as additional budget categories — "Acquisition," "Carrying Costs," "Selling Costs" — but the site never frames it that way.

**Fix:** This is a marketing problem, not necessarily a product problem. Add one sentence to the investor audience card: "Track everything — rehab costs, holding costs, acquisition costs, closing costs — all in one project view." If the product supports arbitrary budget categories (which sub-projects suggest it does), then say it. Show an example project with categories like "Kitchen Reno," "Plumbing," "Electrical," but also "Carrying Costs," "Insurance," "Loan Interest." That single screenshot would tell investors: this tool gets my whole deal, not just my contractor invoices.

## Multi-Project Capability

The Plus tier now shows **8 projects** (up from the 5 I recall in earlier copy — good move if intentional, though the homepage still says "5 projects" in the pricing preview section while the pricing page says 8; this inconsistency needs to be fixed). The pricing page description says "For flippers and investors managing multiple properties." They know who they are talking to.

**8 projects is better. It is still not enough for a serious investor.** Here is my math: I have 6-9 active rehabs at any time. Projects take 3-6 months. Even if I could archive completed projects (which the site STILL does not address), I would hit the 8-project ceiling during my busy season. And I need completed projects accessible for at least 3 years for tax purposes, warranty claims, and deal post-mortems.

The sub-project structure (20 per project on Plus) maps well to budget categories within a rehab: demolition, framing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, drywall, flooring, kitchen, bathrooms, exterior, landscaping, permits, contingency, etc. Twenty categories is plenty for even complex rehabs.

**What is still missing: portfolio-level rollup views.** Can I see total spend across all 8 projects on one screen? Can I compare cost-per-square-foot across my active flips? Can I sort projects by "most over budget" so I know where to focus my attention today? The site implies this exists (the feature copy mentions "Compare costs across projects") but never shows or describes what it looks like.

**Fix:**
1. Add an "Investor" or "Portfolio" tier at $79-99/mo with 15-20 projects. Self-serve. No sales call. This is the tier that serious investors would buy without hesitation. The jump from $49/8 projects to "call us for unlimited" is where you lose real revenue.
2. Explicitly address project archiving: "Completed projects can be archived and accessed anytime without counting against your active project limit." If this feature exists, advertising it is free money. If it does not exist, build it — it is table stakes.
3. Add 2-3 sentences describing portfolio rollup views: "See total spend across every active project. Spot which property is most over budget. Compare rehab costs per square foot across your portfolio." Even if the product is not there yet, this tells investors you understand their workflow.

## Financial Reporting & Export

The homepage says: "Export reports for your partner, lender, or accountant."

The FAQ says: "You own your data and can export it anytime."

**That is it. That is the entire export story on this website.**

I cannot overstate how much this matters. At tax time, my CPA needs:
- Every expense categorized by property (for Schedule E or as cost basis for flips)
- Receipt documentation for every expense over $75 (and ideally all of them)
- Vendor 1099 information (total payments per contractor per year)
- Categorization that maps to IRS categories
- Clean, structured data — not a dashboard screenshot

The site tells me I can "export." It does not tell me: export to what format? CSV? PDF? Excel? Can I filter by date range? By property? By vendor? Can I generate a report that says "Here is everything I paid to ABC Plumbing across all properties in 2025"? Can I pull a year-end summary per property that my CPA can plug directly into my return?

**This is the single most important feature for investor buyers, and it is invisible on the website.** I would argue it is more important than real-time budget tracking. Real-time tracking saves me money during the project. Export saves me money (and pain, and audit risk) every single February for the rest of my investing career.

The comparison table has a "Budget analytics" row showing "Summary" to "Custom dashboards" — but "analytics" and "export" are different things. I can have the best dashboard in the world, but if I cannot get the data out of your system in a format my CPA can use, the dashboard is just a pretty picture.

**Fix:**
1. Add a dedicated row to the comparison table: "Data export" with specifics per tier — e.g., "CSV" / "CSV + PDF reports" / "CSV + PDF + scheduled reports" / "Custom + API."
2. Replace the vague "Export reports for your partner, lender, or accountant" with: "Export project costs as CSV or PDF. Filter by property, vendor, date range, or category. Generate year-end summaries your CPA will actually thank you for."
3. If QuickBooks Online integration exists or is on the roadmap, scream it from the rooftop. "Export to QuickBooks" is three words that would make at least a third of your investor prospects stop evaluating and start paying.
4. Add a sentence about receipt exports: "Download all receipts for a project as a ZIP file for your records." Investors who have been audited know why this matters.

## Vendor Management

Vendor management is Plus-tier only. The feature copy says: "Compare costs across projects to spot who's overcharging."

**This feature alone could justify the subscription.** Let me tell you a story that every investor has lived: I used the same electrician on three consecutive flips. Same scope — rough-in and finish for a 3/2 house around 1,400 sqft. First flip: $8,200. Second flip: $9,800. Third flip: $12,400. I did not catch the escalation until I went back through my spreadsheet months later. By then, I had already paid him. That is $4,200 in margin erosion from one contractor over six months, and I am supposedly paying attention.

If Fierro can show me vendor spend across projects on a single screen — "Here is what you have paid ABC Electric on every project, ever" — that is worth more than the subscription cost per year. The copy hints at this but does not drive it home.

**What I want to know that the site does not answer:**
- Can I see total spend per vendor across ALL projects? (Not just per project?)
- Can I compare two vendors who do the same type of work? (Plumber A vs. Plumber B across similar scopes?)
- Does it track vendor-level metrics over time? (Average cost, payment history, project count?)
- Can I add notes to vendors? ("Great work but slow" / "Never again — walked off job site")
- Can I see which vendors have outstanding invoices vs. fully paid?

**Fix:** Vendor management is an investor's margin protection system. Sell it that way. The Why Fierro page should have a dedicated pain point for investors: "Your contractor charged you $8K last time and $12K this time. Same house. Same scope. Fierro shows you the pattern before you sign the next bid." That copy writes itself because the pain is real and universal.

## Budget vs. Actual Tracking

This is the heart of the product, and Fierro communicates it well. The value proposition "Catch Overruns Before They Hurt" with "Get alerts when a category is trending over budget" is the feature that differentiates this from a spreadsheet. My spreadsheet does not send me a text when electrical hits 80% spent with half the work remaining. Fierro apparently does.

The ChatGPT integration angle is still novel: "'Am I over budget on electrical?' Get real answers from your real data." I have not seen this from CoConstruct, Buildertrend, or any other tool in this space. The ability to ask a natural-language question about my project finances and get an answer is genuinely useful. I can see myself at a job site, pulling out my phone, and asking "What have I spent on the Oak Street flip this month?" instead of opening a spreadsheet and scrolling.

The permission model for AI access (read-only at Free, read & write at Home, configurable per project at Plus) shows thoughtful security design. I do not want my ChatGPT integration accidentally approving expenses.

**What is still missing:** Change order tracking. In the real world, budgets are not static. The GC opens a wall and finds mold. The inspector requires a new electrical panel. The buyer's agent wants different countertops. I need to see: original budget, approved change orders, revised budget, actual spend. The site never mentions change orders, budget revisions, or contingency management.

**Also missing:** Committed costs vs. actual spend. If I have a signed contract with my GC for $45K but have only paid $22K so far, my "remaining budget" should account for that $23K commitment. This is the difference between "how much have I spent" and "how much am I going to spend." Both matter.

**Fix:**
1. Add change order / budget revision tracking to the feature list. "See original budget, approved changes, and revised targets side by side. Know what you planned, what changed, and where you stand."
2. Add committed vs. actual tracking: "Track signed contracts and open purchase orders alongside actual payments. Know your true exposure, not just your spend to date."
3. Contingency tracking: "Set a 10-15% contingency budget. Track draws against it. Know how much cushion you have left." Every experienced investor budgets contingency. The tool should support it explicitly.

## Pricing Analysis

| Tier | Monthly | Annual (effective) | Projects | Sub-projects/project | Team Members | My Take |
|------|---------|-------------------|----------|---------------------|--------------|---------|
| Free | $0 | $0 | 1 | 6 | 2 | Good for kicking tires on one deal. Not a real investor tool. |
| Home | $20 | ~$16 | 2 | 15 | 5 | For the homeowner doing a remodel. Not for me. |
| Plus | $49 | ~$39 | 8 | 20 | 10 | The investor tier. Close but project count is still tight. |
| Builder | Custom | Custom | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited | Enterprise. Requires a sales call. Not for me. |

**The ROI math is dead simple.** I spend $200K+ per rehab. A single overrun I catch at 80% instead of 120% saves $3K-8K easily. One vendor I renegotiate because I have the data saves $2K-5K per project. Fierro at $49/month is $588/year. If it prevents ONE overrun or ONE vendor overcharge per year, the ROI is 5-15x. The tool pays for itself before I finish my first project. This is not a "nice to have" price point — this is rounding error on a single rehab.

**The problem is still the project count, not the price.** I would pay $79/mo or $99/mo right now for 15-20 projects with all Plus features. I would not call a sales team. The gap between $49/8 projects and "call us/unlimited" is where you lose investors who are ready to buy. We are not enterprises. We do not have procurement departments. We have credit cards and no patience for sales calls.

**Contractors are free on Home and Plus tiers.** This is smart and the site should emphasize it more. The fact that my GC can log expenses into my project without paying for a seat removes a massive adoption barrier. On CoConstruct, everyone pays. On Fierro, the investor pays and the contractors use it for free. That is a real competitive advantage buried in a comparison table row.

**Fix:**
1. Add a "Portfolio" or "Investor Pro" tier: $79-99/mo, 15-20 projects, all Plus features, read-only API access, self-serve signup. This is the tier that converts "interested" investors into paying customers.
2. Make the "contractors are free" point more prominent. "Your contractors log expenses for free. You pay once — they benefit too." That removes the "can I get my GC to use this?" objection.
3. Clarify what happens when a project is done. "Archive completed projects. Access your data anytime for tax purposes or deal post-mortems. Archived projects don't count against your active limit." Three sentences. Massive anxiety reduction.
4. The annual pricing saves up to $118/year. Fine. But frame it as an investment: "Bill annually and save $118 — that's two months free." Investors understand annual commitments.

## Comparison to Alternatives

The site still does not name a single competitor. The FAQ handles the spreadsheet comparison reasonably well. But investors are comparing Fierro to specific alternatives, and the site leaves that comparison entirely to me. Most visitors will not do the work.

Here is how I see the landscape:

**vs. Google Sheets / Excel:**
The incumbent. Every investor starts here. Fierro wins on: real-time alerts (spreadsheets do not ping you when electrical is 80% spent), receipt attachment (no more "Receipts" folder on Google Drive with 400 unsorted photos), mobile capture (snap and categorize at the job site), and multi-user collaboration (my PM and I see the same data without merge conflicts). Spreadsheets win on: flexibility (custom formulas, pivot tables, whatever I want), cost ($0), and familiarity. The honest truth: Fierro needs to be 10x better at the things that matter — alerting, receipt management, vendor tracking — to overcome the switching cost of leaving a spreadsheet I have used for years.

**vs. QuickBooks Online:**
Not a competitor. QuickBooks is my accounting system. It tracks income and expenses for tax purposes. It does not track project budgets. It does not tell me whether my plumber is over budget on the Elm Street flip. QuickBooks and Fierro are complementary. The site should make this explicit: "Fierro is the project budget layer that sits on top of your accounting system. Track your projects in Fierro, export to QuickBooks for your books."

**vs. CoConstruct / Buildertrend:**
These are GC tools. Built for general contractors to manage their business — client communication, project scheduling, selections, and yes, budgeting. They cost $400-500+/month. They are designed for the builder's perspective, not the investor's. They are bloated with features I do not need (client portals, lead management, scheduling). If Fierro is the lightweight, investor-first alternative at 1/10th the price, that is a massive selling point. But the site never makes this argument.

**vs. Buildium / AppFolio / Stessa:**
Property management tools for landlords. Rent collection, lease management, maintenance requests. Different problem entirely. Not competitors for rehab cost tracking.

**vs. Doing nothing:**
Most investors do nothing. They track costs loosely, eat the overruns, and hope for the best. They do not realize they are losing $5K-15K per flip to poor cost visibility until they sit down and actually analyze their deals at year-end. The "cost of inaction" is Fierro's strongest sales argument, and the Why Fierro page uses it effectively with the pain point scenarios.

**Fix:**
1. Add a "Fierro vs. Alternatives" section to the Why Fierro page. Three cards: "vs. Spreadsheets" (you lose alerting and real-time sync), "vs. GC Tools" (you pay 10x for features you do not need), "vs. Your Accounting Software" (they are complementary, not competitive).
2. Do not name competitors — frame it by category. "If you're using a spreadsheet..." / "If you're using your contractor's tool..." / "If you're doing nothing..."
3. Lean into the price advantage vs. CoConstruct/Buildertrend. "$49/month vs. $400/month. And you get the features that actually matter to you, the investor."

## API & Integrations

API access remains Builder-tier only (custom pricing). ChatGPT integration is available across all tiers with escalating permissions.

**The ChatGPT integration is the most differentiated feature Fierro has.** No one else in this space offers it. The ability to interrogate your project data in natural language is not a gimmick — it is genuinely useful for investors who need answers fast and do not want to navigate dashboards. "What have I spent on the Oak Street flip?" "Am I over budget on any category?" "What is my total spend with Mike's Plumbing across all projects?" If this works as advertised, it is a killer feature.

The permission model (read-only / read & write / configurable per project) is well-thought-out. Security-conscious investors will appreciate controlling exactly what AI can access.

**But API access locked behind Builder tier (custom pricing, sales call) is a mistake.** Investors who run 10+ properties often have their own systems — deal analysis spreadsheets, custom dashboards, investor reporting tools. We want to pull data out programmatically. Locking this behind an enterprise sales process tells me Fierro does not trust me to use their API, or they do not have one yet. Either way, it creates doubt.

**Fix:**
1. Give Plus tier read-only API access. Let power users pull their data. It builds stickiness, not churn.
2. Add a "Zapier / Make" integration or at least a webhook system at the Plus tier. Even basic automation (new expense triggers a notification in Slack, weekly budget summary emailed to my partner) would signal that Fierro plays well with other tools.
3. Show the ChatGPT integration in action. A 60-second video of someone asking real questions about real project data would be more convincing than any feature list. Put it on the homepage.
4. List planned integrations: QuickBooks, Xero, Zapier, Slack. Even "Coming Q3 2026" signals roadmap thinking and reduces "will this tool grow with me?" anxiety.

## Trust & Credibility

**Still zero social proof.** No testimonials. No case studies. No user counts. No logos. No "Trusted by X investors." No press mentions. No product reviews.

The security FAQ still has one line: "Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest. We use Supabase for infrastructure and follow industry best practices."

**For a tool that handles financial data — my project costs, vendor payments, budget details — this is not acceptable.** I am not entering sensitive financial information about my $200K+ projects into a tool with no evidence that anyone else trusts it. "We use Supabase" is not a trust signal. It is an implementation detail.

Compare this to what I see from other SaaS tools I pay for: Stessa shows "Trusted by 100,000+ investors." QuickBooks has decades of reputation. Even new tools I adopt show me a few testimonials and a security page.

I understand Fierro may be early-stage. That is fine. But early-stage is not an excuse for zero trust signals. It is actually a reason to try harder.

**Fix:**
1. **Testimonials (immediate).** Get 3-5 real users to give specific quotes. "I caught a $3,800 overrun on my third flip that I would have missed completely." Specific numbers beat generic praise every time. Put these on the homepage.
2. **Security page (this week).** Create a dedicated /security page. Cover: data encryption, infrastructure (Supabase/AWS), access controls, data retention, backup policy, data export/deletion rights. Investors managing seven-figure portfolios need to see that you take this seriously.
3. **Team page (this month).** Show me who built this. "Built by [name], a [real estate investor / former GC / construction industry veteran]." If the founders have domain expertise, that IS the trust signal.
4. **Usage metrics (when available).** "X projects tracked" or "Y dollars in budgets managed" — even small numbers are better than no numbers. Growth trajectory signals momentum.
5. **If you have beta users or early adopters, do a case study.** One detailed case study — "How [Investor Name] tracked a 6-property portfolio and saved $XX,XXX in Year 1" — is worth more than any feature page.

## Marketing Tone & Audience Targeting

The site speaks to three audiences: homeowners/DIYers, flippers/investors, and real estate teams. The hero section is audience-agnostic ("Know exactly where your money goes"). The audience cards below are well-segmented.

**The investor copy, when it appears, is excellent.** Whoever wrote the "Flippers & Investors" card and the Why Fierro pain points understands the investor's world. "Catch the overruns that kill your margins before closing day" is not something a product manager writes from a persona doc — that is lived experience.

**But the overall site still leads with homeowner energy.** The three-step "How It Works" section ("Set Your Budget, Track Everything, Stay on Budget") feels like it is describing a kitchen remodel, not a 6-property portfolio. The pain points on the Why Fierro page are 3-to-1 homeowner-to-investor. The getting-started language ("Takes about 5 minutes") is approachable but does not signal the seriousness an investor expects.

**The strategic problem remains:** Homeowners remodel once every 10-15 years. They are one-and-done customers with a 3-6 month lifecycle. Investors do 5-15+ projects per year, every year, for decades. They are high-LTV, high-retention customers who will upgrade tiers, refer other investors, and pay annually. The site should lead with investors and let homeowners benefit as a secondary audience. Right now it is the opposite.

**Fix:**
1. Create an investor-specific landing page at `/investors` or `/for-investors`. Lead with investor language: margins, cost basis, ROI, portfolio views, tax-time exports, vendor management. Use investor-specific pain points. Show an investor-oriented product screenshot (multi-project dashboard, not a single kitchen budget).
2. On the homepage, balance the audience sections 50/50 or even lead with investors. The "Flippers & Investors" card should be first, not second.
3. On the Why Fierro page, add at least one more investor-specific pain point: "You finished a flip and your actual all-in cost was $27K more than your budget. Your margin went from 18% to 6%. It was not a bad deal — it was a tracking problem."
4. Adjust the "How It Works" language to signal scale: "Set up your first project in 5 minutes. Add your next four in 10. See your entire portfolio's budget health on one screen."

## Changes Since v2

To be direct: the score has not changed. The homepage copy is solid. The audience segmentation is smart. The ChatGPT integration is still the most novel feature in the category. But the critical gaps I identified in v2 — social proof, export visibility, portfolio-tier pricing, competitive positioning — remain unaddressed on the website.

The trust score actually dropped a point. In v2, zero social proof was forgivable for a new product. In v3, after multiple rounds of marketing review and iteration, continuing to ship a marketing site with zero evidence that anyone uses or trusts the product is a conscious decision to leave conversion rate on the table.

## The Bottom Line

Fierro is the right product for a real problem in a market with no good solution. The budget-vs-actual tracking with alerts, the vendor spend analysis, the ChatGPT integration, and the contractor-friendly pricing model are all genuinely differentiated. The price point is a no-brainer for anyone managing renovation budgets above $50K. The core concept is sound.

**The website is not closing the deal because it is asking investors to make a trust leap it has not earned.**

I can see the product is good. I can see the team understands the problem. But the site does not give me enough confidence to enter my financial data and commit to a workflow change. That is a high bar — and it should be. I am going to put my project costs, vendor relationships, and budget details into this system. I need to know other investors have done the same and found it valuable.

### The 5 things that would change my score from 51% to 75%+:

1. **Add social proof (impact: +8-10 points across Trust and Overall).** Three real testimonials with real numbers on the homepage. One case study with a named investor (or anonymized with permission) showing actual dollar savings. This alone moves the needle more than any feature addition.

2. **Show the export workflow (impact: +5-7 points on Reporting).** Dedicate a feature section to data export. Specify formats (CSV, PDF). Show a tax-time workflow. Mention QuickBooks compatibility if it exists. "Give your CPA a clean file, not a shoebox" is the line that makes investor CPAs nod.

3. **Add a Portfolio tier at $79-99/mo (impact: +3-4 points on Pricing).** 15-20 projects, all Plus features, read-only API, self-serve signup. This is the tier that converts browsers into buyers. The current gap between Plus ($49/8 projects) and Builder (call us/unlimited) is where real revenue dies.

4. **Add competitive positioning (impact: +3-4 points on Competitive Advantage).** Three comparisons on the Why Fierro page: vs. Spreadsheets, vs. GC Tools, vs. Accounting Software. Do not name competitors. Frame by category. Let investors see themselves in one of these buckets and understand why Fierro is the answer.

5. **Create an investor landing page (impact: +2-3 points on First Impression and Value Prop).** `/for-investors` with investor-first language, multi-project screenshots, margin-protection framing, and tax-time export emphasis. This is the page I would share in my investor meetup group. The current homepage is fine but generic.

### The opportunity is still wide open:

No one owns the "renovation budget tracking for real estate investors" category. QuickBooks does not do it. CoConstruct is for GCs at 10x the price. Spreadsheets are painful and do not alert you. Buildium is for landlords. The market is sitting there, waiting for someone to claim it with a credible product and a credible marketing site.

Fierro has the credible product. The marketing site is 60% of the way there. Close the remaining 40% — which is mostly trust signals, export visibility, and investor-specific positioning — and this becomes the obvious choice. Not "one of the options." THE option. The tool that investors recommend to each other at meetups because it saved them real money on real deals.

Right now, my honest reaction is: "This looks promising. I'd try the free tier and see." The reaction you want is: "Why the hell am I NOT using this?" The distance between those two reactions is not product work. It is marketing work. And most of it can be done in a week.
