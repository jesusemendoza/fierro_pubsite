## Cross-Cutting Themes (Flagged by 2+ Reviewers)

### 1. ZERO SOCIAL PROOF — Third Consecutive Review, Still the #1 Problem (All 3 reviewers)

This has been the top finding for three review cycles. No testimonials, no case studies, no user count, no team page, no security certifications. The homeowner scored Trust at 3.5/10. The investor dropped from 3 to **2/10**, stating: "In v3 of a marketing site, having zero social proof is no longer 'understandable early-stage' — it is a choice." The flipper called it "the single most powerful conversion tool for this audience."

The investor reviewer specifically noted the trust score dropped because repeated marketing iterations without adding social proof signals a conscious decision to deprioritize it, not a resource constraint. This erodes credibility rather than just failing to build it.

**Fix (same as v1 and v2, still unimplemented):** Get 3-5 real testimonials with real names, real project types, and real dollar amounts. Add an About/Team page. Add a dedicated /security page with specific encryption standards, hosting details, and data policies. Even if the product is early-stage, own it transparently: "47 homeowners are tracking $2.3M in renovations."

### 2. PRODUCT SCREENSHOTS LANDED — The Biggest Improvement Since v1 (All 3 reviewers)

The addition of product screenshots is the clear win in this review cycle. The homeowner noted every +0.5 score increase traced directly to the visuals. The flipper said the hero dashboard screenshot with a $35K kitchen remodel "makes the product tangible immediately." The investor called the dashboard "professional."

**But the reviews unanimously agree: screenshots aren't enough.** All three personas asked for a demo video or GIF showing the receipt-to-budget flow in action. The homeowner wants "15 seconds: phone camera opens, receipt photographed, amount auto-detected, budget updates." The flipper wants "30 seconds: real person, real job site, real expense." Static screenshots prove the product exists; motion proves it works.

### 3. AI/ChatGPT STILL OVEREMPHASIZED — Wrong Audience Priorities (All 3 reviewers)

- Homeowner: "I don't care about asking ChatGPT about my budget. I care about opening my phone and seeing a number."
- Investor: "Genuinely useful" but acknowledged it's positioned as a top-4 value prop when it should be a differentiator, not a lead feature.
- Flipper: "A solution looking for a problem. My budget shouldn't require a conversation — it should be a dashboard I glance at."

The AI integration occupies the #2 feature slot on the homepage, above Expense Management, Team Management, Analytics, and Vendor Management. Two of three personas ranked it dead last in priorities.

**Fix:** Move AI Integration to position 5 or 6 on the homepage. Replace its slot with Expense Management (the daily workflow) or Team/Contractor features (the adoption driver). The AI pitch is a genuine differentiator for power users — but it should not come before the core product experience in the feature hierarchy.

### 4. Split Audience — Everyone Still Feels Like a Secondary User (All 3 reviewers)

- Homeowner: "The 'Real Estate Teams' card still makes me feel like I stumbled into the wrong product."
- Investor: "The overall site still leads with homeowner energy... The three-step 'How It Works' feels like it's describing a kitchen remodel, not a 6-property portfolio."
- Flipper: "Trying to serve too many audiences dilutes the message."

The homepage tries to serve three audiences and fully satisfies none. The investor argued the site should lead with high-LTV users (investors/flippers) and let homeowners benefit as a secondary audience. The flipper felt like "an afterthought" behind homeowners.

**Fix:** Create dedicated landing pages (`/for-homeowners`, `/for-investors`, `/for-flippers`). Alternatively, rebalance the homepage to lead with the universal pain point ("renovation budgets are broken") and branch into persona-specific sections with equal weight, starting with the highest-stakes users.

### 5. Export/Reporting Still Invisible (2 of 3 reviewers)

The investor scored Financial Reporting & Export at **3/10 for the third consecutive review**. The entire export story on the website is two sentences: "Export reports for your partner, lender, or accountant" and "You can export anytime." No formats. No workflows. No tax-time scenarios. No QuickBooks mention.

The investor called this "the most important feature for investor buyers" and said it's "functionally invisible." The flipper also wanted lender-ready draw request reports.

**Fix:** Add a dedicated "Data Export" row to the pricing comparison table with format specifics per tier. Replace vague export language with: "Export project costs as CSV or PDF. Filter by property, vendor, date range, or category. Generate year-end summaries your CPA will actually thank you for." If QuickBooks integration exists or is planned, mention it — it's a buying trigger.

### 6. Missing Deal Economics for Investors/Flippers (2 of 3 reviewers)

Both the investor and flipper explicitly want: Purchase price + Rehab costs + Holding costs + Selling costs = All-in cost vs. ARV = Projected profit. The investor noted that rehab costs are only 60-70% of total project cost. The flipper called real-time profit margin "the single most important number in my business."

The site never mentions "cost basis," "ARV," "holding costs," "carrying costs," or "closing costs."

**Fix:** If the product supports arbitrary budget categories (which sub-projects suggest it does), frame it for investors: "Track everything — rehab costs, holding costs, acquisition costs, closing costs — all in one project view." Add an Investment Summary concept showing purchase price, live rehab spend, projected holding costs, and estimated profit. Even as marketing language (not a product feature), this tells investors the tool understands their full deal, not just contractor invoices.

## What's Working (Don't Break These)

- **"Know exactly where your money goes"** — Third review, still the best sentence on the site. All three personas cite it positively.
- **Product screenshots (NEW)** — The biggest improvement this cycle. Dashboard, expense list, team panel, analytics, and mobile views make the product tangible. The homeowner's every +0.5 gain traced to these visuals.
- **Why Fierro page pain points** — Still the emotional center of the site. Homeowner: 8/10 Pain Point Resonance. The contractor billing, budget uncertainty, and reporting scenarios resonate deeply across all personas.
- **Contractor expense logging** — All reviewers flagged "your contractor logs expenses, you approve" as a standout feature. The flipper called the contractor invite model "smart" and noted unlimited contractor invites on Plus is "huge."
- **Free tier as zero-risk entry** — Every persona said they'd sign up for Free to try it. The no-credit-card, no-commitment entry point works.
- **Pricing ROI for flippers/investors** — Both scored pricing favorably (flipper 8/10, investor 6/10). $49/mo vs. catching one $5K+ overrun = obvious math. The flipper called it "the easiest money I'd spend all year."
- **Copy quality** — Still human, specific, and grounded in real scenarios. Not generic SaaS. The "Flippers & Investors" audience card was singled out by the investor as "the best copy on the entire site."
- **Detailed image alt text** — The homeowner noticed: "Even if the images don't load, I get a sense of what the product does." Good for accessibility and SEO.

## Persona-Specific Gaps

### Homeowner-Specific

- **Pricing model mismatch (unchanged from v2):** Annual billing irrelevant for 3-6 month renovations. No project pass ($49 one-time for 6 months). No subscription pause. $20/mo for 3-4 months feels like a commitment; $49 once feels like a purchase.
- **100 MB free storage still a trap:** 20-33 receipt photos at 3-5 MB each. Kitchen remodel generates 50+. Creates bait-and-switch feeling. Fix: bump to 500 MB or auto-resize photos.
- **Budget alerts paywalled:** The core product promise ("catch overruns") is gated to the Home tier. Free users never experience the "Fierro saved me" moment. Basic alerts (90% category threshold) should be free.
- **Missing features:** Receipt OCR/auto-extraction, bank/credit card import, shareable read-only budget link for spouse, project completion PDF report, offline receipt capture.
- **"Why not a spreadsheet" still not killed:** The behavioral argument (Fierro changes daily habits, spreadsheets don't) is hinted at but never lands. Needs a prominent workflow comparison section.
- **Why Fierro page has no visuals:** The best-written page on the site has zero screenshots. Pain points are cinematic, solutions are abstract. Add one screenshot per pain point section.

### Investor-Specific

- **Export/reporting invisible (unchanged, 3/10):** The #1 buying trigger — clean CPA data at tax time — is barely mentioned. Needs format specifics (CSV, PDF), tax-time workflows, receipt ZIP exports, and QuickBooks compatibility.
- **8-project ceiling still tight:** 6-9 active rehabs + need for 3-year archived access = Plus tier maxed out during busy season. Missing tier between Plus ($49/8) and Builder (sales call). Needs $79-99/mo Portfolio tier with 15-20 projects.
- **Project archiving never mentioned:** Completed projects need to be accessible for tax, warranty, and post-mortem purposes without counting against active limits. If this feature exists, it's invisible.
- **No portfolio rollup described:** Total spend across all projects, most-over-budget sorting, cost-per-sqft comparisons. The product may do this ("Compare costs across projects" is mentioned) but it's never shown or detailed.
- **Change order tracking absent:** Original budget vs. approved changes vs. revised budget vs. actual. Non-negotiable for construction budgeting. Never mentioned.
- **Committed costs vs. actual spend not addressed:** Signed contracts that represent future spend should factor into remaining budget calculations.
- **No competitive positioning:** The site never compares Fierro to spreadsheets, GC tools, or accounting software by category. Investors do this comparison mentally — make it easy.
- **Trust score DROPPED to 2/10:** Investor explicitly downgraded because v3 of a site with zero social proof is "a choice, not a limitation."
- **API locked behind enterprise tier:** Plus-tier investors with 10+ properties want programmatic data access. Read-only API at Plus would build stickiness.

### Flipper-Specific

- **No real-time profit/margin view:** ARV vs. all-in cost is the single most important number. Fierro shows budget tracking but not profitability tracking. This is the difference between "expense tracker" and "flip management platform."
- **No draw schedule tracking:** Hard money lenders require draw requests. Generating lender-ready reports would make Fierro non-negotiable in the flipper workflow.
- **No phase/milestone tracking:** Flips have phases (demo, rough-in, finish, punch list). Budget vs. actual by phase matters.
- **No holding cost calculator:** Daily carrying costs (loan interest, insurance, utilities, taxes) should auto-accumulate and add to all-in cost.
- **Speed of entry unproven:** No demo/video showing mobile expense entry. "If it takes 2 minutes to categorize, tag, and upload, I'm not doing it."
- **Native app vs. web unclear:** Still no statement about whether this is a native app, PWA, or responsive web. Matters enormously for job-site use.
- **No offline support mentioned:** Job sites don't always have signal. Snap-now-sync-later is expected.
- **ROI argument not made explicit on pricing page:** The flipper had to do the math himself. "The average flip budget overrun is $X. Fierro costs $49/month. You do the math" should be on the page.

## Priority Action Items (Ordered by Conversion Impact)

### Tier 1 — Fix This Week (Blocking Conversions)

1. **Add social proof.** 3-5 real testimonials with names, project types, and dollar amounts. At minimum: one homeowner, one flipper, one investor. Put them on the homepage and the Why Fierro page. This has been the #1 recommendation for THREE review cycles. The investor trust score dropped from 3 to 2 because continued absence of social proof now reads as a decision, not a limitation. If the product is too early for testimonials, run a beta program with 10-15 users and get quotes within 2-4 weeks. Even "We're in early access — 47 homeowners tracking $2.3M in renovations" would help.

2. **Add a receipt capture demo video/GIF (15-30 seconds).** All three personas asked for this. The static screenshots landed well — now show the product in motion. Phone camera opens, receipt captured, amount detected, budget updates. If the flow is genuinely fast, this single piece of content will convert more visitors than any copy change. Put it on the homepage hero area and the Why Fierro page.

3. **Create an About/Team page.** Who built this? Why? "I went $12K over budget on my first renovation and swore I'd fix this problem." One paragraph and a photo. The origin story IS the trust signal for a product this early.

### Tier 2 — Fix This Month (Losing Revenue)

4. **Reorder homepage features.** Current order puts AI Integration at #2. Recommended order: Budget Tracking, Expense Management, Team Management, Analytics, Vendor Management, AI Integration. Match the user journey (set budget → log expenses → collaborate → monitor → analyze → power features), not the feature roadmap.

5. **Detail export capabilities.** Add a "Data Export" row to the pricing comparison table. Specify formats (CSV, PDF). Describe the tax-time workflow. Mention QuickBooks if it exists. "Give your CPA a clean file, not a shoebox" is the line that converts investors.

6. **Add a Portfolio/Investor tier at $79-99/mo.** 15-20 projects, all Plus features, read-only API access, self-serve signup. The gap between Plus ($49/8 projects) and Builder (sales call) is where real revenue dies. Investors with 10-12 active projects won't call sales — they'll leave.

7. **Add screenshots to the Why Fierro page.** The site's best-written page has zero product visuals. One screenshot per pain point solution. Pain builds emotional momentum; visuals convert that momentum into action.

8. **Create a /security page.** Data encryption (AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit), infrastructure (Supabase/AWS), US data residency, data export rights, account deletion, backup policy. Replace "industry best practices" with actual practices.

### Tier 3 — Fix This Quarter (Competitive Moat)

9. **Create persona-specific landing pages** (`/for-homeowners`, `/for-investors`, `/for-flippers`). Each with persona-native language, relevant screenshots, and targeted pain points. The investor wants to see "margins, cost basis, ROI, portfolio views, tax-time exports." The flipper wants "profit margin, all-in cost, draw schedules." The homeowner wants "simple budget, receipt capture, contractor management."

10. **Offer a homeowner Project Pass.** $49 one-time, valid for 6 months. The homeowner was "95% likely" to buy this but it doesn't exist. Monthly subscription is the wrong shape for someone who renovates for 3-6 months every few years.

11. **Add deal economics language for investors/flippers.** "Track your full deal — purchase to sale." ARV, all-in cost, holding costs, projected profit. Even if the product doesn't fully support this yet, the language signals you understand the full investment workflow, not just the rehab portion.

12. **Add a competitor comparison section to Why Fierro.** Three cards: "vs. Spreadsheets" (no alerts, no real-time sync, no receipt management), "vs. GC Tools" (10x the price for features you don't need), "vs. Accounting Software" (complementary, not competitive). Don't name competitors — frame by category.

13. **Make the ROI argument explicit on the pricing page.** "The average flip goes 10-15% over budget. On a $60K rehab, that's $6,000-$9,000 in lost margin. Fierro Plus costs $588/year. Even one caught overrun puts you 10x ahead." Do the math for the visitor.

14. **Address project archiving.** "Completed projects can be archived and accessed anytime without counting against your active limit." Three sentences. Massive anxiety reduction for investors.

15. **Build and showcase vendor intelligence.** Cross-project cost comparisons, vendor spend trends, contractor reliability patterns. The investor told a story of one electrician's prices escalating from $8,200 to $12,400 over three flips — $4,200 in margin erosion that went undetected. This is the moat no spreadsheet can replicate.

## The Verdict

Fierro showed genuine improvement this cycle. The product screenshots are doing real work — the homeowner's composite jumped from 5.9 to 6.3, with every gain traced to the visuals. The site now proves the product exists, which sounds like a low bar, but matters enormously when you're asking people to trust you with their financial data.

**But the two biggest problems remain the same ones identified in v1:**

1. **Zero social proof (three review cycles, still nothing).** The investor trust score actually dropped from 3 to 2 because at this point, the absence is a signal in itself. For a financial product, this is the #1 conversion killer, and it has been for the entire review history.

2. **Export/reporting invisible (three review cycles, still 3/10).** The most important feature for the highest-LTV segment (investors) is functionally absent from the marketing. Two sentences on the entire site. No formats, no workflows, no CPA language.

### Composite Trends

| Persona | v1 | v2 | v3 | v1→v3 |
|---|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Homeowner | 6.5 | 5.9 | 6.3 | -0.2 |
| Investor | 6.1 | 5.1 | 5.1 | -1.0 |
| Flipper | 5.75 | 7.25 | 7.0 | +1.25 |

The flipper dipped slightly from v2 (7.25→7.0) but remains the strongest persona — the v2 marketing changes clearly resonated there. The homeowner recovered from v2's dip thanks to screenshots. The investor is stalled at 5.1 because the critical gaps (export, project limits, social proof, competitive positioning) are all marketing issues that remain unchanged.

### Path from current state to "undeniable":

**Today (composite ~6.1):** Better than v2. Screenshots help. Still leaving money on the table with zero social proof and invisible export capabilities. Good enough to try on the free tier, not good enough to recommend.

**After Tier 1 fixes (~7.5):** Social proof + demo video + team page would move all three personas from "I'd try this" to "I'd pay for this." The trust barrier drops when real humans vouch for the product and you can see it work.

**After Tier 2 fixes (~8.5):** Feature reorder + export details + investor tier + security page would capture the high-LTV segments currently bouncing. The investor is ready to pay — the site just needs to close the deal.

**After Tier 3 fixes (~9+):** Persona pages + deal economics + competitor positioning + ROI calculator = "why the fuck am I NOT using this?" territory. The product becomes the obvious choice, not just the best available option.

### The honest truth:

The distance between Fierro's current marketing and a site that converts at 2-3x the current rate is **mostly social proof and content work, not product work.** Testimonials, a demo video, export documentation, a security page, and an About page. This is weeks of work, not months. The product is ahead of the marketing. Close the gap and conversions will follow.

The screenshots were a meaningful step forward. Now ship the testimonials, show the product in motion, and tell the export story. Everything else is optimization.
