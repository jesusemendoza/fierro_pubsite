**Reviewer profile:** Full-time house flipper. 3-5 active flips at any given time. Crews of 4-8 subs per project. Buying materials myself at Home Depot, Floor & Decor, and local lumber yards. Currently "tracking" costs with a spreadsheet I hate, a Notes app on my phone, and a shoebox of receipts my accountant yells at me about every quarter.

**Pages reviewed:**
- https://getfierro.com/
- https://getfierro.com/pricing
- https://getfierro.com/why-fierro

## Detailed Review

### 1. First Impression / Hook — 7/10

**The headline: "Know exactly where your money goes."**

It's clean. It's clear. But it's also... safe. Every budgeting tool since Mint has said some version of this. When I land on a page, I need to feel like someone reached into my chest and pulled out the exact anxiety I feel at 11pm when I'm wondering if that plumbing rough-in is going to eat my profit.

**What works:**
- The hero image showing a $35K kitchen remodel with live budget bars — that's real. I can picture my own numbers there.
- "Catch every overrun" — that phrase actually hits. Overruns are the thing that turns a $40K profit into a $12K profit.
- The three-step getting started section ("Set Your Budget, Track Everything, Stay on Budget") makes it feel approachable. I don't have time for a complicated onboarding.

**What doesn't work:**
- The headline tries to serve homeowners and flippers at the same time. A homeowner remodeling their kitchen and a flipper running 5 properties at once have fundamentally different anxieties. The homeowner is worried about overspending. I'm worried about margin compression killing my business.
- No numbers in the hero. Show me: "The average flip goes 17% over budget. That's $12,000 on a $70K rehab. Fierro users catch overruns 3 weeks earlier." Give me something concrete to anchor on.

**Fix:** Create a flipper-specific landing page or at minimum, split the hero into two tracks. When I see "remodeling your home" first and "flipping your next investment" second, I feel like an afterthought. Flippers will pay more and churn less — lead with us.

---

### 2. Value Proposition Clarity — 8/10

This is where Fierro does well. I understood what it does within about 10 seconds of hitting the page. That's rare for a SaaS product.

**What works:**
- "See Every Dollar in Real Time" — yes, that's literally all I want.
- "Catch Overruns Before They Hurt" — the "before" is doing a lot of heavy lifting in that sentence. My current system catches overruns at closing, also known as the worst possible time.
- The "Why Fierro" page nails the problem statements. "You didn't realize the flip was over budget until closing day" — I have literally lived this. Multiple times. It cost me real money.
- The spreadsheet comparison in the FAQ ("spreadsheets don't alert you when your electrical budget is 80% spent with half the work remaining") is a knife twist and I respect it.

**What doesn't work:**
- The AI/ChatGPT integration is featured prominently, but honestly, I don't care about asking ChatGPT about my budget. I care about opening my phone and seeing a red number or a green number. The AI stuff feels like it's there to be trendy rather than to solve my actual problem. Maybe I'm wrong and it's useful — but the site doesn't convince me of that.
- "Connect Your AI Tools" as one of the four main value props feels like a wasted slot. Replace it with something like "Know Your All-In Cost Per Property" or "See Your Profit Margin in Real Time." Those are the things that would make me stop scrolling.

**Fix:** Lead with the pain, not the technology. The AI integration is a feature, not a value prop. The value prop is: "Never close on a flip wondering if you made money."

---

### 3. Speed & Ease of Use (perceived) — 7/10

**What works:**
- "Create a budget in minutes" — good.
- "Snap receipts on your phone" — great, this is the workflow I need.
- Three-step getting started flow is simple and non-intimidating.
- The contractor invite model is smart. If my plumber can log his own expenses and I just approve them, that saves me a massive amount of data entry.

**What doesn't work:**
- I don't see a demo or a video of someone actually logging an expense. The site TELLS me it's fast but doesn't SHOW me. I need to see: phone comes out of pocket, app opens, snap receipt, done. 15 seconds. If it actually takes 15 seconds, SHOW ME. That's your killer demo.
- No mention of how receipt capture works. Is it OCR? Does it auto-fill the amount and vendor? Or am I still typing in "Home Depot, $347.22, Electrical" manually? Because if I'm typing all that in, I might as well use my spreadsheet.
- The site doesn't address the "contractor logs expenses" workflow in enough detail. Can my GC log a sub-invoice? Can he attach a photo of a material delivery ticket? These are real daily scenarios.

**Fix:** Put a 30-second screen recording on the homepage showing the mobile expense entry flow from start to finish. If the flow is actually fast, this single video will convert more flippers than all the copywriting combined. Also, explicitly state whether receipt capture uses OCR/auto-fill or not. If it does, that's a headline feature. If it doesn't yet, get on it.

---

### 4. Flipper-Specific Features — 6/10

This is where the gap is most obvious. Fierro is built for "homeowners, flippers, and real estate investors," and that breadth shows.

**What works:**
- The "Flippers & Investors" section on the homepage shows a portfolio dashboard with 4 active properties side by side. That's exactly what I need — a bird's-eye view of all my flips.
- Vendor management with cross-project cost comparison. If I can see that Contractor A charges me 20% more on electrical than Contractor B across my last 5 flips, that's genuinely useful intelligence.
- "Know your all-in cost per property in real time" — yes, this is the single most important number in my business.
- Budget alerts before overruns — if this actually works and is timely, it's worth the subscription alone.

**What's missing:**
- **ARV vs. All-In Cost.** This is THE metric for a flipper. I need to see: Purchase price + Rehab cost + Holding costs + Selling costs = All-in. Then: ARV - All-in = Projected profit. If Fierro showed me this as a real-time number, I would pay double.
- **Draw schedule tracking.** Most flippers use hard money or private money with draw schedules. I need to track not just what I've spent, but what I've drawn and what's left in my loan.
- **Phase/milestone tracking.** Flips have phases: demo, rough-in, finish, punch list. I need to see budget vs. actual by phase, not just by category.
- **Holding cost calculator.** Every day a flip sits unsold, it costs me money (loan interest, insurance, utilities, taxes). This should be a built-in calculator that ticks up daily and adds to my all-in cost.
- **Comp analysis or ARV tracking.** Even a simple field where I enter my expected sale price so the app can show me my projected margin would be transformative.

**Fix:** Add an "Investment Summary" view per project that shows: Purchase Price, Rehab Budget (with real-time actual), Holding Costs (auto-calculating), Expected Sale Price, and Projected Profit/ROI. This single screen would make Fierro the undeniable tool for flippers. Right now it's a great expense tracker that happens to work for flips. It should be a flip management tool that happens to track expenses.

### 5. Mobile/On-Site Usability — 7/10

**What works:**
- "Snap a receipt on your phone at the hardware store" — they get the use case.
- "Track From Anywhere" as a core value prop.
- The homepage shows a mobile screenshot of the app, which signals they take mobile seriously.
- Receipt photo capture is mentioned multiple times.

**What doesn't work:**
- No mention of offline capability. I work in houses with no cell signal all the time. Gutted houses with no wifi. Can I snap a receipt offline and have it sync later? This is a dealbreaker scenario that's never addressed.
- No mention of a native app vs. mobile web. Is this a PWA? A native iOS/Android app? If I have to open Safari and navigate to a website to log an expense, I'm not doing it. It needs to be one tap from my home screen.
- The mobile screenshot on the homepage is nice but small. I want to see the actual expense entry flow on mobile. Show me the camera integration. Show me the form. Show me how few taps it takes.

**Fix:** Dedicate a section of the homepage to the mobile experience specifically. "Built for the job site" with a large phone mockup showing the expense entry flow. Address offline sync explicitly — flippers will ask about this within 5 minutes of considering the product. If there's a native app, say so prominently. If not, explain why mobile web is just as good (or build the native app).

### 6. Pricing ROI — 8/10

**The math is simple and it's in Fierro's favor.**

Plus tier at $49/month = $588/year. If I'm running 3-5 flips a year with average rehab budgets of $50K-$80K each, a single overrun I catch early could save me $5,000-$15,000. The tool pays for itself the first time it alerts me that my framing contractor is 40% through the budget with 20% of the work done.

**What works:**
- Free tier exists. I can try it on one project with zero risk. Smart.
- Plus tier at $49/month gives me 8 projects. That's enough for most flippers.
- Unlimited contractor invites on Plus. This is huge. Charging per contractor seat would be a dealbreaker since I have different subs on every project.
- No credit card required to start. Low friction.

**What doesn't work:**
- The pricing page doesn't make the ROI argument explicitly. Don't make me do the math — do it for me. "The average flip budget overrun is $X. Fierro costs $49/month. You do the math." Actually, do the math FOR me.
- Home tier only allows 2 projects. A flipper doing even casual volume will outgrow this immediately. The jump from $20 (2 projects) to $49 (8 projects) is a big gap. Consider a middle tier or just bump Home to 3-4 projects.
- Annual billing saves "up to $118/year" — that's fine, but it's not a compelling enough number to highlight. The real compelling number is the cost of NOT using Fierro.

**Fix:** Add an ROI calculator or at minimum a "Cost of doing nothing" section on the pricing page. "If you go 10% over budget on a $60K rehab, that's $6,000 gone. Fierro Plus costs $588/year. Even if we save you from ONE overrun, you're 10x ahead." That's the argument. Make it impossible to say no.

### 7. "Game Changer" Factor — 6/10

**Honest assessment:** Fierro is a good product solving a real problem. But the site doesn't yet make me feel like an idiot for not already using it. That's the bar for "game changer."

**What would make it a game changer:**
- **Real-time profit margin per flip.** Not just "are you on budget" but "are you going to make money on this deal." That's the question I go to sleep thinking about.
- **Historical pattern analysis.** "Your electrical costs have averaged 15% over budget across your last 4 flips. You should budget $X instead of $Y on your next one." THAT would make me a better buyer.
- **Lender reporting.** If I could generate a draw request report that my hard money lender accepts, Fierro becomes a non-negotiable part of my workflow. Right now I'm building these in Excel every time I need a draw. It takes hours.
- **Deal analysis integration.** If I could enter a potential purchase, plug in my rehab estimate based on my historical data, and see a projected ROI before I even make an offer — that's when Fierro goes from "nice to have" to "competitive advantage."

**What currently holds it back:**
- Trying to serve too many audiences dilutes the message. The homeowner doing a bathroom remodel and the flipper running a portfolio have very different needs and very different willingness to pay.
- The AI integration feels forced. It takes up prime real estate on every page but doesn't solve a problem I can feel. "Ask ChatGPT about your budget" is a solution looking for a problem. My budget shouldn't require a conversation — it should be a dashboard I glance at.
- No social proof from actual flippers. No testimonials. No case studies. No "I saved $14K on my last flip because Fierro caught my GC's overrun in week 3." That kind of story would be worth more than every feature description on the site.

**Fix:** Get 3-5 real flippers using the product. Document their stories. Put those stories front and center. One real testimonial from a flipper saying "I almost lost my shirt on a flip and Fierro saved me" is worth 10,000 words of feature copy. Also, strongly consider building a flipper-specific tier or landing page that speaks directly to investment returns, not just budget tracking.

---

### 8. Overall "Would I Pay For This?" — 7/10

**Yes, I would pay for this.** But I'd pay for it reluctantly, the way I pay for QuickBooks — because it's better than the alternative (chaos), not because it makes me excited.

**To get me to a 9 or 10, Fierro needs to answer one question:**

"Will this tool help me make more money on my flips?"

Right now, the answer is: "It'll help you lose less money by catching overruns." That's good. That's worth $49/month. But it's defensive, not offensive.

The offensive version is: "Fierro will tell you your real profit in real time, show you where you're leaking money across all your flips, help you budget more accurately on future deals, and give you the data to negotiate harder with contractors." That's a tool I'd evangelize to every flipper I know.

**Compared to my current system:**
- Spreadsheets: Fierro is better at real-time tracking, alerts, and multi-project management. Spreadsheets are better at being infinitely customizable and free.
- Notes app: Fierro destroys Notes. No contest.
- Memory: Fierro destroys memory. My memory has cost me tens of thousands of dollars.
- Doing nothing: Doing nothing is what most flippers do, and it's why most flippers underestimate their costs. Fierro is clearly better than nothing.

**The real competition** isn't spreadsheets — it's inertia. The site needs to make the cost of NOT switching feel unbearable. Right now it makes the cost of switching feel low (which is good) but doesn't make the cost of staying the same feel high enough (which is the gap).

---

## The Three Things I'd Change Tomorrow

**1. Add a real-time profit/margin view per project.** Purchase price + rehab spend (live) + estimated remaining costs + holding costs = all-in. Subtract from ARV = projected profit. This single feature moves Fierro from "expense tracker" to "flip management platform" and justifies a higher price point.

**2. Put a 30-second mobile expense entry video on the homepage.** If the flow is actually fast, prove it. Don't tell me — show me. A real person, at a real job site, logging a real expense. This will convert more fence-sitters than any amount of copy.

**3. Add flipper testimonials and case studies.** "Before Fierro, I tracked costs in a spreadsheet and routinely went 15% over budget. After Fierro, my last 3 flips came in within 3% of budget. That's an extra $27,000 in my pocket." Even if you have to give the product away to 10 flippers to get these stories, do it. Social proof from peers is the single most powerful conversion tool for this audience.

---

## Bottom Line

Fierro is a solid 7/10 product trying to serve a broad market. For flippers specifically, it solves a real and painful problem — but it doesn't yet go deep enough into the flipper workflow to feel indispensable. The pricing is a no-brainer if it works as described. The site is clean and clear but plays it safe when it should be punching me in the gut with the cost of my current chaos.

The bones are there. The product clearly understands that cost tracking during renovations is broken. But to own the flipper market, Fierro needs to stop being "a budget tracker that works for flips" and start being "the tool that tells you whether your flip is going to make money." That's a subtle but critical difference — and it's the difference between a tool I'd try and a tool I'd never cancel.

**Would I sign up today?** Yes, on the free tier, for my next flip. Would I go to Plus immediately? Probably not — I'd need to see the mobile experience firsthand and confirm the expense entry is actually fast. But if it delivers on the promise, $49/month is the easiest money I'd spend all year. One caught overrun and I'm ahead for the next decade of subscription costs.

**The real question isn't whether Fierro is worth $49/month. It's whether Fierro can make me $5,000/year by helping me stay on budget.** The site needs to answer that question louder.
