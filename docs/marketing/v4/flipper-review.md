# Fierro Website Review: House Flipper Perspective

**Reviewer profile:** Full-time house flipper, 3-5 active flips at any time, crews of 4-8 subs per project, buying materials myself at HD/Lowe's/local lumber yards. Currently tracking everything in a Google Sheet I copy-paste between projects, plus photos in my camera roll I never look at again.

**Date reviewed:** 2026-03-10
**Pages reviewed:** Homepage, Pricing, Why Fierro

---

## The Quick Take

Fierro gets the problem. The "Why Fierro" page reads like someone has been watching me lose money. The pain points are dead-on: receipts in my truck, contractor invoices in texts, budget numbers I reconstruct from memory at 11pm. Where it falls short is showing me, concretely, how FAST I can log an expense and how clearly I can see my all-in cost vs ARV. The site talks about the disease really well but doesn't show me the cure working in real time.

---

## Detailed Evaluation

### 1. First Impression / Hook: 7/10

**Headline:** "Know exactly where your money goes."

This is clean and honest. It doesn't try to be clever. When I'm staring at a $180K rehab wondering if I'm at $140K or $165K spent, "know exactly where your money goes" is literally the thing I need. The subheadline explicitly calls out flipping, which told me immediately I'm not on the wrong site.

**What works:** The homepage shows a flipper portfolio view with 4 properties, rehab budgets, and contractor counts. That screenshot alone made me stop scrolling. That's my life. Someone here gets it.

**What doesn't:** There's zero social proof. No testimonials, no "saved $12K on my last flip," no numbers. For a product targeting people who make decisions based on numbers, the absence of any proof that real flippers use this is a problem. I don't care about your feature list if nobody like me is actually using it.

**Fix:** Put one real flipper testimonial above the fold. Something like: "I caught a $7,200 framing overrun on my third flip that I would have missed in my spreadsheet." That's worth more than any feature bullet.

### 2. Value Proposition Clarity: 8/10

The "Why Fierro" page is the strongest page on the site. The framing is perfect: "Renovations and flips go over budget. Not because people are careless -- because tracking costs across receipts, texts, and spreadsheets is impossible." That's not marketing copy, that's a fact I live every day.

The three pain points they call out are exactly right:
- Unclear contractor billing (I've been double-billed by subs and didn't catch it until the project was done)
- No real-time budget visibility (the "am I at $18K or $28K" example is painfully relatable)
- Hidden overruns until closing day (this is the one that kills your margin)

**What's missing:** The site never explicitly talks about the flip math. My world is: Purchase Price + Rehab Cost + Holding Costs + Selling Costs = All-In Cost. Then: ARV - All-In Cost = Profit. If Fierro showed me that equation on the homepage with a live dashboard mockup, I'd be reaching for my credit card. Instead, it talks in generalities about "budget tracking." Show me the FLIP P&L.

**Fix:** Add a section or screenshot showing: "Purchase: $120K | Rehab Budget: $65K | Rehab Spent: $47,200 | Projected Profit: $38,800" -- that's the view I need. If I can see that number updating in real time as expenses come in, this product sells itself.

### 3. Speed & Ease of Use (perceived): 6/10

This is where I get nervous. The site talks about "snap receipts on your phone" and "budget tracking in minutes," but I have no idea how many taps it takes to log an expense. When I'm standing in the Home Depot parking lot with $340 of PEX fittings in my truck, I need:

1. Open app
2. Pick the project (123 Oak St)
3. Pick the category (Plumbing)
4. Snap the receipt
5. Done

That's 30 seconds max. If it's anything more than that, I'm not doing it. I'll throw the receipt in my center console and forget about it like I always do.

The site never shows me this flow. There's no "log an expense in 4 taps" demo. No video of someone at a job site using it. I have to IMAGINE the experience, and imagining is not what converts skeptical flippers.

**Fix:** Put a 15-second GIF or video on the homepage showing the mobile expense entry flow. Start to finish. Receipt snap to categorized expense. If it's truly fast, SHOW ME. If you're hiding it, I assume it's slow.

### 4. Flipper-Specific Features: 7/10

**What I see that matters to me:**
- Multi-property tracking (essential -- I have 3-5 going at once)
- Per-category budget tracking with alerts ("electrical is 80% spent with half the work remaining" -- that's a real scenario that has burned me)
- Contractor can log expenses and I approve (this is huge -- my GC texts me receipts and I lose them)
- Vendor management with cross-project cost comparison (knowing what I paid for drywall hanging per sq ft across projects is valuable data)

**What's missing:**
- **No mention of change orders.** Every flip has scope changes. The bathroom that was going to be a refinish becomes a gut job. I need to track original budget vs revised budget vs actual.
- **No draw schedule tracking.** If I'm using hard money, my lender releases funds on a draw schedule tied to milestones. Fierro doesn't mention this at all.
- **No ARV or deal analysis.** The flip lifecycle starts BEFORE you buy. If Fierro helped me evaluate deals (rehab estimate templates based on my historical data), that would be transformative.
- **No mention of holding costs.** My hard money loan costs me $1,800/month. Every day a flip runs over schedule costs me real money. Time tracking or projected timeline vs budget burn rate would be killer.

**Fix:** At minimum, add change order tracking and mention holding cost awareness. For a future version, using my historical expense data to help me estimate rehab budgets on new acquisitions would make this indispensable.

### 5. Mobile/On-Site Usability: 5/10

The site mentions "Track From Anywhere" and "mobile receipt capture," but I see no evidence that this is a mobile-first product. The screenshots on the homepage look like desktop dashboards. One screenshot shows a "mobile app showing budget breakdown," but it's described as a budget view, not the expense entry workflow.

I spend 80% of my working day at job sites. I'm covered in drywall dust. I'm wearing work gloves. If the primary interface is a desktop dashboard that has a mobile version, that's backwards. The PRIMARY interface should be the mobile receipt capture, and the desktop should be the reporting/analytics view.

**What worries me:** The site mentions "laptop access, synced data" as a feature. That phrasing suggests the laptop is the primary device, and mobile is the secondary. That's the wrong hierarchy for a flipper.

**Fix:** Dedicate an entire section of the homepage to the mobile experience. Show the phone. Show the camera integration. Show me logging an expense at a lumber yard. Make me feel like this was designed for someone wearing a tool belt, not someone at a desk.

### 6. Pricing ROI: 7/10

**Free:** 1 project -- fine for trying it out on one flip.
**Home:** $20/mo for 2 projects -- this is where I'd start. That's $240/year.
**Plus:** $49/mo for 8 projects -- this is my tier. That's $588/year with annual billing.

**The math:** If this product catches ONE budget overrun on ONE flip, it pays for itself for 5+ years. A single missed double-invoice from a sub is typically $500-$2,000. A single category overrun I catch early (because I got an alert at 80%) could save me $3,000-$10,000 by letting me adjust scope before it's too late.

$49/month to protect margins on flips where I'm targeting $30-50K profit? That's a rounding error. The pricing is not the problem.

**What's weird:** The jump from 2 projects (Home) to 8 projects (Plus) is big. I'd want a tier at maybe 4-5 projects for $35/mo. Also, "free contractors" is listed as a feature (5 on Home, unlimited on Plus) -- I don't understand what a "free contractor" means vs a "team member." The distinction is unclear.

**What's missing:** No mention of per-project pricing. Some flippers do 1-2 flips a year. Paying monthly when you have a 3-month gap between projects feels wasteful. A per-project option ($30/project?) might convert more casual flippers.

**Fix:** Clarify the contractor vs team member distinction. Consider a per-project pricing option for lower-volume flippers. And put the ROI math front and center: "One caught overrun pays for 5 years of Fierro."

### 7. "Game Changer" Factor: 6/10

Right now, Fierro looks like a better-organized spreadsheet with receipt capture and contractor access bolted on. That's genuinely useful, but it's not a game changer. Here's what would make it a game changer:

1. **Historical cost database.** After 10 flips, I should be able to say "my average kitchen gut reno costs $22,400" and use that to estimate my next deal. That turns Fierro from a tracking tool into a deal analysis tool.

2. **Real-time profit projection.** Show me: "Based on current spend rate, your projected profit on 123 Oak St is $32,100, down from $41,000 at project start." That's the number that keeps me up at night. Show it to me live.

3. **Contractor performance data.** Not just what a contractor charges, but: on-time completion rate, change order frequency, quality of work (based on whether I had to bring them back). That's intelligence, not just bookkeeping.

4. **Lender-ready reports.** My hard money lender wants draw request documentation. If Fierro could generate that automatically from my categorized expenses, that saves me 2 hours per draw request.

The AI/ChatGPT integration is interesting -- being able to ask "what did I spend at Home Depot last month across all projects?" is useful. But it feels like a nice-to-have, not a core differentiator. The core differentiator should be: "Fierro makes you a more profitable flipper." That message isn't landing yet.

**Fix:** The site needs a section specifically for flippers (not bundled with homeowners doing a kitchen remodel) that speaks to: deal analysis, profit tracking, contractor intelligence, and lender reporting. These are the things that would make a flipper say "I can't run my business without this."

### 8. Overall "Would I Pay For This?": 7/10

**Yes, I would try it.** The free tier for one project is a no-brainer. I'd put my next flip on it and see if the receipt capture and budget alerts actually work in the field. If the mobile experience is genuinely fast (under 30 seconds to log an expense), I'd upgrade to Plus within a month.

**What would stop me:** If the mobile expense entry is clunky or slow. If categorizing expenses requires more than 2 taps. If the app is buggy or slow on cellular data at a job site. Any friction at all and I'm back to my spreadsheet, because at least my spreadsheet doesn't crash.

**What would make me a lifer:** If after 3 flips, I could pull up my average cost per square foot for a full gut renovation, broken down by trade. That data doesn't exist anywhere in my current workflow. That's the kind of intelligence that makes me a better buyer and a more profitable flipper.

## Top 3 Things to Fix Right Now

1. **Show me the mobile expense entry flow.** A 15-second video of someone at a job site logging a receipt. This is the #1 thing that would convert me. If it's fast, prove it. If it's not fast, make it fast, then prove it.

2. **Add social proof from real flippers.** One testimonial with a dollar amount saved. "Caught a $4,800 plumbing overrun on my flip at 412 Elm" is worth more than every feature bullet on the page combined.

3. **Show the flip P&L view.** Purchase price + rehab spent + holding costs = all-in cost. ARV - all-in cost = projected profit. If that number is live-updating as I add expenses, this product sells itself to every flipper who sees it.

## The Bottom Line

Fierro understands the problem better than any tool I've seen. The "Why Fierro" page could have been written by reading my mind. But the site is selling features when it should be selling outcomes. I don't care about "budget alerts" -- I care about "never lose money on a flip because you didn't see the overrun coming." Same thing, different framing, completely different emotional impact.

The product has the bones to be the thing every flipper uses. Right now, it's positioned as a nicer way to track expenses. It should be positioned as: "the reason your last flip made $45K instead of $28K." That's a product worth 10x the asking price.
