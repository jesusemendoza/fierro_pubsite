## Cross-Cutting Themes (Flagged by 2+ Reviewers)

### 1. ZERO SOCIAL PROOF — Fourth Consecutive Review, Still the #1 Problem (All 3 reviewers)

This has now been the top finding for **four review cycles**. No testimonials, no case studies, no user count, no team page. The homeowner scored Trust at 4/10 and said testimonials are "the single biggest gap on the entire site." The investor scored Trust at 4/10 and said "the site has no evidence that anyone has ever used this product." The flipper said "I don't care about your feature list if nobody like me is actually using it."

Every persona independently suggested the exact same fix: one real testimonial with a specific dollar amount saved on a specific project. All three noted that a single credible quote would outweigh every feature description on the site.

**Fix (same as v1, v2, and v3, still unimplemented):** Get 3-5 real testimonials with names, project types, and dollar amounts. Add an About/Team page. Even beta user quotes work. If the product is too early, be transparent: "We're new. 47 homeowners tracking $2.3M in renovations." Honesty builds more trust than silence.

### 2. NO DEMO VIDEO / MOTION CONTENT — All Reviewers Want to See It Work (All 3 reviewers)

All three personas asked for a video or GIF showing the product in motion. The homeowner wants a "10-second video of someone snapping a receipt and watching the fields populate." The flipper wants a "15-second GIF of someone at a job site logging a receipt." The investor wants a "sample CSV export screenshot."

Static screenshots prove the product exists; motion proves it works. The receipt-to-categorized-expense flow is the single most demonstrable value moment, and it's invisible on the site.

**Fix:** Create a 15-30 second GIF/video showing: open app → pick project → snap receipt → expense auto-categorized → budget updates. Put it on the homepage hero area. If the flow is genuinely fast, this single asset will convert more visitors than any copy change.

### 3. SPLIT AUDIENCE — Everyone Still Feels Like a Secondary User (All 3 reviewers)

- Homeowner: "The site tries to serve homeowners AND flippers AND 'real estate teams' — and while that's fine, it slightly dilutes the message."
- Investor: "The homeowner messaging dominates. The hero example is a $35K kitchen remodel. The first audience card is 'Homeowners & DIYers.'"
- Flipper: "The site is selling features when it should be selling outcomes."

The investor specifically noted: "An investor wants: 'know your exact cost basis, maximize your ROI, give your CPA clean data, scale without losing control.' The messaging that closes a homeowner is too soft for an investor."

**Fix:** Create persona-specific landing pages (`/for-homeowners`, `/for-investors`, `/for-flippers`). The product is the same but the story is different. Alternatively, lead the homepage with the universal pain point ("renovation budgets are broken") and branch into persona-specific sections with equal weight.

### 4. MISSING DEAL ECONOMICS / FLIP P&L (2 of 3 reviewers)

Both the investor and flipper explicitly want: Purchase Price + Rehab Cost + Holding Costs + Selling Costs = All-In Cost vs. ARV = Projected Profit.

The flipper put it bluntly: "If Fierro showed me that equation on the homepage with a live dashboard mockup, I'd be reaching for my credit card." The investor called it "a missed layup" that Fierro never connects rehab costs to cost basis.

**Fix:** Add a section or screenshot showing flip P&L: "Purchase: $120K | Rehab Budget: $65K | Rehab Spent: $47,200 | Projected Profit: $38,800." Even if the product doesn't track purchase price today, the framing tells investors and flippers that Fierro understands the full deal, not just the receipts.

### 5. COMPETITOR COMPARISON STILL ABSENT (2 of 3 reviewers)

Both homeowner and investor called for explicit comparison content. The homeowner identified 5 alternatives (Google Sheets, QuickBooks, Mint, Buildertrend, physical notebook) and noted "the site could do a better job of explicitly calling out these alternatives." The investor noted Fierro "never says 'here's why Fierro instead of QuickBooks.' It assumes I'll figure out the advantage myself."

**Fix:** Add a comparison section to the Why Fierro page. Three cards: "vs. Spreadsheets" (no alerts, no real-time sync, no receipt management), "vs. QuickBooks" (purpose-built for renovation vs. generic accounting), "vs. Doing Nothing" (the real cost of lost receipts and missed overruns). Be specific and honest.

### 6. EXPORT / REPORTING STILL UNDERSOLD (2 of 3 reviewers)

The investor scored Financial Reporting & Export at **5/10** (up from 3/10 in v3, but still weak). The homeowner wants to know "what happens to my data when I downgrade." Neither sees sample exports, format details, or tax-time workflows.

The investor: "The site says 'give your accountant clean data at tax time' but never shows a sample export or explains what fields are included." The homeowner: "My accountant will love me. Right now I hand her a folder of receipts and a prayer."

**Fix:** Show a sample CSV export — just a screenshot of 10 rows in a spreadsheet showing the columns. Create a "Tax Time" section showing how an investor's CPA would use Fierro data. Specify formats (CSV, PDF). Describe what fields are included.

## What's Working (Don't Break These)

- **"Know exactly where your money goes"** — Fourth review, still the best sentence on the site. All three personas cite it positively. The homeowner: "I knew what Fierro does within five seconds."
- **Why Fierro page pain points** — Consistently the emotional center of the site. Homeowner gave Pain Point Resonance a **9/10** (up from 8). The flipper: "The 'Why Fierro' page could have been written by reading my mind." The investor: "Every investor has lived that exact moment."
- **Product screenshots** — Still working. The flipper specifically called out the portfolio view with 4 properties: "That screenshot alone made me stop scrolling. That's my life." The investor called the dashboard "exactly what I need to see every morning with my coffee."
- **Contractor expense logging with approval** — All reviewers flagged this as a standout. The homeowner: "This is actual accountability, not just tracking." The flipper: "My GC texts me receipts and I lose them."
- **Free tier as zero-risk entry** — Every persona said they'd sign up for Free. The flipper: "The free tier for one project is a no-brainer."
- **Pricing structure overall** — Homeowner scored Pricing at 8/10 (up from 5). The flipper: "The pricing is not the problem." The investor: "At $49/month, Plus is cheaper than QuickBooks Online Plus ($99/month) and infinitely more focused."
- **Copy quality** — Still human, specific, and grounded. The homeowner: "The copy is genuinely good. It's clear, specific, and avoids most SaaS cliches." The flipper: "The pain points are dead-on."
- **Budget alerts concept** — The homeowner: "If it actually does this, that alone might be worth $20/month." The flipper: "electrical is 80% spent with half the work remaining — that's a real scenario that has burned me."

## Persona-Specific Gaps

### Homeowner-Specific

- **Data retention after project unclear:** "What happens after my project is done? Do I keep paying $20/month to access my records? Can I downgrade to Free and still see my data?" Add a clear FAQ about post-project data access.
- **Repetitive CTA:** "Stop wondering where your money went" appears on every page — by the third time it's wallpaper. Vary it.
- **AI section feels bolted on:** "The rest of the copy is about real problems; the AI section is about 'connecting to your tools.' It reads like a different product."
- **Spreadsheet comparison could be stronger:** The FAQ answer is decent but "should be a gut punch, not a gentle nudge." Show the pain of matching receipt photos to spreadsheet rows at tax time.
- **Missing features:** Receipt OCR confirmation, bank/credit card import, shareable read-only budget link for spouse, change order tracking, offline receipt capture.
- **No live demo / interactive preview:** "Let me see a sample project dashboard without signing up."
- **Post-project value unclear:** After the remodel is done, what do I have? Insurance records? Tax documentation? Resale value proof?

### Investor-Specific

- **8-project ceiling still tight:** "What happens when I have project #9? I hit a wall and have to jump to Builder (contact sales)." Needs a Portfolio tier at $79-99/mo for 15-20 projects.
- **Cost basis never mentioned:** "For an investor, the cost basis of a property is the number that determines your taxable gain. Fierro tracks rehab costs, but the site never connects those dots."
- **Committed costs vs. actual costs not addressed:** "If I have a $15K contract with my GC and I've paid $5K, my committed cost is $15K, not $5K." Show a three-segment budget bar: spent, committed, remaining.
- **No sample export shown:** "Show a sample CSV export. Just a screenshot of 10 rows."
- **API locked behind enterprise:** "Investors who are scaling are exactly the people who want API access." Move read-only API to Plus tier.
- **No QuickBooks/Xero integration mentioned:** "If my data can't flow from Fierro to my accounting software without manual CSV export, that is a friction point."
- **Vendor analytics not shown:** "Can I see that Vendor A charges me $4,500 for rough plumbing vs. Vendor B at $3,800?" Show it.
- **No competitive comparison page:** "Have a dedicated comparison page. 'Fierro vs. QuickBooks for Real Estate Investors.'"

### Flipper-Specific

- **Mobile experience looks desktop-first:** "The screenshots look like desktop dashboards. I spend 80% of my working day at job sites." Dedicate a homepage section to the mobile experience showing someone at a job site.
- **No change order tracking mentioned:** "Every flip has scope changes. I need to track original budget vs revised budget vs actual."
- **No draw schedule tracking:** "My lender releases funds on a draw schedule tied to milestones. Fierro doesn't mention this at all."
- **No holding cost awareness:** "My hard money loan costs me $1,800/month. Every day a flip runs over schedule costs real money."
- **No historical cost database concept:** "After 10 flips, I should be able to say 'my average kitchen gut reno costs $22,400' and use that to estimate my next deal."
- **Contractor vs team member distinction unclear:** "I don't understand what a 'free contractor' means vs a 'team member.'"
- **Positioned as expense tracker, not profit tool:** "The site is selling features when it should be selling outcomes. I don't care about 'budget alerts' — I care about 'never lose money on a flip.'"

## Priority Action Items (Ordered by Conversion Impact)

### Tier 1 — Fix This Week (Blocking Conversions)

1. **Add social proof.** 3-5 real testimonials with names, project types, and dollar amounts. This has been the #1 recommendation for **four review cycles**. All three personas independently identified it as the single biggest gap. One real quote from one real user with a specific dollar amount is worth more than every feature bullet on the site. If the product is too early, run a beta with 10-15 users and get quotes within 2-4 weeks. Even "47 homeowners tracking $2.3M in renovations" helps.

2. **Add a receipt capture demo video/GIF (15-30 seconds).** All three personas asked for this — again, for the second consecutive review. Show the mobile flow: phone opens, receipt captured, expense categorized, budget updates. The flipper: "If you're hiding it, I assume it's slow." This is the #1 proof point for the core product experience.

3. **Create an About/Team page.** Who built this? Why? The homeowner: "I'm about to put my financial data into your system — I want to know you'll exist in 6 months." One paragraph, a photo, and an origin story. The founder's renovation horror story IS the trust signal.

### Tier 2 — Fix This Month (Losing Revenue)

4. **Add a competitor comparison section.** All three personas mentally compared Fierro to alternatives (spreadsheets, QuickBooks, CoConstruct, doing nothing). Make the comparison explicit on the Why Fierro page. Three cards: vs. Spreadsheets, vs. QuickBooks, vs. Doing Nothing. Be specific and honest.

5. **Detail export capabilities.** Show a sample CSV export screenshot. Specify what fields are included. Describe the tax-time workflow. Mention QuickBooks if integration exists. "Give your CPA a clean file, not a shoebox" converts investors.

6. **Clarify post-project data access.** The homeowner's biggest unanswered question: "Do I keep paying to access my records after the project ends?" Add an FAQ entry. If you can downgrade to Free and retain read-only access, say so prominently.

7. **Add a Portfolio/Investor tier at $79-99/mo.** The gap from Plus ($49/8 projects) to Builder (contact sales) loses scaling investors. 15-20 projects, Plus features, read-only API, self-serve signup.

8. **Reframe the product as outcomes, not features.** The flipper's core criticism: "I don't care about 'budget alerts' — I care about 'never lose money on a flip because you didn't see the overrun coming.'" Same features, different framing, completely different emotional impact.

### Tier 3 — Fix This Quarter (Competitive Moat)

9. **Create persona-specific landing pages** (`/for-homeowners`, `/for-investors`, `/for-flippers`). The investor wants cost basis, portfolio rollups, and tax-ready exports. The flipper wants flip P&L, draw schedules, and real-time profit. The homeowner wants simple budgets and receipt capture. The product serves all three; the marketing should too.

10. **Show the flip P&L view.** Purchase + Rehab + Holding = All-In vs. ARV = Profit. Live-updating as expenses come in. The flipper: "If Fierro showed me that equation on the homepage with a live dashboard mockup, I'd be reaching for my credit card."

11. **Add deal economics language for investors.** "Know your all-in cost basis per property — purchase, rehab, holding costs, and closing costs — the number your CPA actually needs." Even as marketing language, this tells investors you understand the full deal.

12. **Make the ROI argument explicit on the pricing page.** The flipper did his own math: "If this catches ONE budget overrun on ONE flip, it pays for itself for 5+ years." Put that math on the pricing page for the visitor.

13. **Dedicate a homepage section to mobile.** Show the phone at a job site. Show the camera integration. Make flippers feel this was designed for someone wearing a tool belt, not sitting at a desk.

14. **Add a live demo / interactive preview.** The homeowner: "Let me see a sample project dashboard without signing up. Show me a $40K kitchen remodel with 8 budget categories. Let me click around. If the product is good, let the product sell itself."

15. **Address change orders and committed costs.** Non-negotiable for construction professionals. Show a budget bar with three segments: spent (dark), committed (medium), remaining (light). This signals Fierro understands how real projects work.

## The Verdict

Fierro showed significant improvement this cycle. The homeowner composite jumped from 6.3 to **7.3** (+1.0), and the investor jumped from 5.1 to **6.4** (+1.3). These are the largest single-cycle gains in the review history. The pricing perception improved dramatically across the board — the homeowner went from 5/10 to 8/10 on pricing, suggesting recent pricing page changes or clearer tier communication are landing.

The flipper dipped from 7.0 to 6.6 (-0.4). This appears to be a different reviewer calibrating more critically on mobile experience (5/10 vs. previous 7/10) — the flipper's core praise ("Why Fierro page is the strongest page on the site") is consistent with previous reviews.

**But the elephant in the room remains: zero social proof, four review cycles running.**

All three reviewers independently said the same thing: one real testimonial with a specific dollar amount is worth more than every feature description on the site. This isn't a nice-to-have anymore. The homeowner called it "the single biggest gap." The investor called it "the single highest-priority fix." The flipper said "I don't care about your feature list if nobody like me is actually using it." When three independent personas with different needs all name the same #1 problem, that's as clear a signal as marketing data can provide.

### Path from current state to "undeniable":

**Today (composite ~6.8):** The best scores yet. Pricing is landing. Copy is strong. Screenshots work. Pain points resonate deeply (9/10 from homeowner). But trust remains the bottleneck — every persona is willing to try the free tier but hesitant to commit money to a company they know nothing about.

**After Tier 1 fixes (~8.0):** Social proof + demo video + team page would collapse the trust gap. The product promise is strong enough that proof of delivery is the only missing conversion element. The homeowner is already at "I'd sign up for free and upgrade if it works." Testimonials bridge "if it works" to "it works."

**After Tier 2 fixes (~8.5-9.0):** Competitor comparisons + export details + investor tier + outcome framing would capture the high-LTV segments. The investor said: "Fix those three things and you have a product that every investor in every REIA meeting in the country would be talking about."

**After Tier 3 fixes (~9.5+):** Persona pages + flip P&L + deal economics + mobile showcase + interactive demo = "why the fuck am I NOT using this?" territory. The flipper said it best: "The product has the bones to be the thing every flipper uses. It should be positioned as: 'the reason your last flip made $45K instead of $28K.'"

### The honest truth:

The site has improved meaningfully. Pricing perception is up across the board. The copy is genuinely good — the Why Fierro page earned a 9/10 for pain point resonance, which is remarkable. The product screenshots continue to do real work.

But the gap between "I'd try this" and "take my money" is still social proof. Four review cycles. Same finding. Same fix. It's not a product problem, it's not a copy problem, it's not a pricing problem. It's a trust problem, and it has one solution: show that real people use this product and that it saved them real money.

The distance from current state to a site that converts at 2-3x the current rate is **testimonials, a demo video, and a team page.** That's days of work, not weeks. Everything else is optimization on a foundation that's already solid.

Ship the social proof. The product has earned it.
