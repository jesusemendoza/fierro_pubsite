# Fierro Website Review: Homeowner Perspective

**Reviewer profile:** Homeowner who self-manages renovation projects. Currently juggling a kitchen remodel and planning a bathroom redo. Has a shoebox of receipts, a half-updated Google Sheet, and a contractor who texts invoices as iPhone photos. Has been burned by overruns before. Has never heard of Fierro until right now.

**Pages reviewed:** Homepage, Pricing, Why Fierro
**Date:** March 10, 2026

---

## First Contact: What the Hell Is This?

I landed on the homepage and within five seconds I knew what Fierro does: it tracks renovation spending. The headline -- "Know exactly where your money goes" -- is clear, direct, and doesn't make me decode any marketing jargon. Good. The subhead nails it further: "track every dollar, catch every overrun, and stay on budget."

That's exactly what I want. I'm not looking for "AI-powered construction management synergies." I want to know if I'm getting screwed on my kitchen remodel. This headline says Fierro will tell me. Passed the five-second test.

## The Pain Points: Do They Get Me?

The "Why Fierro" page is where the site really earns its keep. Let me walk through the scenarios they describe:

**"Your contractor sent you a bill and you have no idea if it's right"** -- Yes. This happened to me literally last month. My electrician sent a bill for $4,200 and I had no way to verify if that matched what we agreed to. I paid it because what else was I going to do, argue? This section hit a nerve.

**"You set a budget but you have no idea if you're actually on track"** -- They use a $35K kitchen remodel example. That's almost exactly my budget. And the question "Are you at $18K or $28K?" is the exact question I ask myself every week. Right now my answer is "I think maybe $22K? Let me check... actually I'm not sure." This is painfully accurate.

**"Your partner asked for a project update and you spent an hour pulling numbers"** -- My wife asks me this every Sunday. I spend 45 minutes opening bank statements, scrolling through texts, and updating our Google Sheet before I can give her an answer. By then she's annoyed and I'm defensive. This is a real scenario.

**"You didn't realize the flip was over budget until closing day"** -- I don't flip houses, so this one doesn't hit me personally, but I can see how it would destroy someone doing investment properties.

The pain points are real and specific. This isn't generic "manage your projects better" copy. Someone who wrote this has either renovated a house or talked extensively to people who have. That earns credibility.

## The Features: What Would I Actually Use?

Let me go through these as a homeowner doing 1-2 projects:

**Budget tracking with categories** -- Yes. I want to set a number for electrical, plumbing, cabinets, countertops, flooring, and see how each one is tracking. This is the core of what I need.

**Receipt capture on my phone** -- This is huge. I'm standing in Home Depot spending $340 on random plumbing fittings and I need to snap that receipt before it ends up crumpled in my truck. If this works well, it's a game-changer for me.

**Contractor logging with approval** -- This is the feature that made me lean forward. If my contractor can log his expenses and I can approve them, that eliminates the "he said $4,200 but I thought we agreed to $3,800" problem. This is actual accountability, not just tracking.

**Budget alerts** -- "Your electrical budget is 80% spent with half the work remaining." If it actually does this, that alone might be worth $20/month. Finding out you're over budget when there's still time to adjust is completely different from finding out when the project is done.

**CSV/PDF export** -- My accountant will love me. Right now I hand her a folder of receipts and a prayer.

**AI/ChatGPT integration** -- Interesting but feels like a nice-to-have. "Am I over budget on electrical?" is a question I could answer by just looking at the dashboard. The AI stuff feels more like a selling point for tech-forward users. I'd use it occasionally but it's not why I'd sign up.

**Vendor management** -- Only in the Plus tier at $49/month. I have maybe 5 vendors on my kitchen remodel. I don't need a management system for them. This feels like an investor feature that got put behind a paywall, which is fine -- I don't need it.

## Pricing: Is This Reasonable?

Here's where I need to do some real math.

**Free tier:** 1 project, 6 sub-projects (budget categories), 2 team members, 100MB receipt storage. This is actually usable for a simple project. Kitchen remodel with 6 categories? That might work. But only 2 team members means I can add my wife OR my contractor, not both. That's a deliberate limitation to push me to pay, and I respect the hustle, but it's annoying.

**Home tier at $20/month ($192/year):** 2 projects, 15 sub-projects, 5 team members, 5 contractors (free!), 5GB storage, budget alerts, export. This is the tier built for me. $20/month for a kitchen remodel that's costing me $35,000 is nothing. That's 0.06% of my budget. If it catches even one $500 overrun, it's paid for itself for two years.

**Plus tier at $49/month:** This is for people managing multiple properties. Not me. Fair enough.

**Builder tier (custom):** Enterprise. Not me.

**The verdict on pricing:** $20/month is fair. It's less than one trip to Home Depot for stuff I don't need. The free tier is genuinely useful for testing, and the upgrade to Home is a no-brainer if you're doing a real renovation. I don't feel like they're trying to gouge me.

**One concern:** What happens after my project is done? Do I keep paying $20/month to access my records? Can I downgrade to Free and still see my data? This isn't clear anywhere on the site, and it matters. If I'm paying $20/month for 18 months during a remodel and then I need to keep paying forever just to see my expense history, that changes the math. **Fix this: Add a clear FAQ about what happens to your data after a project ends or you downgrade.**

## The "Why Not Just Use a Spreadsheet?" Question

The pricing page actually has this as an FAQ, and the answer is decent: "spreadsheets don't alert you when your electrical budget is 80% spent with half the work remaining." True. My spreadsheet is a dumb grid. It doesn't warn me about anything. It doesn't let my contractor log expenses. It doesn't snap receipts.

But the answer could be stronger. Here's what would really sell me:

- **Spreadsheets require you to remember to update them.** I update mine every 2-3 weeks. By then I've forgotten half the cash purchases. Fierro lets me log at the point of purchase.
- **Spreadsheets don't have an approval workflow.** My contractor texts me a photo of a receipt. I say "ok." That's not a system. That's a prayer.
- **Spreadsheets die when you share them.** My wife edits the format. My contractor doesn't know how to use Google Sheets. Fierro gives everyone a role-appropriate view.
- **Spreadsheets don't attach receipts to line items.** I have a Google Sheet AND a folder of receipt photos AND a pile of physical receipts. Good luck matching them up in March when I'm doing taxes.

**Fix this: The FAQ answer should be a gut punch, not a gentle nudge. Make the spreadsheet comparison visceral. Show the pain.**

## Trust & Credibility

Here's where things get thin.

**What I see:**
- "Your data is encrypted in transit and at rest" -- Good, table stakes.
- "We use Supabase for infrastructure" -- I have no idea what Supabase is. This means nothing to me as a homeowner. It might impress developers.
- "No credit card required" -- Good, lowers the barrier.
- Copyright 2026, so the company exists and is current.

**What I don't see:**
- No testimonials from actual users. Zero. Not one quote from a homeowner saying "I saved $3,000 on my kitchen remodel because Fierro caught an overrun." This is the single biggest gap on the entire site.
- No company information. Who are you? Where are you based? Is this a one-person side project or a funded company? I'm about to put my financial data into your system -- I want to know you'll exist in 6 months.
- No case studies. Show me a real project. "$45,000 bathroom remodel, tracked from day one. Here's what the dashboard looked like. Here's the overrun we caught at week 6."
- No security certifications or compliance badges.
- No information about data portability. If Fierro shuts down, what happens to my data?
- No phone number, no physical address, no team page.

**This is a problem.** I'm a homeowner about to trust you with detailed financial data about a major purchase. I need more proof that you're real, reliable, and not going to disappear with my data.

**Fix this:** Add at minimum: (1) 3-5 real user testimonials with names and project types, (2) an "About" page with team info, (3) a case study showing a real project tracked in Fierro, (4) a data portability guarantee. These are not optional -- they're prerequisites for trust.

## Mobile Experience

The site mentions "snap a receipt on your phone at the hardware store" multiple times, which is great because that's exactly where I'd use it most. But I can't verify the actual mobile app experience from the website alone.

**What I want to know and don't see:**
- Is there a native mobile app or is it a web app I open in my browser?
- Can I snap a receipt and it auto-extracts the amount, vendor, and date? Or do I have to type all that in manually?
- Does it work offline? Cell service at my hardware store is spotty.
- How fast is it? If it takes more than 30 seconds to log an expense, I'll stop doing it.

**Fix this: Add a section specifically about the mobile experience. Show a screenshot of the receipt capture flow. If OCR auto-fills the expense details, say so -- that's a major selling point. If there's a native app, link to it.**

## Competitors and Alternatives

As a homeowner, here's what I'd compare Fierro to:

**Google Sheets / Excel (free):** My current system. Works fine until it doesn't. No alerts, no receipt capture, no contractor collaboration. But it's free and I already know how to use it.

**QuickBooks ($30+/month):** Way too much for a homeowner. Built for businesses. I'd feel like I'm using a chainsaw to cut a sandwich.

**Mint / personal finance apps:** Track spending by bank category but don't understand "renovation project" as a concept. My kitchen remodel expenses are mixed in with my grocery runs and gas fill-ups.

**Buildertrend, CoConstruct, etc.:** Built for professional contractors, not homeowners. Way too complex, way too expensive.

**A physical notebook:** Laugh all you want, but a lot of homeowners track expenses this way. It works until you lose the notebook or need to total things up.

**Fierro's positioning is actually smart.** It sits in a gap that none of these alternatives fill: serious enough to be useful, simple enough for a non-professional, and priced for someone doing 1-2 personal projects. The site could do a better job of explicitly calling out these alternatives and explaining why Fierro is better than each one.

**Fix this: Add a comparison section. "Fierro vs. Spreadsheets." "Fierro vs. QuickBooks." "Fierro vs. Doing Nothing." Make it a page or a section on the Why Fierro page. Be direct.**

## Copy Quality

The copy is genuinely good. It's clear, specific, and avoids most SaaS cliches. Specific observations:

**Strengths:**
- "Know exactly where your money goes" is a strong headline. Simple, direct, speaks to a real anxiety.
- The $35K kitchen remodel example on the Why Fierro page is brilliant. It's a real number for a real project. It makes me think "that's my project."
- "Your contractor logs, you approve" is a feature described in exactly the right way. It's a workflow, not a feature list.
- "No more chasing invoices over text" -- this is specific enough to feel true. Generic copy would say "streamline your communication."
- The three-step getting started section is clean and believable. "Takes about 5 minutes" is a credible claim.

**Weaknesses:**
- "Stop wondering where your money went" is used as a CTA heading on every single page. By the third time I see it, it's wallpaper. Vary it.
- The AI section feels bolted on. The rest of the copy is about real problems; the AI section is about "connecting to your tools." It reads like a different product.
- "Built for every kind of project" as a section heading is generic. Something like "Whether it's a bathroom or a 10-unit flip" would be stronger.
- The "Why homeowners and investors choose Fierro" section would be stronger as "Why homeowners and investors SWITCHED TO Fierro" -- but only if you have the testimonials to back it up.

## What's Missing That Would Make Me Say "Shut Up and Take My Money"

1. **A live demo or interactive preview.** Let me see a sample project dashboard without signing up. Show me a $40K kitchen remodel with 8 budget categories, some overruns, some receipts attached. Let me click around. If the product is good, let the product sell itself.

2. **Real user stories.** "We saved $4,300 on our kitchen remodel" is worth more than any feature description. I need social proof from people like me.

3. **A "what happens when things go wrong" story.** Show me a scenario where a homeowner caught a $2,000 overrun at week 4 instead of week 12. Make it narrative. Make me feel the relief of catching it early.

4. **Receipt OCR demo.** If the receipt scanning auto-fills vendor, amount, and date, SHOW ME. That's the moment I'd go from "interesting" to "I need this." A 10-second video of someone snapping a receipt and watching the fields populate would be more persuasive than 500 words of copy.

5. **Post-project value.** After the remodel is done, what do I have? A complete record for insurance? For taxes? For resale value documentation? Tell me the data lives on and serves me after the project ends.

6. **A "total cost of your renovation" calculator.** Enter your project type and budget, and Fierro shows you the average overrun for that type of project and how much Fierro would save you. Gamify the decision.

## Scores

### 1. First Impression / Hook: 8/10
The headline works. The subhead works. I knew what this was in under 5 seconds. I didn't bounce. The only reason it's not a 9 is that it lacks a visual -- a screenshot of the actual product dashboard -- to anchor the promise. "Know exactly where your money goes" + a screenshot of a clean budget dashboard = immediate credibility. Words alone leave me wondering if the product actually looks that clean.

### 2. Value Proposition Clarity: 8/10
Very clear. Track renovation spending, catch overruns, collaborate with your contractor. I don't have to guess what this does. The slight ding is that the site tries to serve homeowners AND flippers AND "real estate teams" -- and while that's fine, it slightly dilutes the message. If I'm a homeowner, the flipper content is noise. But it's well-segmented enough that I can find my lane.

### 3. Pain Point Resonance: 9/10
This is the site's strongest quality. The scenarios on the Why Fierro page are almost uncomfortably accurate. The $35K kitchen example, the contractor invoice you can't verify, the weekly partner update scramble -- these are real problems described by someone who has lived them. The copy doesn't say "managing renovations is hard." It says "your contractor sent you a bill and you have no idea if it's right." That specificity earns a 9.

### 4. Pricing Fairness: 8/10
$20/month for the Home tier is genuinely reasonable for a renovation that costs $20K-$100K. The free tier is usable enough to test with. The only missing piece is clarity about what happens after the project -- do I keep paying to access my records? That unanswered question costs a point. Also, the jump from 2 team members (Free) to 5 (Home) feels calculated to force an upgrade when you want your contractor AND your spouse on the project. Smart business, slightly annoying as a customer.

### 5. Trust & Credibility: 4/10
This is the biggest weakness on the site. No testimonials, no team page, no case studies, no company information beyond a copyright line. I'm being asked to trust a faceless entity with detailed financial data about a major life purchase. The copy is good enough that it feels like a real product, but the trust signals are almost nonexistent. For a product in the financial data space, this is a serious gap. This score would jump to 7 or 8 with testimonials, a team page, and a single case study.

### 6. Feature Completeness (for my needs): 7/10
The core features -- budget tracking, expense logging, receipt capture, contractor collaboration, alerts, export -- cover my needs well. What's missing: (1) I don't know if receipt capture includes OCR or if I'm manually typing everything, (2) no mention of integration with bank accounts or credit cards for automatic expense import, (3) no mention of tracking change orders specifically -- my contractor changes scope regularly and I need to track the impact on budget, (4) no insurance documentation features. The AI stuff is nice but not what I'd pay for.

### 7. "Why Not Just Use a Spreadsheet?" Factor: 7/10
The site addresses this directly, which is good. The answer is decent but not devastating. The contractor approval workflow is the strongest argument -- spreadsheets can't do that. Budget alerts are second. Receipt capture is third. But the site doesn't hammer these advantages hard enough. A side-by-side comparison showing "Week 6: your spreadsheet says everything is fine. Fierro shows you're $3,200 over on electrical." would be much more effective than the current FAQ answer.

### 8. Overall "Would I Pay For This?": 7/10
I'm interested. I'm not yet sold. The product concept is exactly what I need. The pricing is fair. The copy is good. But I need to see the product (demo/screenshots), I need to see proof it works (testimonials/case studies), and I need to trust the company behind it (team page/about section). Right now I'd sign up for the free tier to test it, and if the product delivers on what the copy promises, I'd upgrade to Home at $20/month without hesitation. The website gets me to "I'll try it." It doesn't yet get me to "take my money." That last mile is about trust and proof, not features or copy.

---

## Overall Score: 7.3 / 10

## Summary: What Fierro Gets Right and What Needs to Happen

**Gets right:**
- Clear, specific, jargon-free copy that speaks to real homeowner pain points
- Smart positioning in a gap no competitor fills well
- Fair, transparent pricing with a usable free tier
- Strong core feature set (budget tracking, receipt capture, contractor collaboration)
- The Why Fierro page is genuinely persuasive

**Needs to happen (priority order):**
1. Add 3-5 real user testimonials with specific dollar amounts saved
2. Add a team/about page -- put faces and names behind the product
3. Add product screenshots or an interactive demo to the homepage
4. Clarify what happens to data when you downgrade or cancel
5. Add a competitor comparison section (vs. spreadsheets, vs. QuickBooks, vs. doing nothing)
6. Show the mobile receipt capture experience with a screenshot or short video
7. Add a case study: one real project, tracked from start to finish
8. Vary the CTA copy -- "Stop wondering where your money went" appears on every page
9. Clarify OCR capabilities for receipt scanning
10. Address change order tracking as a feature

**Bottom line:** Fierro is solving a real problem that I personally have. The site communicates the value clearly. But it's asking me to trust a company I know nothing about with financial data I care deeply about. Close the trust gap and this goes from "I'll try the free tier" to "why the hell wasn't I using this already."
