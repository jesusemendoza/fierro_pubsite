# Fierro Site Review: Real Estate Investor Perspective

**Reviewer Profile:** Real estate investor, 5-20+ active renovation/flip projects at any given time. Currently using a mix of QuickBooks, Excel, and CoConstruct. Needs to track cost basis across properties for tax purposes and investment decisions. Has never heard of Fierro before today.

**Date:** 2026-03-10
**Pages Reviewed:** Homepage, Pricing, Why Fierro

---

## The 30-Second Gut Check

I landed on the homepage and within five seconds I knew what this product does. "Know exactly where your money goes." That is the single most important sentence in renovation investing, and they nailed it. The subheadline confirms it: track every dollar, catch every overrun, stay on budget. I'm not scrolling away. I'm reading.

But here is where it gets complicated. The more I read, the more I see a product that *almost* speaks to me but keeps hedging its bets between someone remodeling their kitchen and someone managing a portfolio of flips. Those are fundamentally different businesses with fundamentally different needs, and right now, Fierro is trying to be both at once.

---

## Detailed Evaluation

### Does This Solve My #1 Problem?

My number one problem is knowing exact project costs across multiple properties. Not approximately. Not "we're about 80% through." Exact, to-the-dollar costs broken down by property, by category, by vendor, in real time.

**What Fierro shows me:** Budget tracking with categories and targets, real-time spent-vs-remaining bars, and a portfolio view showing multiple properties side by side. The flipper portfolio screenshot with 4 active properties showing address, budget, spend, and health status — that is exactly what I need to see every morning with my coffee.

**What's missing from the pitch:** I don't see anything about tracking cost basis for tax purposes specifically. For an investor, the cost basis of a property (purchase price + rehab costs + holding costs) is the number that determines your taxable gain. Fierro tracks rehab costs, but the site never connects those dots. It never says "here's your total cost basis per property." That is a missed layup.

**Fix:** Add a section or at least a bullet point that explicitly says something like: "Know your all-in cost basis per property — purchase, rehab, holding costs, and closing costs — the number your CPA actually needs." Even if the product doesn't track purchase price today, the framing matters.

### Multi-Project Capability

The Plus tier gives me 8 projects with 20 sub-projects each. For $49/month. Let me do the math: 8 projects with 20 sub-categories is 160 line-item buckets. That is... actually decent for most active flippers. I typically have 6-10 active projects at any time.

**The problem:** What happens when I have project #9? I hit a wall and have to jump to "Builder" (custom pricing, contact sales). That cliff from $49 to "call us" is uncomfortable. I don't want to talk to a sales rep. I want to give you $79/month or $99/month for 15-20 projects and keep going. The missing middle tier between $49 and "enterprise" is going to lose investors who are scaling.

**Fix:** Add a tier between Plus and Builder. Call it "Portfolio" or "Investor Pro" — something like $89-99/month for 15-20 projects with everything in Plus plus API access. The jump from 8 projects to "contact sales" is a conversion killer for people who are scaling from 8 to 15 properties. That is exactly the growth phase where you want to capture customers, not push them to a sales call.

### Financial Reporting & Export

CSV and PDF export is table stakes and they have it. But only on Home tier and above (not Free). That is fine. The real question: what does the export actually look like? The site says "give your accountant clean data at tax time" but never shows a sample export or explains what fields are included.

**What I need from an export:** Property address, category, vendor name, date, amount, receipt reference, approval status — ideally in a format my CPA can drop into their tax software without reformatting. Schedule E reporting requires costs broken down by property and by type (repairs vs. improvements vs. capital expenditures). Does Fierro know the difference? The site does not say.

**Fix:** Show a sample CSV export. Just a screenshot of 10 rows in a spreadsheet. Let me see the columns. Better yet, create a "Tax Time" feature page or section that walks through exactly how an investor's accountant would use Fierro data. "Here's what your CPA gets" with an actual example. That alone would close deals.

### Vendor Management

This is a Plus-tier feature, which makes sense. The site promises: build a directory of contractors/suppliers, link invoices to vendors and categories, compare costs across projects. That last bullet — "compare costs across projects to spot who's overcharging" — is gold. That is the exact conversation I have with myself every quarter when I notice my plumber's bills keep creeping up.

**What's missing:** The site doesn't show what vendor analytics actually look like. How deep does the comparison go? Can I see that Vendor A charges me $4,500 for rough plumbing on average vs. Vendor B at $3,800? Can I see total spend per vendor across all projects for the year? Can I see if a vendor's pricing has increased over time? The feature description is promising but vague.

**Fix:** Show a vendor spend summary screenshot. A table showing each vendor, total spend, number of projects, average cost per project, and maybe a trend indicator. Investors obsess over this data. If the product already does this, showing it on the site would be extremely compelling. If it doesn't do this yet, build it — it is a massive differentiator vs. spreadsheets and QuickBooks.

### Budget vs. Actual Tracking

This is the core of the product and it looks solid. The visual progress bars showing spent vs. remaining per category, the budget health indicators, the alerts before you go over — this is exactly right. The Why Fierro page has a great pain point: "You estimated $35K for the kitchen remodel. You've been paying invoices for two months. Are you at $18K or $28K?" Every investor has lived that exact moment.

**What's missing:** I don't see anything about committed costs vs. actual costs. In renovation, there's a difference between what you've paid and what you've committed to pay (signed contracts, approved change orders). If I have a $15K contract with my GC and I've paid $5K, my committed cost is $15K, not $5K. The site mentions "committed costs" once in passing on the Why Fierro page but doesn't explain it or show it in the product screenshots.

**Fix:** Make committed vs. spent a first-class concept in the marketing. Show a budget bar with three segments: spent (dark), committed (medium), remaining (light). That visual alone would tell sophisticated investors that Fierro understands how real projects work — not just receipt tracking, but financial forecasting.

### Pricing Analysis

| Tier | Price | Projects | My Verdict |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Free | $0 | 1 | Fine for kicking the tires |
| Home | $20/mo ($16 annual) | 2 | Too limited for investors, good for homeowners |
| Plus | $49/mo ($39 annual) | 8 | The investor entry point. Decent value. |
| Builder | Custom | Unlimited | Hate "contact sales" for what should be $99-149/mo |

At $49/month ($470/year annually), Plus is cheaper than QuickBooks Online Plus ($99/month) and infinitely more focused on renovation projects. It is cheaper than CoConstruct (which starts around $99/month for builders). It is more expensive than a spreadsheet ($0) but the time savings would pay for itself in the first month if the product actually works.

**The value question:** If Fierro saves me from one $2,000 budget overrun per year (which it should, easily), it pays for itself 4x over. The ROI case is strong but the site never makes it explicitly. Nobody on the pricing page says "Fierro costs $49/month. The average renovation overrun it catches is worth $2,000-5,000. You do the math."

**Fix:** Put an ROI calculator or at least an ROI statement on the pricing page. Something concrete: "The average Fierro user catches their first budget overrun within 30 days." Even if you don't have the data yet, frame the value in terms of money saved, not features gained.

### How It Compares to My Current Stack

**vs. QuickBooks:** QuickBooks is an accounting tool I've forced into project management. It can track expenses by "class" (which I use for properties) and by vendor, but there is no concept of budgets, no real-time health indicators, no contractor collaboration, and exporting project-level data requires reports that take 20 minutes to configure. Fierro is purpose-built for the workflow QuickBooks fumbles at. But QuickBooks handles the full accounting lifecycle — accounts payable, receivable, bank reconciliation, 1099s. Fierro does not, and the site never addresses how it fits alongside QuickBooks.

**vs. CoConstruct:** CoConstruct is built for custom home builders, not investors. It handles specs, selections, scheduling, and client communication. It is massive overkill for a flipper tracking rehab costs. Fierro is lighter, cheaper, and more focused. But CoConstruct has deep estimating tools that Fierro apparently does not.

**vs. Buildium:** Buildium is property management (rent collection, tenant screening, maintenance). Different problem entirely. Fierro is for the renovation phase, not the hold phase. The site should probably mention this distinction for investors who might confuse the two.

**vs. Excel:** Excel is free and infinitely flexible, but it is a solo sport. My contractors cannot log expenses into my spreadsheet. There are no alerts. There is no mobile receipt capture. And the spreadsheet is always out of date because updating it is a chore I put off. Fierro's FAQ addresses the spreadsheet comparison well: "spreadsheets don't alert you when your electrical budget is 80% spent with half the work remaining." Good answer.

**What the site should do:** Have a dedicated comparison page or section. "Fierro vs. QuickBooks for Real Estate Investors." "Fierro vs. Spreadsheets." Real, specific, honest comparisons. Not generic "we're better" but "here's exactly what QuickBooks can't do for renovation tracking." Investors research before they buy. Give them the comparison they're going to make anyway.

### API & Integrations

API access is Builder-tier only (custom pricing). That is a problem. Investors who are scaling are exactly the people who want API access — to pull data into their own dashboards, connect to their accounting software, or feed data into portfolio analytics tools. Locking it behind an enterprise paywall means the growing investor (your ideal customer) can't programmatically access their own data.

The ChatGPT integration is interesting and genuinely differentiating. Being able to ask "Am I over budget on electrical?" and get a real answer from real data is compelling. But I don't use ChatGPT for business analytics — I use spreadsheets, dashboards, and my accountant. The AI angle is a nice-to-have, not a must-have, and it gets a lot of real estate on the site relative to its importance for investor buyers.

**Fix:** Move API access to Plus tier, or at least offer read-only API on Plus. And add integrations that actually matter to investors: QuickBooks sync, Xero, or even Zapier. If my data can't flow from Fierro to my accounting software without manual CSV export, that is a friction point I'll feel every month.

### Trust & Credibility

**What's there:** Clean design, professional screenshots, data security mention (Supabase, encryption at rest and in transit), "no credit card required" friction removal. The product looks real and polished.

**What's missing:** Zero testimonials. Zero case studies. Zero logos. Zero "10,000 projects tracked" social proof. Zero press mentions. The site has no evidence that anyone has ever used this product. For an investor about to trust it with financial data across 8 properties, that is a significant gap. I need to know that other investors are using this successfully.

**Fix:** This is the single highest-priority fix. Get 3-5 testimonials from real users. Even beta users. Even friends. A flipper who says "I caught a $4,000 electrical overrun on my third flip" is worth more than any feature description. If the product is too new for testimonials, be transparent about it: "We're new. We're building this for investors like you. Here's our roadmap." Honesty builds more trust than silence.

### Marketing Target Audience

The site tries to serve three audiences: homeowners doing a single renovation, flippers/investors managing multiple properties, and real estate teams. The homeowner messaging dominates. The hero example is a $35K kitchen remodel. The first audience card is "Homeowners & DIYers." The first FAQ is "I'm just doing one renovation."

There is nothing wrong with serving homeowners, but investors and homeowners have completely different buying psychology. A homeowner remodeling their kitchen is spending their own money, doing it once, and is emotionally invested. An investor is spending OPM (other people's money), doing it repeatedly, and is financially motivated. The messaging that closes a homeowner ("never wonder where your money went") is too soft for an investor. An investor wants: "know your exact cost basis, maximize your ROI, give your CPA clean data, scale without losing control."

**Fix:** Consider a dedicated landing page for investors (getfierro.com/investors). Different hero, different examples, different language. The product is the same but the story is different. The investor page should lead with portfolio management, cost basis tracking, vendor analytics, and tax-ready exports. The homeowner page can keep the kitchen remodel. Don't make me mentally translate "kitchen remodel" into "full gut rehab at 1247 Oak Street."

## Scores

### 1. First Impression / Hook: 8/10

"Know exactly where your money goes" is a strong headline. The dashboard screenshot makes it real. I understood the product in under 5 seconds. Loses points because the hero example (kitchen remodel) signals "homeowner tool" before it signals "investor tool." The subheadline tries to bridge both audiences but the visual anchors me in homeowner land.

### 2. Value Proposition Clarity: 7/10

The core value prop is clear: track renovation costs in real time. But the site undersells the investor-specific value. Budget tracking is necessary but not sufficient — I also need cost basis tracking, vendor analytics, and tax-ready reporting. The site hints at these but never makes them front and center for the investor audience.

### 3. Multi-Project Capability: 7/10

8 projects on Plus is workable for most active flippers. The portfolio dashboard screenshot is compelling. But the cliff from 8 projects ($49) to unlimited (custom pricing) is a gap. And I don't see any cross-project analytics or portfolio-level rollup views described on the site. Can I see total spend across all 8 properties on one screen? Total by vendor across all projects? The site doesn't explicitly show this.

### 4. Financial Reporting & Export: 5/10

CSV and PDF export exist. The site says "give your accountant clean data at tax time." But there is zero detail about what the exports contain, no sample shown, no tax-specific framing, and no integration with accounting software. For a product targeting investors who are legally required to track cost basis, this is underdeveloped in the marketing — whether or not the product itself handles it well.

### 5. Pricing Value (for investor scale): 7/10

$49/month for 8 projects with vendor management and analytics is genuinely good value compared to alternatives. The annual discount to $39/month makes it even better. But the missing middle tier between $49 and enterprise, and API access locked behind enterprise, knocks it down. At my scale (10-15 projects), I'm stuck in an awkward position.

### 6. Trust & Credibility: 4/10

This is the biggest weakness. No testimonials, no case studies, no user count, no press, no logos, no third-party validation of any kind. The product looks polished and professional, which helps. But an investor about to centralize their financial data needs more than good design. They need evidence that this works and that the company will be around next year.

### 7. Competitive Advantage vs. Alternatives: 6/10

Fierro occupies a genuine gap in the market: purpose-built renovation cost tracking that is lighter than CoConstruct, more focused than QuickBooks, and more collaborative than spreadsheets. That positioning is strong. But the site never makes explicit comparisons. It never says "here's why Fierro instead of QuickBooks." It assumes I'll figure out the advantage myself. I will, because I'm motivated, but most visitors won't.

### 8. Overall "Would I Pay For This?": 7/10

Yes, I would try it. The free tier makes that easy. Would I commit to Plus at $49/month and migrate my workflow from QuickBooks + Excel? That depends on what the product actually does once I'm inside — and the site gives me enough to be intrigued but not enough to be certain. The lack of social proof is the biggest barrier. I'd need to see it work with real data on a real project before I'd move my other properties over.

## Overall Score: 6.4 / 10

---

## The Bottom Line

Fierro is solving a real problem that nobody else is solving well. Every real estate investor I know is cobbling together QuickBooks, Excel, and text messages to track renovation costs. The fact that a purpose-built tool exists at $49/month should be a no-brainer. And yet the site doesn't make it feel like a no-brainer for investors specifically.

The product positioning is split between homeowners and investors, and neither audience gets the full-throat pitch they deserve. The homeowner gets the hero section; the investor gets a sidebar mention. The features are there (budget tracking, vendor management, team collaboration, export) but the investor-specific framing is missing (cost basis, portfolio rollups, tax reporting, ROI analysis).

**Three things that would move this from "I'd try it" to "why the fuck am I NOT using this":**

1. **Social proof.** One real testimonial from one real flipper who caught one real overrun. That is worth more than every feature bullet on the site combined.

2. **Investor-specific landing page.** Stop making me read past kitchen remodel examples to find the investor value. Give me a page that leads with "Manage your entire flip portfolio in one place" and shows me 8 properties with rollup analytics.

3. **Tax-time story.** Show me exactly what my CPA gets. A sample export, a cost basis summary, a per-property breakdown that maps to Schedule E. Make "tax time" the easiest day of my year instead of the worst.

Fix those three things and you have a product that every investor in every REIA meeting in the country would be talking about.
