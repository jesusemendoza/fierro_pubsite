# General Contractor Perspective Analysis

> "I run a crew, manage subs, and juggle 3-10 jobs. I need to track costs, manage change orders, and know which jobs are making money and which are bleeding."

## Verdict: This is clearly the target user, but still would NOT sign up today

## Trust Barriers

### The #1 Problem: No Proof Anyone Uses This
- Zero testimonials from contractors
- Zero case studies
- Zero industry endorsements or associations (no NAHB, AGC, or local HBA logos)
- GCs are conservative adopters — they use what other GCs recommend
- Without social proof, this tool is invisible to how contractors actually choose software

### No Product Visibility
- A GC needs to see: Can I create a budget with 50+ line items? How does the mobile receipt capture work? What do the reports look like?
- None of this is shown — not a single screenshot
- "See How It Works" scrolls to a 3-step text explanation, not a demo

### No Company Credibility Signals
- No "About" page, no team, no industry experience mentioned
- Was this built by people who've worked in construction? Or engineers who think they understand it?
- GCs trust tools built by people who've been on job sites

## Messaging Problems

### Too Generic
- "Construction cost control that keeps your projects profitable" could be any construction SaaS tagline
- Doesn't differentiate from Buildertrend, CoConstruct, Procore, or even a well-maintained spreadsheet
- No clear positioning: Are you replacing my PM tool? My accounting? Or sitting alongside them?

### The AI Pitch Misses the Mark
- GCs don't care about "MCP servers" or "ChatGPT reading your data"
- They care about: "Get a weekly cost report without touching a spreadsheet"
- The technical framing of AI is a turnoff for the average GC — it should be invisible, not a headline feature

### "15-Minute Setup" Creates Doubt
- A GC managing a $500K commercial TI has complex budgets with divisions, CSI codes, and multiple cost types
- "15-minute setup" signals this is a toy, not a professional tool
- Better framing: "Start simple, scale to full job costing"

### The Change Order Gap Is a Dealbreaker
- Change orders are where GCs lose the most money
- The #1 pain point in construction cost management
- Not mentioning it is like selling a car without mentioning it has an engine
- Even if the app supports it, not marketing it is a massive miss

## Pricing Concerns

### Free Tier Is Too Restrictive for Real Testing
- 1 project, 6 sub-projects — a real job might have 20+ cost categories
- Can't evaluate the tool on a real project with these limits
- Should offer: 1 project with unlimited sub-projects on Free

### Plus Tier Questions
- $49/mo for 5 projects — competitive if features are there
- But "Advanced analytics" is vague — what analytics specifically?
- "Priority support" — what's the response time? SLA?

### Builder Tier Opacity
- GCs running 10+ jobs need this tier
- "Contact Sales" with no pricing guidance = friction = bounce
- Should at minimum show "Starting at $X/mo"

## Competitive Landscape (What GCs Compare Against)

| Competitor | Strength vs. Fierro |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Buildertrend | Full PM suite, established, massive user base |
| CoConstruct | Custom home builder focused, client portal |
| Procore | Enterprise standard, deep integrations |
| Knowify | QuickBooks integrated, job costing |
| Spreadsheets | Free, familiar, flexible |

Fierro doesn't position against any of these. A GC reading the site has no idea why they should switch.

## What Would Fix This

1. **Add screenshots immediately** — show the budget view, expense entry, mobile capture, and a dashboard
2. **Add testimonials** — even 3 beta users would transform credibility
3. **Mention change orders** — even if basic, it needs to be on the features list
4. **Position against alternatives** — "Unlike Buildertrend, Fierro focuses purely on cost control without the bloat"
5. **Show the mobile experience** — if there's a mobile app, show it. If it's responsive web, show that
6. **Add an "About" or "Our Story" page** — who built this and why
7. **List integrations** — even if it's just CSV import and QuickBooks planned, say so
8. **Reframe AI as a benefit, not a technology** — "Get AI-generated cost reports" not "MCP server with ChatGPT compatibility"
9. **Add a demo video** — 2-minute walkthrough of creating a budget and tracking an expense
10. **Show industry knowledge** — a blog post about "Top 5 Reasons GCs Go Over Budget" would do more for trust than any feature description
