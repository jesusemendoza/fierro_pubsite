# Builder (Production & Custom Home) Perspective Analysis

> "I build 10-50+ homes a year. I need job costing per unit, draw management for lenders, vendor contracts, and a system my PMs and field supers can all use without training."

## Verdict: Not even close to ready for this user

## Trust Barriers

### Enterprise Buyers Need Enterprise Signals
- No SOC 2 compliance mention
- No uptime SLA
- No data backup/recovery policy visible
- No onboarding or implementation support described
- No customer success story at this scale
- "Dedicated account manager" on Builder tier — but who? What team?

### The ToS Is a Liability
- "Suspend or terminate your access with or without cause, with or without notice"
- A builder with 30 active projects in the system cannot accept this risk
- This clause alone would stop any serious buyer during legal review
- The AI liability disclaimer ("not responsible for data processing by third-party AI models") would also be flagged by legal

### No Industry Credibility
- No association memberships (NAHB, HBA)
- No conference presence
- No industry publications or thought leadership
- Builders at this scale buy from vendors they meet at IBS or JLC Live

## Messaging Problems

### Wrong Level of Sophistication
- "Every dollar. Every pour. Accounted for." — catchy but reads as small-scale
- For a production builder, the pitch should be: "Job costing across your entire pipeline"
- "5-minute setup" is the opposite of what enterprise buyers want to hear — they want "white-glove onboarding"

### No Understanding of Builder Workflows
- Production builders have: model-based budgets, lot premiums, option pricing, purchase orders, variance analysis by community
- Custom builders have: allowances, selections, client change orders, draw schedules, spec vs. contract pricing
- The site mentions none of this — it reads like a generic expense tracker with a construction paint job

### This Is a Completely Different Product
- The gap between what Fierro offers (expense tracking, budgets, vendor directory) and what builders need is enormous
- This isn't a "missing features" problem — it's a "different market segment" problem
- Fierro as described is a cost-tracking layer; builders need a full construction ERP

## Pricing Concerns

### "Custom" Pricing Is Expected but Not Helpful
- Enterprise buyers expect custom pricing, but they also expect:
  - A pricing page that says "Starting at $X/seat/mo"
  - An ROI calculator
  - A comparison vs. current tools (Sage, Buildertrend, etc.)
  - Volume discounts for multi-community deployments
- None of this exists

### No Implementation Cost Transparency
- How long does onboarding take?
- Is there data migration support?
- Is training included?
- What's the contract term? Month-to-month or annual commitment?

## What Would Fix This (If Targeting Builders)

### Short Term: Don't Target Builders Yet
- Remove "Builder" tier naming or reframe it as "Team" or "Business"
- Don't promise what you can't deliver — builders will evaluate and reject, damaging brand
- Focus on the GC/flipper market where the current feature set has value

### Long Term: If You Want Builders
1. Build AIA billing, draw schedules, and lien waiver tracking
2. Add purchase order management
3. Build a client/owner portal
4. Get SOC 2 certified
5. Integrate with Sage and QuickBooks
6. Hire a customer success team with construction experience
7. Attend IBS, JLC Live, and regional HBA events
8. Get 5 reference customers in the builder space
9. Build cost code templates (CSI, custom)
10. Add multi-entity and multi-community support
